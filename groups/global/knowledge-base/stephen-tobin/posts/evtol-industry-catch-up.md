# eVTOL: Industry Catch up

*Looking at the changes in the last year*

The eVTOL industry is experiencing a shakeout as it approaches commercialization next year. Lilium and Volocopter faced financial challenges they could not overcome, and are now owned by Chinese companies.

Airbus withdrew from the industry, ending its ten-year development of the CityAirbus, with no plans to bring it to market. Boeing continues developing the autonomous Wisk air eVTOL but has released very little news recently.

The market is a true emerging technology likely to be a significant disruptive force. The 2024 market was estimated to be $1.2 billion, and forecasts for 2030 range from $4 billion to $30 billion, with a median forecast of $14 billion and a CAGR of 66%.

As with all these markets, choosing the right companies to invest in is crucial for success. We missed the big winner in the last 12 months but avoided the three losers.

Having reviewed each company's Q1 earnings call, a few trends are emerging. The most important are the realization that the current crop of aircraft does not have sufficient range or payload to satisfy large sections of the potential market and the need for Autonomy. There will never be enough pilots to run all of these air taxis.

JOBY, ACHR, and EVTL are all developing hybrid propulsion systems that extend range and payload. In light of this, I am adding New Horizon Technology and XTI to my watch list. They are small, underfunded outfits that started working on hybrid powertrains some years ago. New Horizon is already building its 7-seater 1,000-mile hybrid aircraft, and XTI is testing its hybrid propulsion system.

I will review Horizon soon and have been offered an interview with the CEO.

## Company Personalities

The nature of the management of each company remains clear

**Archer:** The perpetual overpromisers

**Joby:** Prioritizing Actions over Words

**Eve**: Slow and Methodical

**Vertical:** Detailed planners

**Ehang:** Fast movers

### Hybrid Propulsion

JOBY flew its hybrid machine [523 miles earlier last year without advance notice](https://www.jobyaviation.com/news/joby-demonstrates-potential-regional-journeys-landmark-hydrogen-electric-flight/).

Vertical announced that they have a hybrid propulsion system already developed. It is currently being tested on a rig that will be fitted into a VX4 in Q1 next year.

Archer announced a partnership with Anudril to work on a system. This shows that Archer is a long way behind, Joby is flying, Vertical is testing, and Archer is working on a plan.

Neither Ehang nor Eve has announced plans for a hybrid drive train.

## Autonomy

**Ehang** is already fully autonomous.

**Joby** bought Xwing in June 2024 and flew autonomous aircraft as part of a US training operation, landing at multiple airports.

**Vertical** has partnered with Honeywell to develop its flight control and autonomous operations.

**Eve** is relying on Embraer and Embraer’s project Easy, which is already flying autonomously in Brazil.

**Archer** has to use Boeing autonomy, it was part of the settlement to avoid litigation with Wisk Air. Boeing has not released any recent news about the progress of this project, apart from changes to the management team at Wisk.

### Certification

**Joby** is 62% through the final phase and has consistently released updates of where it is. Joby has begun Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) flights and has multiple piloted aircraft flying simultaneously. Joby maintains 2025 for certification and Q1 2026 for first commercial flights. In the past, they have consistently beaten their guidelines.

**Archer and Vertical** are planning their first piloted flights later this quarter. Archer has been waiting for a final ruling from the FAA (they said everyone else was waiting for the same ruling, but that is not true; both Joby and Vertical already have their complete authorization plans).

**Vertical** is targeting 2028 for certification and full operations in 2030.

**Eve** is still on the drawing board but is currently building its first prototype also targeting 2028 and 2030.

**Ehang** is fully certified and operational.

Archer says it will become certified in the USA and the Middle East this year and start operations next year. This seems a stretch, to say the least, and I expect to hear a story about why they are having to delay soon.

## Manufacturing

**Ehang** has a fully operational large-scale manufacturing plant and is expanding its manufacturing footprint.

**Eve** has a complete manufacturing facility through its parent Embraer.

**Joby** has a pilot line in operation and has manufactured 5 aircraft. They have agreed to fund the construction of the commercial-scale site with Toyota, and the fit-out of the building has begun.

**Archer** has yet to agree to its manufacturing agreement with Stellantis but claims it will be making 650 planes a year in 2026. Once more, I doubt it. They are currently producing aircraft at two sites, but are vague about exact numbers and dates.

**Vertical** is going asset-light and will subcontract its manufacturing once type approval is received.

### Business Models

The business models are heavily dependent on service and maintenance. Both Vertical and Eve expect half of their revenue to come from the annual battery change in their aircraft, and all companies are working to develop maintenance and support partners around the world.

Vertical and Eve are focused on selling aircraft to airlines and as a result have developed large order books. The quality of those orders is in doubt but many of them will likely turn into revenue.

Joby and Archer plan to sell and operate aircraft. They concentrate on the US markets and the Middle East for their first operations. Joby has demonstrated its capability at multiple airports and has already released its initial flight routes; the first aircraft for the Middle East is in transit. Archer is yet to fly in any city and is planning its first piloted flight soon. The first aircraft for the Middle East will leave sometime later this year.

Ehang remains focused on low-altitude tourism in China, with multiple orders to fulfill and commercial operations beginning.

## Quarter 1 Reviews

**Joby**

I think Joby is a long way ahead in this race. They don't say much, but they deliver a lot. I think they will be the first to be certified in the US and the Middle East and the first to fly in both. I will remain long JOBY and add whenever possible.

In the last quarter, Joby achieved significant milestones, including a 12-percentage-point acceleration in FAA certification, reaching 62% completion, and its first pilot-on-board transition flight. The company is expanding flight testing, now operating five aircraft from two locations and achieving multiple daily flights. Joby also powered on its fifth pilot production aircraft and announced an expanded manufacturing facility to double capacity. Partnerships are growing, notably with Virgin Atlantic in the UK for future service, and continued support from major companies like Toyota.

Joby is pursuing multiple market strategies, including direct sales, partner-operated services in international markets, and direct consumer operations for air taxi services. Financial highlights include securing agreements for Toyota's $500M commitment and a Q1 net loss of $82 million, which shows a significant decrease compared to the previous quarter due to a favorable warrant and share revaluation. Despite some industry challenges, such as FAA "headwinds," Joby maintains its timeline for commercialization and is actively developing autonomous and longer-range hybrid aircraft capabilities. The company is also preparing for the launch of eVTOL services in Dubai, starting with a limited number of aircraft and vertiports, with plans for exponential growth as infrastructure expands.

**Archer**

I do not trust this management team and never have. I still believe they stole the designs for the Maker aircraft (the one before the current machine) from Wisk Air when they realized their first machine was flawed and were lucky to escape the courts when Boeing bought Wisk. I do not believe their timelines or accept many things they said in Q1 earnings. They have powered the share price ahead, but I think it is floating on hot air.

Archer is moving forward with its plans. Their "Launch Program" aims to deploy aircraft in early adopter markets, starting with a delivery to Abu Dhabi Aviation (ADA) in the UAE. Piloted flight testing is underway in California, with piloted flights in the UAE planned soon. Archer is collaborating with Etihad for pilot training and is adapting existing helipads for initial operations in Abu Dhabi, with plans to announce their initial national network of twelve nodes. They are also ramping up manufacturing capabilities at their facilities in Silicon Valley and Georgia.

Archer is also focusing on strategic partnerships, including an exclusive partnership with Anduril for hybrid electric VTOL aircraft for defense applications, and a collaboration with Palantir for an AI-powered aviation software platform. They have secured additional equity capital and have a potential funding agreement with Stellantis to support the scaling of Midnight production. Archer has resolved a key certification issue with the FAA, advancing their path to compliance, and is progressing in their relationships with both the FAA and GCAA. They aim to balance aircraft deployment with manufacturing ramp-up, with a goal of reaching target gross margins at higher production volumes.

**Eve Air**

Eve benefits from its parent company's knowledge and understanding of certification. Compared to Joby, they are going slowly, but I fully expect them to meet their timeline and become operational.

Eve Air Mobility is actively progressing with its eVTOL program. They are currently conducting ground tests on their full-scale engineering prototype, a remotely piloted aircraft, in preparation for its first flight, which will validate their previous models. They also test individual motors, with the pusher motor already tested and lift motor testing scheduled before installation. Parts are being built for their first five conforming aircraft, and the assembly line is ready. Lab tests will be conducted on all eight motors to verify thrust, and a full-scale wiring harness is built but still on a test rig. Their Sao Jose dos Campos facility will exclusively assemble the conforming prototypes, with five or six planned for certification.

Eve has a significant preorder backlog of around 2,800 non-binding letters of intent for its eVTOL aircraft, valued at nearly $14 billion, from 28 customers across nine countries-Eve did mention some of these LOIs will not become orders due to changing priorities. They are also focused on maintenance and support with contracts for their EVE TechCare suite, potentially generating $1.6 billion in revenue. Financials show a Q1 2025 investment of $45 million in program development and an $8 million SG&A expense, resulting in a net loss of $49 million. They have $288 million in cash and a total liquidity of $411 million, sufficient to fund operations through 2026. Eve is on track to meet this year's milestones, with initial flights of the full-scale prototype planned for 2025 and a piloted, manned conforming prototype to be built this year and flown next year. They are actively onboarding new customers globally and exploring various funding options for future development.

**Vertical Aerospace**

Now the only European player and likely to be a big player. They have improved their management and balance sheet and have a clear route to certification with the UK authorities.

Vertical Aerospace's Flightpath 2030 strategy aims to lead the eVTOL sector by 2030. They have set clear financial and operating metrics, including a certification timeline, and report significant progress. Their eVTOL aircraft emphasizes safety, passenger and luggage capacity, and comfort. They are on track to complete piloted flight tests in 2025, with a capital-efficient program that spends approximately 75% less than peers through certification. Key technical milestones have been achieved, including record-breaking piloted high-speed taxi tests. Piloted wingborne flight is anticipated to commence in Q2, potentially positioning them among the top two OEMs for piloted flight tests. They plan to double their airborne test fleet this year.

Vertical Aerospace has finalized and expanded their partnership with Honeywell for flight controls and aircraft management systems, a contract valued at approximately $1 billion over the next 10 years. They are adopting the combined charging standard (CCS). The company has announced an advanced hybrid eVTOL program with a range exceeding 1,000 miles for defense, cargo, emergency services, and long-range commercial missions. Their larger airframe aims to dominate the eVTOL space. Vertical anticipates that battery replacements will constitute 50% of its revenue by the 2030s with a gross margin over 40%. They are developing advanced battery packs and hybrid powertrains, with the hybrid aircraft expected to fly in Q2 2026. Q1 2025 net cash used in operations was about $30 million, with full-year spending guidance of $110-$125 million. Certification with the CAA, aligned with EASA's SC-VTOL standard, is targeted for 2028. They have a global order book and are collaborating with customers to develop their airframe.

**Ehang**

Ehang is fully operational and not really in the same market as the others. It has a monopoly in autonomous flight operations and remains the only certified eVTOL operator. They are focused on fulfilling their orders, building the manufacturing base, and extending their certification from China to other Asian countries.

Q1 results have not been released yet.

## Conclusion

I still like JOBY and EH and will hold these positions; I will investigate Horizon, and may invest when that review is complete.
