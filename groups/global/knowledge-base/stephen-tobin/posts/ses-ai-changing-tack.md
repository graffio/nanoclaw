# SES AI: Changing Tack

*A review of the 4 trade alerts issued in January*

I have issued four buy alerts so far, and now I will review each situation.

# Firstly, let's talk about SES AI.

There have been some significant changes in the company's management, which is causing concern. The president and chief strategy officer, Rohit Makharia, left the company without any reason on January 22nd. However, SES has not released any press statement regarding this event. They only issued a press release on January 29th, welcoming Brian Krzanich to the board as an independent director. Mr Krzanich is a former CEO at Intel, and his appointment would normally have boosted the share price. But the sudden and unexpected departure of Rohit has overshadowed his appointment. In December, Rohit sold a large number of shares (212,000) in SES, which suggests that he knew he was about to leave. This sale brings the total number of insider sells in 2023 to 26, which is unusually high. Rohit has sold almost 600,000 shares in the last 12 months, which raises more questions than answers. Selling on such a large scale makes me reconsider my bullish stance.

Rohit had been a big-name signing when he arrived in 2021; he had previously been with GM for 18 years and led much of their work on new battery technology. We have no information about why he left, but his departure and the heavy selling that preceded it is not a good sign.

It could be that GM is no longer interested in SES and that Rohit's contacts were less valuable; it may also be that the decision was mutual, more of a retirement perhaps, as Rohit was allowed to keep his 2024 bonus. The big issue is the way this has been handled, no information to shareholders is a problem for me.

As you know, I am a relatively short-term trader; I do not get attached to these companies and would rather take a small loss and look to re-buy if things change.

The previously identified technical pattern broke down when the news was released. I will close this trade for a loss of around 15%. SES will stay on my buy list, and I will await confirmation from the company about its plans before buying again.

I still like the tech, and if the B sample news arrives, I will buy again; investors with a longer-term view may want to continue holding.

A turnaround may have occurred in the previously identified area, and if that's the case, a target price of around $7 seems reasonable. The trade was initially taken at half size. I plan to upgrade it to full size and put the stop-loss below the identified turning point to limit risk.

### Cybin

Cybin announced that the FDA had cleared its phase 2A study of CYB004, which is the first of a number of FDA approvals we are awaiting this quarter and bodes well for the bullish call.

Price action did not really respond to the news, and the price continues to drift lower. This is a penny stock trade, and I will continue to hold for now. The stop loss remains at 50% and take profit 200%
