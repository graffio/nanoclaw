# Trade Alert: Buying Electrovaya

*Buying again following meeting with CEO*

We exited Electrovaya (ELVA) earlier this year when it became clear they would miss 2024 revenue targets by a long margin, and the long-signalled bus market sales were not going well.

Last Thursday, I had a 45-minute interview with the CEO, during which I discussed my concerns regarding the company. The CEO gave me a lot of new information, much of which is not available in the public domain but is not secret, covering all aspects of the business.

The detailed article will be published on SeekingAlpha early next week but I am buying now.

[Subscribe now](https://stephentobin.substack.com/subscribe?)

## The revenue miss

ELVA's three largest customers, all Fortune 500 retail operations, requested a delay in delivering batteries for forklift trucks because the construction of new warehouses was running behind schedule. All three companies were experiencing supply train problems with electrical switching operations, meaning the facilities would not be ready until Q1 2025. Since the meeting, these companies have placed additional orders for an additional $3.5 million, confirming in my mind that they remain committed in the longer term.

## The Bus market

This is going nowhere; it is no longer a potential market and is not part of Electrovaya's short-term plans. Strangely, the problem is that the batteries are too good for the auto market. They provide a lifespan of 3.5 million miles, far bigger than needed, and as a result, they can’t get the premium price they ask for.

# Technology

The infinity battery (now 28,000 supplied) has delivered a life cycle of 14,000 and is still getting 95% power. This is in a different league from the rest of the market, many of whom are at 1,000 for 80%. Nobody is close to this performance, and it means the company has a technological moat around its business that looks sustainable.

The battery's safety is also next-generation, ELVA has supplied 28,000 infininity batteries and over 30,000 of the previous generation. These 58,000 batteries have been in the field for almost 10 years. There has not been a single safety incident or battery failure. This astonishing performance again separates Electrovaya from the rest of the market.

## New Markets

They have an advantage in mission-critical, high-usage use cases. They identified four vertical markets: material handling, Mining / Construction, Defense, and locomotives.

They lead the material handling market through their Toyota contract, which has been expanded in both time and brand. The CEO guided to 90% of revenue coming from this market in 2025. (in 2024 it will be 100%)

### Construction,

ELVA has just been awarded an SOP from Sumitomo, one of the 5 large Japanese trading houses, for excavator use. Delivery will begin Q4 2025 but begin to ramp in Q1 2026. Sumitomo is also offering substantial other incentives and potential markets. Sumitomo Finance will be a major contributor going forward.

### Defense

They received an SOP from a defense company (unnamed) with a start date in mid-2025. This approval process took 18 months and is expected to be the first of many.

### Locomotives

An agreement with IRT has numerous short-track locomotives being tested by the US Defence Department.

## Capacity

After the award of the Sumitomo SOP, EXIM bank approved a $50 million loan that will be enough to build out the New York facility, doubling production in time for the Sumitomo contract.

Earnings are next week so I am buying Today. I have a target of $10 per share. I have puy an order on IBKR for MidPrice execution.
