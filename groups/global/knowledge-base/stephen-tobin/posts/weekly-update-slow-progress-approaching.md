# Weekly Update: Slow Progress Approaching Year End

*Another Friday down day spoilt a good week*

The indices turned lower on Friday with the Nasdaq leading the way down. Our tech-heavy portfolio followed the indices lower, and a couple of stocks dropped significantly after news releases.

The portfolio fell $397 (2%) to $17,922. In December, we are showing a 2% profit, while the S&P 500 is down 0.3%.

One holding, a space stock, fell by almost a third following news about dilution and changes to the balance sheet; the business case was unaffected, and the position remains just above water.

I opened two new trades last week. Recent trades are all performing reasonably well, considering the prevailing risk-off sentiment. I made a small adjustment to the plan, taking a more safety-first approach, in mid-November when risk assets started to sell off quite sharply.

## Emerging Technology Investing

The core of my trading strategy remains focused on identifying and investing in high-potential emerging technology sectors. While these opportunities inherently carry increased risk and volatility, they are the foundation of the outsized performance we target, offering the highest potential for substantial short- and medium-term gains.

I have ten companies on the immediate buy list at the moment 1 in each of batteries, defence, new materials, Sensing, semiconductors and AI with 2 in electricity generation and 2 in recycling.

It is unlikely all of the companies on the buy list will become trades in the immediate but I would expect them all to become investments by the end of Q1 2026

I continue to look for new sectors and Psychedelic drug therapy remains the one I expect to add to the portfolio in the near term.

The one missing Sector is Quantum Computing, our most profitable sector to date. I do not currently have any exposure or any trades planned in the immediate future. Prices in the sector currently make it unattractive on a risk-reward basis.

The Portfolio as a whole remains above target as we approach the halfway stage in the five-year plan.

## Weekly Digest: 8 – 13 December 2025

The past week was quiet for the coverage universe: only **Byrna Technologies** issued preliminary fiscal-quarter results,

## Byrna Technologies (BYRN)

### Preliminary FY-4Q25 Earnings (quarter ended 30 Nov 2025)

-   **Revenue:** US$35.1 million, up **26 %** year-on-year from the comparable FY-4Q24 period.
    
-   **Full-year revenue:** US$118.0 million, up **38 %** versus FY-2024.
    
-   **Profitability metrics:** Gross margin, operating income, and EPS were not disclosed in the preliminary release.
    
-   **Guidance:** Management did not provide formal FY-2026 outlook in this update.
    

### Management Commentary

-   CEO **Bryan Ganz** highlighted a year of “clear operational progress,” citing strengthened brand visibility, broader physical retail distribution, and the launch of the **Byrna CL** launcher as key growth drivers.
    
-   Ganz added that inventory conversion supported stronger cash generation in 4Q25.
    

### Balance-Sheet & Capital Actions

-   **Cash & equivalents:** > US$15 million at 30 Nov 2025, up from US$9 million at 31 Aug 2025, reflecting inventory monetisation.
    
-   **Share buybacks:** The company repurchased > US$1 million of common stock during the quarter.
    

Aurora Innovation (AUR)

-   **Detmar Logistics** selected Aurora to deploy an expanded fleet of autonomous trucks for a major energy producer, broadening an existing relationship.
    
-   The press release reiterated that forward-looking statements are subject to the risk factors detailed in Aurora’s 2024 Form 10-K.
    

Vertical Aerospace (EVTL)

-   **Released the new Aircraft.** The newly released aircraft was a big upgrade to the previous design, more than expected by the market and caused a bit of a pullback, no changes were made to the time line and the new aircraft had multiple improvements. EVTL plan to build seven new aircraft and will offer multiple configurations to airlines, I would expect to see new orders being accepted from Q1.
    

Virgin Galactic (SPCE)

-   **Dilution:** Multiple agreements announced to reduce the companies debts included a plan to sell shares and pre funded warrants. Money raised will be used to repurchase convertible notes and retire some debt.
    

WeRide (WRD)

-   **Dubai Launch:** in collaboration with UBER the robotaxi service went live on Dubai roads
    

NorthStar (ROOF)

-   **Dilution:** A CAD$30 million shelf agreement was announced to fund the company’s growth, dilution is one of the prices we pay for investing in these small companies. I still hope to build the position in ROOF when commercial operations are announced. It should be this month but delays are another one of the prices we pay for investing in emerging tech.
