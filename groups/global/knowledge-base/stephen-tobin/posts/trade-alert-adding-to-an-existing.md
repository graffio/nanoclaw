## Headlines from earnings

-   Record Quarterly and Annual Revenues, Up 48.4% and 11.7% Year-Over-Year, Respectively
    
-   First GAAP Profitable Quarter; Adjusted Net Income (Non-GAAP) Up 96.4% Year-Over-Year
    
-   Non-GAAP Profitability Achieved for Second Consecutive Year
    
-   EH216-S Commercial Operations in China Expected to Launch in March 2026
    
-   VT35 Unveiled with First Public Demonstration Flight; Initial Deliveries Completed
    
-   Thailand AAM Sandbox Trials and Commercial Operation License in Progress
    

The start of operations in March 2026 is a big deal, I said recently they were expecting 100 ground crews to become licensed in H1 2026, allowing full-scale commercial operations to begin, although the full training system is still under development, the company reported the pool of operators has been significantly expanded.

A second major announcement was the start of sales of the VT-35, Ehang’s second aircraft, with 5 sold in Q4. In total, EH sold 100 aircraft in Q4 v 78 in Q4 2024 and 41 in Q3 2025. Total sales in 2025 were 221 units, up only slightly on 2024. The growth in Q4 allowed them to deliver an all-time high in sales, suggesting momentum has returned.

Gross margin increased to 62% (from 60.8), and the operating loss dropped to US$0.9 million from US$13.4 million a year earlier. The company maintained its strong balance sheet, with US$162 million in cash on hand.

**Commercial Operations**

Ehang has guided ticketed sales in two locations in March for fee-paying tourists. Can’t overstate the importance of this milestone

**More Guidance.**

The long-range VT-25 has started selling. It is still going through the approval process, but has completed demonstration flights, including transition. No guidance yet on the certification schedule

Ehang expects the Thailand sandbox to be operational this year. Japan testing has been expanded to 18 cities in partnership with Mitsubishi, Qatar has granted operational authorization, and flights between designated points have begun, not yet fee-paying.

The manufacturing footprint was expanded, enabling 1,000 units per year (compared to Joby at 50 per year), and the firefighting EH216-F has entered production and been delivered to emergency rescue departments.

**Conclusion**

We have had Ehang in the SW Trade portfolio for 560 days, making it our longest investment. The position remains underwater, but I think patience will pay off. They remain ahead of the field, and with the start of commercial flights this year, the press and news coverage, as well as the newly developed profitability, should drive this stock higher.

**Current Positions**

Both EH investments are quite old and relatively small compared to today's standard position, allowing for this investment.
