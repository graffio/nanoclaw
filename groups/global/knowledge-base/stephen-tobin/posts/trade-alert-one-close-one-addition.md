## Trade Alert: Closing SES.AI

Following Wolfpack's short report, I conducted a deep dive and concluded that the risk is too great.

Issues are:

1.  I suspect the Li-ION joint development programs are lost, the two remaining ones were supposed to renew this quarter, but there has been no announcement. The CEO did refer to one of them, saying he expects a continuation in Q1 2026. I think this will be some “working together” agreement on new electrolytes rather than the Li-ION JV.
    
2.  The 2170 battery, released with great fanfare in January, using the first electrolyte discovered by the MU, does not seem to be selling, with no reported contracts.
    
3.  The Drone market has not progressed; no major contracts have been announced, and the two original ones are with smaller companies, which is not sufficient to drive sales.
    
4.  The company set revenue targets of $10- $20 million at the start of the year. In Q3, they reported record revenue, but 50% of that revenue came from the newly acquired Battery Storage company. Management did not change guidance and reiterated their intention to remain within the same range. If we take out the battery storage products, it means guidance on the existing business was cut to $3 -$13 million.
    

I could be wrong and will review after next earnings, but the risks seem weighted to the downside for now, and I will exit with a small loss today. A big rise in the bookings for MU would change my mind, but so far, we have not seen evidence of this.

SES has been a good company for us indeed; it is a top performer, so I am sorry to see it go. (Chart below includes the open trade)

**Investment Case: Electro Optic Systems (EOS)**

It is all about counter drone technology, there is no proven winner in this field, but EOS has a range of solutions and appears to be picking up orders and expanding their product offering.

I am slightly reducing my target price to $9.60 due to the MARRS acquisition's capex requirements. This will reduce short-term cash generation but is likely to increase long-term revenue and profits. My revised revenue and EBITDA targets are in this image.

I covered the company in [Trade Alert #78 on 22 October.](https://stephentobin.substack.com/p/trade-alert-buying-drone-stocks?utm_source=publication-search)

EOS is a market leader in Counter-Unmanned Aerial Systems (C-UAS) and is leveraged to increasing global defense spending on C-UAS technologies. Positive news flow is expected over the next 6 months from C-UAS and Remote Weapon Station (RWS) contract awards.

**Key Drivers of Todays Addition:**

1.  **Strategic C-UAS Acquisition:** EOS has acquired MARSS Group’s drone interceptor business for €5.5m (A$10m).
    
    -   **Technology:** Interceptor drones are an emerging, hard-kill C-UAS technology, effective against threats like loitering munitions (e.g., Shahed drones). The performance of this type of drone in Ukraine makes it a must-have for all defence agencies.
        
    -   **Low-Cost Strategy:** EOS is positioning its interceptor as a lowest-cost solution, aiming to price it below US$10k per unit, significantly under competitors (US$50-60k). This is crucial for cost asymmetry in warfare.
        
    -   **Development:** Expect 12-24 months of further development and up to A$10m in investment over three years, with potential customer funding.
        
    -   **Market:** This acquisition favourably leverages EOS to the global C-UAS thematic, providing a complementary layer to existing C-UAS solutions (jamming, kinetic, directed energy).
        
2.  **Strong Contract Visibility:**
    
    -   The recent A$20m Slinger contract award (Nov-25) secures an estimated 50% of my 2026 revenue forecast.
        
    -   Material upside exists for 2027 and beyond, contingent on the award of at least one additional laser contract in CY26e.
        
    -   They remain the only company with a High Energy Laser sale booked.
        
3.  **Pullback in Place:**
    
    -   Following the recent pullback EOS has been on my pre buy list as I work through the fundamentals, it is another example of price doing badly while the company fundamentals improve.

**Competitive Landscape (Interceptor Drone Market):**

-   Anduril (US), Fortem Technologies (US), Origin Robotics (Latvia), and the Merops program (NATO) are key competitors.
    

The Merops drone is an immediate competitor and has been responsible for over 1,000 loitering drone destructions in Ukraine (50% of total). It validates the interceptor drone idea. EOS have the simplest and oldest marketing plan, they will offer the same functionality as the NATO Merops drone at a fraction of the price.

Other key awards include:

-   Missile-based C-UAS solutions (Raytheon’s Coyote, AeroVironment’s Freedom Eagle-1) cost significantly more (US$100k - $200k per unit).
    

**Key Risks:**

-   **Sales Revenue Risk:** Dependence on lumpy, non-recurring, one-off contracts.
    
-   **Geopolitical Risks:** Exposure to changes in political stability and conflict resolution, impacting market demand. Management have said that a resolution of the conflict in Ukraine would not have a material impact on EOS but it would almost certainly have an impact on the share price.
