# Event: Meeting with D-Wave Management

*A lot of additional information adds color to this trading opportunity*

On Friday, July 26th, I had a Zoom meeting with Alan Baratz and John Markovic. D-Wave called the meeting to discuss my recent article published on SA. Alan Baratz led the meeting and used a slide deck prepared for discussions with investment banks to direct the conversation.

D-Wave raised several important points, which I will summarize below. They were unwilling to have the meeting recorded but implied they may do a recorded interview in the future. I think the whole purpose of the meeting was for them to decide if they want to work with me in the future. At the end of the meeting, I was invited to attend the upcoming earnings release and have a private discussion to receive more information.

## Key points from the discussion

D-Wave's ownership of all its technology, independent of universities, sets it apart. With over 200 patents on quantum, the third-largest number in the industry, and sixty percent of these patents covering annealing and gate machines, D-Wave's technology is truly exclusive.

They believe it will be 7-10 years before any company produces a functional gate computer, saying that until then, they will be too noisy intermediate machines.

Annealing machines cannot solve differential equations needed in chemistry and other applications, so they are restricted in their applications.

They used a research paper from the Boston Consulting Group to discuss market opportunities. BCG believes the 30-year TAM for quantum computing is $850 billion and the 15-year TAM is $400 billion. BCG believes 25% of the market will be suitable for annealing technology only and 25% for gate computing only. The remaining 50% could be ideal for both technologies.

D-Wave showed evidence that gate computing will never be able to solve optimization problems as efficiently or accurately as annealing technology.

D-Wave believes it is the only company developing annealing technology and that its patent portfolio provides a moat around the business. This means a competitor will take billions of dollars and at least 10 years to catch up. As a result, D-Wave should capture the entire TAM for annealing computers.

The newly released Advantage2 processor has 1,200 qubits with improved connectivity and coherence. It was used to produce the Quantum Supremacy result published in Nature. While working on this paper, D-Wave hired a team of university professors to try to spend a year working on heuristics that would enable the problem to be solved classically; it was when they received confirmation from the team it could not be done classically that they published. A team at Caltech is trying to disprove the paper but so far has failed. The Advantage2 processor will reach 7,000 qubits soon; that device is already manufactured, and the Advantage3 will arrive in 2026/7.

When the Advantage3 arrives, D-Wave believes it can run as a replacement for large-scale data centers in conjunction with a small number of GPUs. The electricity cost will be divided by 1,000, as the D-Wave machine uses no more electricity than a single GPU (when air cooling is taken into account). The Advantage3 contains unreleased tech that speeds up x20 over the current machine.

#### Gate Computers

The current qubits are not suitable for gate computing. They only deliver spin up or spin down when measured, not the 3D space result required for gate computers. The current Qubits are flux superconductors, and the technology developed to manipulate and read them will apply directly to fluxonium qubits, on which D-Wave is developing a gate computer. Fluxonium qubits will fit into the manufacturing and technology stack already developed. They are going straight to an error-corrected logical qubit. Each logical qubit will be a 5 x 5 array of single qubits. The first released machine will be a scaled useful device, but it is probably a decade away.

Zapata remains a key partner, and the relationship has been extended. It includes the data center use case and training of AI large and small language models. The new hybrid solvers are key enabling technologies that allow parts of a problem to be pushed to GPU or quantum.

The recently publicized NTT Docomo computing use case, which runs on D-Wave machines, is a significant milestone for the company. D-Wave's machines are the only ones available worldwide, highlighting the company's unique position in the quantum computing market.

#### Finances

The CFO was not 100% happy with my DCF; however, after the discussion, he agreed that my costs and earnings were similar to his. The problem was a technical accounting one: the difference between GAAP EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA that D-Wave uses, and Free Cash Flow to the Firm, which I use.

The CFO is happy they have the funds in place to continue developing the business, referring to two significant shelf registrations and his intention to raise money by selling stock.
