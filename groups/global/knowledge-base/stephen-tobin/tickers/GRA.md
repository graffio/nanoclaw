## Company Overview

NanoXplore is a Canadian graphene technology company that produces and supplies graphene-enhanced materials for transportation, oil/gas, and industrial applications. They are one of the few companies commercially producing graphene at scale.

**Products:**

- Graphene-enhanced polymers and composites for automotive
- Tribograf graphene lubricant additives (in partnership with CPChem)
- Sheet moulding compound (SMC) for automotive lightweighting
- Active anode material (CSPG) initiative — cancelled in 2026

**Revenue model:** Supply of graphene and graphene-enhanced materials to automotive OEMs and industrial customers. All prices in CAD.

- Q2 FY2026: CAD 27.6M revenue (-17% YoY, +18% sequential)
- Adjusted gross margin: 21.5%
- Cash: CAD 30.1M
- FY2026 narrowed guidance: CAD 115-120M

**Key contracts:**

- Volvo SMC contract: CAD 9-10M annual revenue, production starting H1 2028
- CPChem Tribograf deliveries began
- First commercial dry-process graphene module on schedule for April 2026

**Strategic changes:** Cancelled CAD 100M active anode material (CSPG) initiative to focus on core graphene business.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis:** Graphene is an emerging material with massive potential across multiple industries. NanoXplore is one of the few companies commercially producing graphene at scale. Stephen has been following graphene generally — "Graphene generally is developing. Which is the best bet is a question, if we can get on the right company a fortune is to be made."

**Current view (Feb-Mar 2026):** Position down significantly (-28% as of Mar 08). Earnings showed most metrics worse. Stephen acknowledged concerns but remains optimistic on the graphene sector broadly: "Just need to be open and careful." Dependent on a pickup in auto manufacturing, which may not arrive soon given oil crisis.

**Concerns:** Oil crisis assessment — Short-term risk Medium, Medium-term risk High. "NanoXplore is dependent on a pickup in auto manufacturing, which might not arrive for some time." Revenue declined 17% YoY although sequential improvement noted. The stock's performance has been "hampered more by the market than the business" per Stephen.
