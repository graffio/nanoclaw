*Product Launch imminent*

I discussed Quantum-Si (QSI) in an August article published on the Seeking Alpha platform. I covered its technology and potential products, it is operating in the Proteomics industry, a disruptive part of the pharma industry likely to be larger and more influential than the gene therapy and editing industry.

At the time, I believed the price fall might have been over, but it took another leg down. The technical chart does look much more bullish now that the second leg down appears to be complete, and evidence of a turn higher is clear. I am writing a second article on QSI with an in-depth view of the progress made since the last article, which should be published early next week.

# Fundamental Outline

QSI's new CEO has been in place for a year now and has successfully transformed the company from a science experiment into a commercial operation. The restructuring has resulted in more commercially defined goals for the company.

QSI has been following a controlled launch of its products in the last quarter, but a full launch is expected in Q1 2024. That launch should improve awareness and begin the march toward profitability.

QSI produces hardware that laboratories can use to analyze the proteome. They sell a hardware device named Platinum that uses a kit based on their proprietary M2 silicon chip to analyze single protein molecules. The kit is the primary consumable device and includes various reagents that enable the machine to identify different parts of the protein being analyzed.

The CEO has shelved a second piece of hardware called Carbon. Carbon was designed to cover sample preparation, but the new strategy is to utilize existing prep methods rather than change customers' workflow.

Version 2 of the kit will be launched slightly before the full commercial launch of Platinum with newer, more accurate reagents and an improved feature set.

Recent press releases have focussed on the appointment of distributors worldwide and the first scientific paper being written using data gathered from Platinum.

The user base remains academic for now, but that should become more varied with the increased publicity surrounding the launch, improved feature set, and published academic papers.
