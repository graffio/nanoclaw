# Weekly Review: Pullbacks are an opportunity

*Large cash balance means big profits ahead *

The drawdown has continued as risk assets continue to sell off. As I trade in one of the riskier parts of the market, small-cap pre-profit companies, we feel the pullback more.

The All time high of the portfolio was in mid October when it hit $20,250, since then it has fallen to $16,262 a drawdown of 19.7%. A peak-to-trough 19.7% is reasonable given the high-beta portfolio I hold, and we now have a record cash balance of $8,717, more than 50% of the portfolio and greater than the total investment in the account ($7,000).

I am gearing up for a spending spree; many of the stocks I follow are down over 50% and are now of the bargain basement variety. In the past I have struggled to find companies that were undervalued; many were premium priced due to their exciting technology. In the coming weeks I will be identifying the best opportunities.

## DroneShield

We were hit hard by the collapse of the Australian counter-drone manufacturer after its CEO sold his shares. It could be that I bought a complete con, and the CEO boosted the share price before cashing out at the expense of retail shareholders. It could be the board’s incompetence of allowing its senior managers to cash out in one large sale or it could be nothing more than press speculation.

Suppose we assume the company is real and it actually has products, which seems likely considering the repeat orders from NATO government companies and the proven use of the products in Ukraine and Israel. In that case, the company and its shareholders have suffered due to the small size of the Australian market and the added scrutiny that comes with it. During the same month, the CEO of Droneshield sold $40 million in shares, and the CEO of D-Wave, a similar-sized company in the quantum field with no significant revenue, sold $40 million without any press coverage, broker reaction, or significant price movement. It is pretty likely that Alan, the CEO of D-Wave, spoke to his leading brokers before making the sale to ensure they were OK with it. Alan is a savvy operator; clearly, Oleg from DroneShield is not. Either way, we recorded a 59% loss, our largest loss to date.

## Longer Term: $100K on target

We remain above target on the longer-term goal of trading $250 a month into $100k, in the last 27 months, we have invested $250 a month and now have over $16k. The annualized return so far is 122%, well above the 82% we need to make the target, and we are almost halfway through the project.

## Simple Plans are Best

The trading plan is simple: buy emerging technology companies in sectors that offer outsized returns.

The targeted sectors do change as industries evolve. Recycling is becoming a key sector, along with energy generation and autonomous vehicles. The drone and LiDAR sectors can no longer be considered emerging and will fall off my radar in the coming weeks.

## Next Steps

The next stage of the trading plan will be to deploy the large cash balance by acquiring emerging technologies at discounted valuations to drive future profits.

The nature of pullbacks and the way small companies are hit hardest mean that those who trade them offer the greatest potential. I have generated a lot of profit and now sit on a lot of cash; I am going to make the most of this opportunity.

I will look at the stocks we still own and decide which, if any, I want to add to when the opportunity presents. The decision will be based on the commercial progress the companies have made, the sector they are in and how far the price of the stock has fallen. (I cover this in the paid part of this email)

After that, I will turn to the watchlist using the same criteria of commercial progress and discounted price to identify the best opportunities for outsized returns.

With such a large cash balance and an enormous pullback I am very confident about the results we will achieve in 2026 and am looking forward to the next few weeks as we build the positions that will deliver enhanced profits.

As soon as I think a low is in place, I will be making multiple purchases, adding to many of the holdings in the portfolio.

I expect to buy a second recycling micro cap next week, it has not suffered any drawdown so I want to get in ASAP, I am targeting Tuesday for the trade alert.

Cadre continues to make progress, in recent weeks they announced a $50 million win for one of their US defence subsidiaries, the bomb disposal contract is finally complete and the move into nuclear energy looks to be going well. I like this business.

The technical pattern is not that great, perhaps because of the quality of the business and the fact that it pays a growing dividend, it has not pulled back into an area I would consider buying. Price is down 13% peak to trough so it is probably not a bargain buy at the moment, but I will want to keep it.

**EH**

Progress has been a little slow, I had been invited to do a launch flight but that has been pushed back to the new year without a firm date. Clearly, the 216's operations are taking longer to commercialize than expected. The new aircraft, with its longer range, is making good progress and will likely move quickly through the approvals process. Sales are going OK and the manufacturing operation is large and growing, it is just those paying customer flights, without them I don’t want to add and if they don’t arrive I want to get out.

**ACMR**

Our only silicon chip-related company produced a very mediocre earnings report and average forward guidance. Margins are under pressure and we didn’t see the kind of knock out guidance we might have expected from a company that is operating in the heart of the AI chip boom.

I think it is time to exit this one.

**APTV**

Commercially everything seems to be going well, we have the plan to float the subsidiary which will raise significant cash but it probably will not happen until the markets improve. This may account for the significant pullback the stock has seen. APTV remains at the heart of the autonomous and connected vehicle market, as a large company it will be affected by drops in production by car makers,the last earnings showed it is improving the number of dollars per car. The Motional JV offers additional autonomous taxi upside and I will be tracking that carefully.

Despite the large pullback, 21% peak to trough, I am not going to add too much uncertainty regarding the spin-off and what the future demand will look like for the new APTV. I intend to hold, but it is not a firm conviction.

**ASPI**

ASPI delivered an extraordinary earnings call; the company is growing rapidly, expanding its operations and vertical markets whilst planning a major spin-off. The number of new products they are intending to produce and customers they intend to serve shows that their goal is now to be the largest integrated enrichment company in the world. They have transitioned from targeting niche markets with a need for specific enriched isotopes to building a company to target every buyer of enriched isotopes and the bigger the customer the better.

Many of these technologies, isotopes and acquired companies were unknown to me before the earnings call and it will take some time to investigate all of them. If the managers are correct in their views and plans then ASPI is probably a hundred bagger. I have begun investigating each element to make some judgment on the probability of success. They do have one thing going for them: they have proven they can build enrichment facilities at a fraction of the cost of other technologies and use these plants to produce commercial products.

Key Points from the original plan:

Shipped Commercial samples of Silicon-28, first batch for sale due before year end. Two customers signed up and a third contract arrived during the quarter. The plant has been adjusted to enrich Germanium and carbon.

Enriched ytterbium is now being shipped to multiple potential customers, expecting commercial shipments in 2026. Shipped lutetium-177 for the first time.

Regulation for 4 new enrichment plants gained and equipment ordered and expected to be on line in 2026 enriching gadolinium, nickel, zinc and ytterbium. Contract for barium 136 received.

Carbon-12 delayed due to feedstock issues (now resolved) but will ship to multiple customers in December.

Radio Chemical revenue increased 24% to $3.6 million (above guidance)

New Business:

Regeneren industrial gas company acquisition moving towards completion, working to bring the plant to nameplate capacity. The number of drilling rigs increased and ASPI engineers were running the sites. The move into drilling for gas was unexpected, it does provide feedstock but I am not sure why they needed to buy the company.

Two radiopharmacy acquisitions announced one in the US one in Europe, this is due to the success of the SouthAfrican operation and the very short half life of the active ingredient, it cannot be shipped globally and needs local manufacture.

QLE (subsidiary to be sold off) now targets cesium-137, a waste product from the nuclear industry, and intends to enrich it to a commercial product for the semiconductor market, now looking at strontium-90 and carbon 14. Signed an MOU with Fermi in the US to develop an 11 gigawatt data center complex with regulation to produce LEU, LEU plus and HALEU. I have not heard of LEU plus before this call and neither had the only analyst on the call.

A UK site is being prepared and Terra Power operations continue.

Lithium-7 is being targeted, no other producer in the west, and 80% of existing plants require it. (it is used for PH balancing not as a fuel) Lithium-6 being targeted for use in nuclear fusion.

Acquired a cayman island building company Skyline Builders and intend to sell off its assets as they are none core, the company will be used to source critical materials and since acquisition has raised $40 million. Acquired a 20% stake in a company with critical mineral assets in Asia. Acquiring Supercritical, a company with technology to extract uranium from seawater.

Announced new Kube technology for cleaning nuclear waste.

Such an amount of news, unprecedented in my experience.

**TSE:GRA**

New material remains a key sector but I continue to find it difficult to identify more opportunities. The companies have lots of promise but so far only NanoXplore shows any real commercial traction. There has been a slowdown in the use of its products in autos, but that reflects the slowdown in customer production rather than a problem with the product. **HSAI**

LiDAR is moving off my watchlist, no longer an emerging technology. It has been a good sector, Hesai delivered two big wins but has fallen back sharply following the last earnings call, a 50% pullback means it likely has value going forward. I intend to hold but am unlikely to add and would probably exit if the market continues to drop, the company has delivered scaled operations and enormous market share. It has become a value play rather than an emerging play and at the moment it seems good value. Once these companies achieve scale as manufacturers they are dependent on the overall economy and that is the risk for Hesai, they need a booming economy with people buying new cars and trucks.

**SPCE**

My super risky space bet continues to make progress, they must have started taking bookings again with $0.4 million in revenue booked last quarter. Progress towards next year’s flights continues but we should expect at least a one quarter slippage. They are forecasting fall 2026 for first flights, if they make H1 2027 I will be happy. They have enormous potential to be the first mover in a massive industry, space tourism and low gravity industrial experimentation.

The pullback is 37% and some possibility we have a low but I don’t expect it to hold. The high risk profile will stop me from adding in the short term but I will be hoping to add in the more medium term.

**EVTL**

I will be looking to add in the medium term but not yet. The pullback from October is minimal, but in EVTL's case, it is a continuation of a much longer correction. I will need confirmation of a significant change in the market’s opinion before adding.
