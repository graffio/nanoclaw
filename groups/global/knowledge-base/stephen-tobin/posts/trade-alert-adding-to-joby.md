# Trade Alert: Adding to Joby

*Increasing Position size*

EVTOL is one of the emerging technologies we are tracking. We already have investments in Ehang (EH) and Joby. Ehang has been very profitable for the last three years and remains the only certified aircraft. Ehang may face some competition in China as Volocpoter, the German company making a similar vehicle, has been bought out of administration by a Chinese automaker.

We are very interested in Vertical Aerospace (EVTL), competition in Europe has decreased as Airbus has announced it will not continue with its proposed eVTOL. I am still completing due diligence on EVTL but may buy it next month.

Joby was one of the first investments we made. We invested $250; however, our maximum investment has risen to $500 due to the amount of cash we have generated. The Joby holding is currently at $310, only 3% of the portfolio. I will increase it by placing a mid-market order on IBKR for 28 shares.

I still expect 2026 to be the big year for Joby. Hopefully, certification will arrive this year, and early next year, we will see multiple cities flying Joby aircraft.

Joby has made significant progress across certification, manufacturing, and commercialization of their eVTOL aircraft in the past year. They've delivered multiple aircraft, achieved milestones in testing, secured substantial funding (including $500 million from Toyota), and are planning to begin passenger service demonstrations in Dubai soon. Joby is also focused on scaled manufacturing and has a strong partnership with the U.S. Department of Defense. They are preparing for FAA certification testing and are optimistic about the coming year as they move towards delivering their service.

Joby signed a deal with Virgin Atlantic to run a service in the UK, countries like the UK with its dense population are well suited to JOBY. London and Birmingham are the two largest cities in the UK; they are only 100 miles apart, yet a journey by car takes over 3 hours. A transfer from Birmingham airport to Heathrow airport in London (Europe's largest international hub) would require two bus journeys, a train, and walks to get the connections. A Joby S4 could make the trip in around 25 mins, terminal to terminal.

**Financial Highlights (Q4 & Full Year 2024):**

-   **Cash & Short-Term Investments:** $933 million at year-end, expected to exceed $1.4 billion with the first Toyota investment tranche.
    
-   **Net Loss (Q4):** $246 million, including a $150 million operational loss and a $97 million other loss (primarily due to non-cash revaluation of derivative liabilities).
    
-   **Adjusted EBITDA Loss (Q4):** $119 million.
    
-   **Cash Use (2024):** $450 million, within guidance.
    

**Outlook for 2025:**

-   **Cash Use:** Expected to be between $500 million and $540 million.
    
-   **Focus:** Advancing testing and certification, preparing for passenger operations.
    
-   **Increased Spending:** Higher R&D, capital expenditures (CapEx) due to facility expansion in Marina, California, and Ohio.
    
-   **Key Milestones:** Delivery of first aircraft to Dubai, starting flight TIA testing, carrying first passengers.
