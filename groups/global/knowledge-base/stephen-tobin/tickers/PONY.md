## Company Overview

Pony AI is a Chinese autonomous driving company operating commercial robotaxi services in China's four tier-1 cities: Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Beijing, and Shanghai. The company also has a growing autonomous trucking division and international robotaxi operations expanding into Qatar, Singapore, Luxembourg, and South Korea.

**Strategy:** RoboTaxi First, China First, Tier 1 Cities First. Stephen views this focused approach positively as it offers the quickest route to scaled operations and keeps Pony away from direct competition with Waymo.

**Products and Operations:**

- Commercial robotaxi fleet scaling from 250 vehicles toward over 2,000 by 2026 and 3,000 by 2027
- Gen-7 robotaxi with substantially reduced bill of materials (70% reduction in 2025, further 20% guided for 2026)
- Autonomous trucking division with Gen-4 trucks manufactured by SANY and Dongfeng at thousand-unit scale
- Integration with ride-hailing apps including AMAP (Alibaba) and partnership with Uber
- Toyota partnership producing bZ4X robotaxi, 1,000 units planned for 2026

**Revenue trajectory:**

- FY2024: Revenue declined 62% due to expiration of R&D licensing agreements and end of JD payment milestones — Stephen viewed this as a sign of transition from R&D to commercialization
- Q1 2025: Taxi fares up 800% YoY, fleet expanding 400%, operational footprint up to 800 square miles (20x larger than San Francisco)
- Q3 2025: Revenue grew 72% YoY; Guangzhou operations achieved city-wide breakeven
- CNY 2026: Shenzhen YTD revenue passed entire 2025 total by Feb 16; averaging 26 paid trips per vehicle per day

**Key milestones:**

- Sunlight Mobility asset-light partnership for Gen-7 robotaxi rollout across nearly 200 cities
- Added to Nasdaq Golden Dragon Index (Jun 2025) and MSCI China Index (2026)
- Unit economics breakeven achieved in Guangzhou (Q3 2025) then Shenzhen (Mar 2026)
- 1,000 robotaxi deal for deployment in Shenzhen (Jun 2025)
- Founders voluntarily extended 23% shareholding lockup by over 550 days (May 2025)

## Competitive Position

Stephen's robotaxi market research identified a \$34 trillion market opportunity by 2030 (Ark Invest forecast). The market is highly concentrated due to extreme technical difficulty and capital requirements.

**vs Waymo (GOOG):** Clear frontrunner in the US with the largest fleet and most commercial rides. Pony's China-first strategy avoids direct competition. Stephen sees room for multiple winners given the enormous market size.

**vs Baidu:** The "Google of China" with ApolloGo service. Similar leadership in China as Waymo has in the US. More miles driven than Waymo but Pony is growing faster from a smaller base.

**vs WeRide (WRD):** Stephen treats PONY and WRD as a single correlated investment. Pony's focused robotaxi strategy offers "a more straightforward path to profitability" versus WeRide's broader approach (taxis, buses, road sweepers across global markets). The two stocks are highly correlated, with WRD typically at a premium. Stephen expects mean retracement when they diverge.

**vs Tesla:** Stephen is "very skeptical" — Tesla's camera-only FSD approach lacks the full sensor suite (LiDAR, radar, cameras, ultrasound) that every other company considers essential. NHTSA investigated Tesla FSD safety in reduced visibility conditions. Tesla had to seek help from Baidu for FSD v13 in China.

**China risk:** Anti-Chinese sentiment, potential US delisting threats, and tariff impacts create periodic sell-offs. Stephen notes these are "without merit" fundamentally but create volatility. His base case is "growth in China and the East, with the US and the West lagging."
