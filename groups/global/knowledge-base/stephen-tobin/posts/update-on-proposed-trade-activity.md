# Update on Proposed Trade Activity

*Having to Show Patience*

The markets remain volatile and challenging to predict; it is a time to be cautious, conserve capital, and wait. Some decisions will have to be made, and those are discussed below.

## Companies to Increase Position Size

QBTS

I think D-Wave (QBTS) may have the most disruptive technology in the world. It is capable of disrupting the supercomputing, AI, GPU, Blockchain, and electricity supply industries.

No other emerging technology has such potential. Multiple industries are adopting the D-Wave annealer. Ford has begun using it to schedule a paint shop in Turkey, NTT docomo is using it to improve capacity on cellular networks in Japan. The number of industries that will be optimized by the D-Wave annealer grows almost daily. Last week, Boeing announced they are looking at D-Wave to improve its manufacturing process, reliability, and throughput. Boeing publicly stated that no other technology can optimize their one million parts per aircraft manufacturing process.

D-Wave has demonstrated that they can run AI and Blockchain technology at scale using 1,000 times less energy than the equivalent GPU technology.

There is a chance they will be the only Quantum Computing company to become commercial! The competing Quantum Gate computing technology of all other Quantum computers remains error-prone and small-scale, plus they are at risk of dequantization.

Dequantization: finding classical algorithms that can solve problems previously thought to be accessible only by quantum computers is gathering more attention in the computing world. This post from the excellent [QuantaMagazine covers recent cases](https://www.quantamagazine.org/what-is-the-true-promise-of-quantum-computing-20250403/).

D-Wave is not threatened by Dequantization. Its benefit is that it can solve these problems faster using less electricity. It is not an algorithm-based technology, so new algorithms will not be able to match it. Two recent papers suggested they could do classically what D-Wave has suggested is its proof of quantum supremacy. Both are bogus and revolve around the way D-Wave simulated spin glass lattices. D-Wave used supercomputers to simulate small cases to prove they got the correct answer for those simple cases. D-Wave then modeled much bigger structures beyond what could be done on the supercomputer.

One of the two articles said that because they got the same answer as the supercomputer for the small structures, they were not actually quantum, and the second paper said that because the supercomputer could solve the small case, it could solve the rest. Both papers are completely bogus; to prove they can solve large lattice problems, the authors need to do exactly that: solve a large lattice problem. After all, that is what D-Wave did.

## Companies to re-enter

**SES:** An excellent set of Q1 figures has SES back in my buying sights. The price dropped drastically after I last bought them, and we exited for a 41% loss at $0.73. Today, the price is at $0.52, so we avoided a lot of additional loss.

The original thesis behind the investment looks strong. The new electrolyte and the 2170 battery should develop significant sales, and as they are built in Asia for sale in Asia, they could avoid the worst excesses of the Trump war.

If the stock drops below $0.50 again, I will add 300 shares to the demonstration account, but I will review the decision before I act.

**Hesai**: We did very well out of Hesai buying at $5.48 and selling at $20.71 for a 278% return. The share price has fallen to $12.63 today due to a recent short seller report and ongoing tariff issues. The short report was utterly bogus, but once again, the authors have made a fortune. Short reports on Chinese companies follow a common thread: The companies are spies of the Chinese government, their customers are not real, their products don’t work, and they stole the technology from the US. We have made money many times, including our biggest ever win, buying Chinese companies after a bogus short report.

We will be able to buy cheaply again, getting a good company that is in even better shape than when we last bought it for a knockdown price. I haven't decided when or how much yet, but I will be buying at some point.

## New Companies to Buy

I have been reviewing the robotaxi market and just sent an article to SeekingAlpha about WeRide. The market is poised for exponential growth, but I have not identified the right target yet. I am following WeRide, Pony, and Baidu. All three have the potential for extraordinary growth, and I will begin acquiring shares soon.

I am still very keen to get more exposure to China. Although I am not ready to buy just yet, Xiaomi could be an excellent candidate; it makes excellent phones and other devices, and has a fast-growing electric car division. Xiaomi shares appreciated hugely, going from $7 in February 2024 to $40 last month. Xiaomi would benefit from increased phone sales if Apple loses ground in China (very likely at present) as the premium domestic supplier. My wife had a Xiaomi smart watch when we lived in Southeast Asia, and she still maintains it was better than an Apple watch.

Last week, I was certain I would buy Xiaomi this week, but caution is called for; we must preserve the capital and act at the right time.

Should I decide to buy it, I will send the complete analysis I did last week.

## Final Words

To make money in stocks, you must have the vision to see them, the courage to buy them, and the patience to act at the right time.

At the moment, we are waiting for the right time.

The portfolio continues to fall, dropping 8% so far in April, following losses of 8% and 4% in February and March. Cash now accounts for 52% of the portfolio. It is a poor few months, but we are outperforming the benchmarks by a vast amount and hopefully will continue to do so.
