# First Alert of 2026: Bottom fishing 2025

*An emerging tech AI company with few friends*

At the end of every year, I perform two tasks: Firstly, I look at companies that have performed particularly badly; often, the worst performers one year are the best the next. Secondly, I review all of the emerging technology sectors I am tracking - some are new areas I want to get into. Most are existing areas where I review which companies will become targets.

In this case, a company came up in the first search that is in one of the new areas I want to get into.

I have decided to invest, the share price has pulled back more than 50%, short interest has reached high levels, and numerous quant strategies have moved to sell ratings.

All of this has happened while this AI technology stock has continued to perform well, lift its revenue guidance, and forecast break-even operations next year.

With industry-leading technology, growing adoption of its **Agentic AI solutions**, a large customer base, and the singular benefit of being non-aligned to the big tech companies, I think this investment has the chance to double in value in the first half of 2026.

Agentic AI is one of the key sectors I believe will make progress in 2026, and this will be my first investment in this technology.

Agentic AI will be the next evolution of AI, moving beyond ChatGPT and other LLMs to systems that, rather than responding to prompts, can set goals, plan complex steps, and use external tools without human intervention.

The big deal about Agentic AI is that it acts; it doesn’t just respond to a prompt and give relevant information. I think we will hear more about Agentic AI in 2026 than we did about ChatGPT in 2025.

## **Core Products and Technology**

SoundHound’s core technology stack is built around proprietary Speech-to-Meaning, which unifies speech recognition and language understanding in real time. The technology is designed to support more complex conversations with low latency. The company has developed its Human Assisted Understanding (HAU), a patented process that combines AI with human understanding in real-time, enhancing the accuracy and fluidity of customer interactions.

SoundHound’s product portfolio spans multiple high-value verticals with its voice and conversational AI technology at the foundation:

-   **Restaurant Solutions**: The company offers Smart Ordering, Dynamic Drive-Thru, and Smart Answering solutions that are deployed at over 15,000 restaurant locations across major brands, including Chipotle, MOD Pizza, and Five Guys.
    
-   **Enterprise Solutions**: The Amelia platform (v7.3) powers AI agents that listen, reason, and act, incorporating speech-to-meaning while improving latency through its Agentic+ framework.
    
-   **Automotive Solutions**: SoundHound provides voice assistants for automotive OEMs, with Jeep vehicles across Europe now featuring SoundHound’s flagship Chat AI voice technology.
    
-   **Voice Commerce**: The company has launched voice-commerce capabilities that enable restaurant reservations, parking payments, and voice ordering, with global OEM rollout expected in 2026.
    
-   **Robotics:** a large win with a Chinese robot maker expands the footprint of operations and validates the power of the non-aligned business model
    

The company’s technology strategy combines deterministic flows with machine-learning models to improve reliability and reduce inconsistency that can occur with LLM-only systems in production environments.

## **Acquisition Strategy and Integration**

SOUN has developed a tuck-in acquisition strategy that has driven growth in both its customer base and its tech stack. Management has demonstrated its ability to integrate these acquisitions and reduce costs, which should normalize margins to the previously reported 70%. They fell below 60% early in 2025.

### **2024 Acquisitions**

SoundHound executed three strategic acquisitions in 2024 that significantly expanded its market reach:

-   **SYNQ3 (December 2023)**: A $25 million acquisition of this conversational AI-ordering company made SoundHound the largest provider of voice AI for restaurants in the US, extending its reach to over 10,000 signed locations and integrating 20+ national and multinational brands.
    
-   **Allset (June 2024)**: Acquisition of this online ordering platform connected restaurants to local customers, bringing restaurant relationships, engineering skills, and marketplace know-how for an improved voice commerce ecosystem.
    
-   **Amelia (August 2024)**: An $80 million acquisition of this leading enterprise AI software company brought over $45 million in recurring AI software revenue, 200+ new brands, and expanded SoundHound’s scale and reach in conversational AI across new verticals and hundreds of enterprises.
    

### **2025 Acquisition**

-   **Interactions (September 2025)**: A $60 million cash acquisition of this pioneer in AI for customer service and workflow orchestration, with potential for additional payments based on revenue milestones. ## **Integration Progress**

SoundHound’s integration strategy follows a repeatable playbook that has shown early success:

-   The company is migrating Amelia platform clients from third-party legacy vendors to its Polaris Multimodal Multilingual Foundation model, reducing error rates by up to 3x while improving accuracy, speed, and cost.
    
-   SoundHound can migrate away from third-party voice AI providers, helping increase the profitability of acquired businesses like Interactions.
    
-   15 of Amelia’s 150 enterprise customers have begun migrating to version 7.0, with adoption expected to accelerate with v7.3.
    
-   The company expects to realize $20 million in annualized cost savings in 2026 from expense synergies from the Interactions integration.
    
-   Gross margin has been impacted by the acquisitions (down from 77.2% to 58.5% year-over-year), but I expect it to move back toward historical levels as integration progresses.

SoundHound is demonstrating strong execution with accelerating revenue growth and improving operational metrics:

-   The company raised **full-year 2025 revenue guidance to $165-$180 million** , representing **89-110% year-over-year growth**.
    
-   The company expects to be EBITDA positive at the high end of guidance
    
-   SoundHound ended Q3 2025 with **$269 million in cash and no debt**, providing ample liquidity to fund growth initiatives.
    
-   Model forecasts **FY25E revenue of $168 million**, **FY26E revenue of $260 million**, and **FY27E revenue of $320 million**, with adjusted EBITDA improving from **\-$56.0 million** in FY25E to **\-$0.7 million** in FY26E and to **+$16 million** in FY27E.
    
-   Management guidance was for break-even profitability in 2026 and continued high growth.
    

The company’s operating leverage trajectory is driven by three factors: **revenue scale and improving cloud efficiency**, **acquisition synergies**, and a **moderation in opex growth** as go-to-market investments begin to convert at higher productivity.

## The Technology

SOUN positions itself as offering **superior speed and accuracy** compared to major competitors such as Google Assistant and Amazon Alexa. However, independent expert assessments paint a more nuanced picture of its competitive position in the voice AI market.

SoundHound consistently asserts **best-in-class performance** across multiple official communications:

-   The company claims its proprietary **Speech-to-Meaning** technology unifies speech recognition and language understanding in real time, specifically engineered for **low-latency performance** critical in environments like drive-thru ordering and in-vehicle use.
    
-   Investor presentations repeatedly characterize SoundHound’s technology as **“surpassing competitors in speed, accuracy, and complex language understanding”** across 25 languages on both cloud and hardware-embedded devices.
    
-   SEC filings state that SoundHound’s voice AI **“delivers best-in-class speed and accuracy in numerous languages”** to product creators across automotive, TV, IoT, and customer service industries.
    
-   SoundHound’s **“production-first” approach** emphasizes **low latency, high accuracy, and deterministic reliability** while selectively incorporating generative models where they improve outcomes.
    

## **Independent Expert Perspectives**

Expert insights present counterpoints to SoundHound’s performance claims. A former SoundHound software engineer reported that during their tenure, the company’s technology “was somewhat lagging behind possible competitors such as Microsoft, Amazon, and Apple” and noted that in blind tests conducted by Deutsche Telekom, SoundHound consistently came in second place to Amazon in raw performance metrics but comprehensively beat Google and Microsoft. Another expert, a former competitor, observed that while SoundHound has strength in vertical-industry focus, smaller competitors such as ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and LiveKit offer better collaboration tools and SDKs at lower prices, making them more attractive for developer-focused implementations. Furthermore, a former manager acknowledged SoundHound’s strong customer reception from automotive brands like Hyundai and Mercedes but noted significant challenges competing with Google in automotive, where “if Google is already the OS... it was much less likely that customers wouldn’t just take the default Google Voice technologies.”

Independent validation supports SoundHound’s performance claims, notably through its designation as a **Leader in the IDC MarketScape for Worldwide General-Purpose Conversational AI Platforms 2025** , where the assessment highlighted strong customer satisfaction with the quality of SoundHound’s voice AI, including AI-generated voice responses. The IDC evaluation specifically praised SoundHound’s **“accurate intent recognition, even for complex requests, combined with superior voice channel capabilities”** and noted its ability to handle complex, multi-intent queries. Recent earnings call data indicate, compared to incumbent solutions, SoundHound’s early adopter customers are achieving **up to a 10x improvement in containment rates,** a **25% higher end-user Net Promoter Score, and a 15% higher customer satisfaction**, all while requiring **up to 35% less** deployment effort.

## Conclusion

Agentic AI is an emerging technology I plan to leverage for gains in 2026. SOUN will be my first investment in the commercialization of this end-to-end technology.

SOUN has commercial validation, a key requirement for my strategy. It is growing quickly, reporting triple-digit revenue growth, and is expanding into more verticals. The big R&D spend has already been made and the products are fully developed with a proven ability.

SOUN will provide voice-to-meaning-to-action technology in its core markets. It has been adding 1,000 restaurants a quarter to its customer base. It continues to make progress in Auto, with double-digit deals in the Indian market, in addition to its existing positions in Europe and the rollout of its product across all Jeep vehicles in 2026. SOUN has agreed to provide its products to a Chinese IoT manufacturer and a robot manufacturer. They operate with 7 of the 10 largest global financial institutions, with 3 increasing the products they buy and 2 renewing multi-year contracts in 2025.

2026 could be the year SOUN breaks into new markets with its Agentic AI solution, entering the health, insurance, and communications sectors.

Currently, SOUN products focus on call centers and customer service. I think 2026 will be the year the technology breaks out into broader markets.

A heavy amount of short interest combined with a sharp pullback offers the chance of an immediate re rating and with profitability now on the near term horizon I think that will happen sooner than later.
