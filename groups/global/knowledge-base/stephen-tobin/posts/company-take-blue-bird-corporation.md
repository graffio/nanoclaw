# Company Take: Blue Bird Corporation

*Planning an exit*

We first bought BLBD in October 2023 @$18.23 and added in November 2024 @$41.15. The current price is $37.24 and it is time to make a plan about what to do with the position.

BLBD manufactures school buses in America. They were a turnaround story when we first bought them: unprofitable with near-stagnant sales. However, it was clear that their leadership position in alternative fuel buses was going to drive significant revenue growth and return the company to profits.

BLBD is still a leader; they have the lion's share of the electric fuel bus market, market leadership in the gasoline-powered market, and an exclusive position in the propane-powered market.

They have just delivered an outstanding earnings report with record profit margins, increased EBITDA, and on-target revenue, helped by their ability to raise prices on their diesel and gasoline-powered vehicles. Parts income was also a record figure and appears to be a growing part of the business.

Under normal circumstances, I would increase my price target and look to add to the position. The increased profitability is a huge positive, as they are now running at all-time high EBITDA margin levels.

However, the outlook for BLBD is being hit hard by changes introduced by the new administration. A large part of the positive story for BLBD is sales from the IRA-funded clean bus initiative that was putting $5 billion into providing new school busses in the US; BLBD was doing very well out of that initiative, increasing sales and hiring new staff to build its BEV. The funding payouts have been temporarily halted. It is not an immediate concern as they have 1,000 electric bus orders on backorder (a record representing 6 months of production). However, I am concerned about the impact if the funding does not restart.

Other administration issues include the tariffs on Canada and Mexico, which forced BLBD to lower the bottom end of its cash generation guidance for fiscal 2025 despite holding firm with revenue guidance.

Mr Trump has also implied he will disband the Department of Education, which would inevitably plunge education in the US into a period of turmoil. This would likely lead to a focus on other things rather than on new buses. There must also be a chance that total funding for education will be cut, and schools will decide to keep their aging diesel-powered vehicles.

Conclusion

Despite producing excellent results and maintaining guidance for 2025, increased risk must be associated with this position. Reluctantly, I have decided to apply a stop loss and will close the position at $33.40 should the price fall back to that level. The wave chart I am using is shown below. This could mark a turn higher, hence my decision to hold the positions.
