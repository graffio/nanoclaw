# Trade Alert: ARIS Decision

*Takeover leaves options*

I have two open positions in Aris Water (ARIS)

After markets closed yesterday, Western Midstream Partners (WES) announced that it had agreed to acquire ARIS for $1.5 billion.

The agreed takeover price is significantly below the fair value I had calculated for ARIS and follows a very upbeat earnings report.

Shareholders in ARIS have three options:

1.  Sell shares in the market
    
2.  Exchange each Aris share for 0.65 WES shares
    
3.  Accept the $25 per share in cash offered by WES
    

The deal is dependent on ARIS shareholder approval. Still, the chance of them not approving is close to zero due to the ownership structure of ARIS and the concentration of its voting power. Retail shareholders hold only 0.03% of this stock; readers of this newsletter could well be the only non-institutional owners.

The deal is most likely being done at the request of the major oil company customers (and major voting rights holders of Aris), who will benefit from a significantly reduced cost structure. I am sure this is already a done deal.

All shareholders need to make their own decision, but **I will be selling my shares in the market today, targeting $24.80 and higher than $24.70.**

I am not a long-term investor, and my active management style requires the recycling of capital, so the money generated from this deal will fund a purchase of another emerging technology company.

Several of our companies have reported this week with very mixed results. Although I am an active manager, I do not act hastily and will assess the results thoroughly before acting. It does look like we will be able to add to two existing holdings and perhaps need to exit one.

I still have three new companies I hope to buy this month, one is a definite buy, and I am writing up the trade alert at the moment, the others are waiting for this quarter’s earnings.

The only trade made so far this month is showing an 8% return. The Portfolio remains in a solid position with a large cash balance and consistent profits.
