# Trade Alert: Building Positions in Drone Technology

*Hyper growth is forecast for this sector*

The total global drone market is forecast to grow from $40 billion last year to $70 billion this year and top $200 billion by 2030.

The counter-drone industry is much smaller but is forecast to grow much faster, from $2 billion last year to $6 billion this year and $30 billion by 2030.

There are fewer players in the counter-drone market, and the technical barriers to entry are much greater. You could build a drone in your garage; you can buy the bits on eBay, but try ordering the bits to build a counter-drone missile, and you will quickly see the moat around this business sector.

The Drone market could easily become a low-price commodity market with limited profit potential, but the counter-drone market is likely to see increasing margins and more long-term SaaS contracts.

Today, I will increase my exposure to counter drones. I did really well in this sector in 2025, with some large profits booked

## The December Update

DroneShields’ [December trading update](https://cdn-api.markitdigital.com/apiman-gateway/ASX/asx-research/1.0/file/2924-03048971-2A1649704&v=undefined?utm_source=Investors&utm_campaign=d359799c67-EMAIL_CAMPAIGN_11_14_2019_9_39_COPY_01&utm_medium=email&utm_term=0_5a1ea83533-d359799c67-%5BLIST_EMAIL_ID%5D) was positive in every respect. (it is unaudited) They reported revenue of $217 million, beating my October 2025 forecast by 9% and my March forecast by 55%.

In Q4 2025, revenue grew by 94% YoY, and possibly even more importantly, high-margin SaaS revenue jumped an astonishing 475% to $4.6 million, with **another $18 million committed for 2026**.

Cash flow came in at +$7.7 million v -$9 million the year before and management announced they expect to be consistently cash flow positive moving forward.

Further clarity was given on the pipeline. It contains more than 300 deals. 14 of those deals are over $35 million, 35 are over $10 million, and the largest is unchanged at $800 million.

The pipeline has 14% in the US, 40% in the EU, 5% in the UK and Australia, and 21% in Asia (not China).

The only potential negative was the decrease in the total pipeline from $2.5 billion to $2.1 billion. That drop is in Australian dollars, so part of it reflects changes in exchange rates and perhaps the removal of low-probability cases.

The committed revenue for 2026 stands out almost as much as the extraordinary growth in 2025. More committed revenue before the start of 2026 than was generated in the whole of 2024.

The Drone threat and geopolitical instability generally provide a strong tailwind for Droneshield. Their hardware continues to sell well and benefits from quarterly software updates that add new drone signatures as they are discovered. The number of units in the field and their proven wartime success increases the salability of their products, creating a flywheel that smaller companies cannot match.

Of importance is the relationship with the major defence primes, as the CEO discussed in our interview, the big defence primes are customers, not competitors, integrating Droneshields technology into their large-scale systems.

DroneShield is integrating multiple new sensor technologies into its product and significantly expanding its manufacturing footprint in 2026. Manufacturing floorspace is increasing by 650% to 3,000 sqm in Sydney; the European manufacturing plant will come online in Q1 2026, and the US plant is due to come online in Q3.

Of course, this does mean higher costs; leases for the Aussie expansion alone will add $5 million a year to the cost base, and that is before salaries and other costs are included.

## Conclusion

DroneShield has been a wild ride but its business performance is clear, fast revenue growth, earlier than expected profitability and 2026 revenue commitments a record figure more than double what I had expected.

2026 is likely to be an inflection year for counter-drone technology. DroneShield seems to be well placed to make the most of the opportunity, it has industry-leading technology, proven wartime experience, and large repeat customers who are signing up for the SaaS offering in record volumes.
