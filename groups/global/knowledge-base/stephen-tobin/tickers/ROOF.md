## Company Overview

Northstar Clean Technologies is a Canadian company that recycles asphalt shingles into reusable liquid asphalt and other materials. The business is low-tech but Stephen sees significant scale potential as the company rolls out multiple processing sites.

**Business model:** Recycling asphalt shingles into liquid asphalt for sale to road construction companies. Revenue from selling recycled asphalt and related byproducts.

**First site (Canada):** Operating but experiencing technical problems preventing nameplate capacity. First commercial sale of liquid asphalt to Colas completed in December 2025 (ahead of January guidance). Five-year supply deal in place.

**US expansion:** Original US site fell through. Alternative site selection expected by end of 2025. Next 3 US sites not expected to open until after they exit 2026. US site approvals proving difficult.

**Funding model:** First site mainly funded by grants. CEO indicated he could replicate grant-funded model for next four sites. Extended convertible debenture maturity from Feb 2026 to Feb 2027. Stephen expects some dilution but less than initially feared. Announced US \$10M non-binding term sheet (March 2026).

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis:** Recycling is a key emerging sector for Stephen's portfolio. ROOF is a micro-cap asphalt recycling play with enormous scale potential. "Really expect this to be a ten bagger in 2027." Stephen classified it as a "slow burn with multiple financing rounds" — small initial position with plans to add over time.

**Key milestone:** First commercial sale of liquid asphalt to Colas in December 2025. Stephen: "The first revenue generating sale of recycled material is a good step forward and comes in advance of management guidance who had indicated January."

**Post-earnings view (Nov 2025):** "Liked the earnings call, obviously rather low tech but the message was clear. Moved the stock to my pre-buy list and am waiting for a suitable entry point." Expected first site to prove the business model before scaling.

**Current view (Mar 2026):** "I still like the look of Roof, I keep wanting to add but will wait for the update of course that runs the risk of missing a move." Oil crisis assessment raised concerns: "ROOF is still developing the first sites and will need ongoing capital. They may be just too risky in this environment." Short-term risk: High, Medium-term risk: Medium.
