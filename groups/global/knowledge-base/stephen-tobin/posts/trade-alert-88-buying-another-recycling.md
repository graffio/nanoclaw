## The Background

The recycling industry is a target area, a sector ripe for disruption that needs a mix of different technologies to cope with the multitude of materials currently going to landfill or being dumped into the environment. It is an old saying in the UK that “where there’s muck there’s brass,” which means that where rubbish is being dumped, there is cash to be made.

I have covered several recycling technology companies. They all have very similar business models. In every case, they take unwanted material destined for landfill and transform it into a new, valuable product.

Each material needs a different technology to recycle it, some are easy to deal with for instance food waste- it can easily be composted and sold to gardeners. Many are extremely difficult to recycle and require cutting-edge technology and engineering excellence to build a viable site.

Recyclers have demand on both sides. They get push demand due to regulation and tipping fees, corporations are keen to send as much of their waste as possible to recycling. Pull-through demand comes from manufacturers seeking to demonstrate their environmental responsibility.

Recyclers all face the same challenges: first, proving their technology works; then building a large-scale site; and finally moving to commercial operations. When they move to commercial operations, they need two things: feedstock and customers. Customers appear to be the easy bit: as long as their output is of good quality and they can make positive margins at market price, a ready market awaits. But feedstock is another deal entirely, where they get the feedstock from is a big problem and there is no simple solution.

Large multi-site corporations face a similar, but opposite, problem: a retail operation with hundreds of sites produces waste nationwide. Each site will make almost every kind of waste, cardboard, plastics, metal, batteries, food… the list is endless. These large companies are under pressure to reduce their landfill use, it is an expensive and highly regulated way to dispose of waste and will never generate income. Recycling would be preferred by most however the complexity of getting each waste stream to a suitable recycler from every store is an administrative task so daunting that most do not do it and dump all the waste.

This tiny company just might have the technology to bridge this problem.

## Trade Alert #88: Buying Quest Resource Holdings (NASDAQ:QRHC)

**Key Takeaway:** With a market cap of under $50 million, this is another true micro cap, like ROOF we cannot expect immediate results, and the investment is full of risk. Large drawdown is possible, perhaps even probable, and I will allow it to run below my usual levels.

Given the risk profile, I will take a Half Size position and look to grow my holdings in 2026 when milestones are hit. I will target a position of $250, the same as in ROOF. I expect to buy another microcap next week and will follow the same plan.

## Initial Points

Quest has undergone a **fundamental operational transformation** during 2023-2025, evolving from a struggling waste broker into a **technology-enabled, nationally-scaled managed-waste platform** positioned for sustainable profitability. The company’s strategic pivot, combined with record customer acquisition and comprehensive cost restructuring, has created a foundation for sustained growth despite near-term margin pressures.

QRHC achieved a **commercialization inflection point in 2024**, with eight new customer wins expected to generate at least seven figures each annually. The company’s **“land-and-expand” strategy** has proven effective, with five expansion agreements secured with their largest existing clients.

**Strategic pivot toward share-of-wallet expansion** reflects market realities, with wallet share opportunities representing more than 50% of QRHC’s pipeline and carrying faster sales cycles with higher initial margins. The company maintains comprehensive national coverage across all U.S. zip codes plus Canada and Puerto Rico, serving 90,000 client locations.

**Management changes** brought experienced leadership: Perry Moss was elevated to CEO in March 2025 with 30+ years of industry experience, and Nick Ober joined as SVP Operations with 17 years of freight and waste management experience.

The **debt refinancing in December 2024** was transformative, reducing the blended interest rate by 150 bps, decreasing annual interest expense by $1.0M, and increasing PNC revolver capacity to $45M.

## Business Model

Quest operates an **asset-light, end-to-end waste, recycling and sustainability platform** that is deliberately positioned between the vertically-integrated landfill majors and thousands of single-stream local haulers.

Quest uses a network of **3,500 vendors, 25 k trucks and 1,000 recycling facilities** , giving it on-demand capacity without owning hard assets. Competitive bids across this network compress disposal costs, while rising vendor “pull-through” lowers service disruptions per management commentary. Strength of these relationships is core IP.

Using their network Quest handles **100+ solid and liquid waste streams** – from cardboard, metals and used oil to hazardous and universal waste – via tailored programs that span collection, transport, treatment, resale and ESG reporting.

Quest offers its large customers seven bundled “national solutions” that cover every zip code in the U.S. plus Canada, enabling one contract, one invoice and 24/7 support for Fortune-1000 customers with multi-site, complex footprints.

Quest designs a bespoke system for every large client using the collection and recycling facilities on offer in a geographically acceptable area. It can guarantee that its customers recycle as much as possible, minimizing environmental impact.

**Proprietary Technology & Data Analytics**

A key offering is a new cloud service that allows each customer to see exactly how much waste from each of their sites has been recycled. **Cloud ERP & Portal (fully migrated 2019)** integrates order-to-cash, vendor dispatch, compliance documentation and client dashboards; the interface is **zero-touch** , meeting EPA data standards and producing auditable ESG metrics used in quarterly business reviews.

Management is exploring a **subscription model for data access**, the data will be useful for recycling companies as they choose where to build their next site and for waste haulers to make informed decisions about what types of trucks and facilities will generate the most income.

R&D roadmap targets AI/IoT to automate waste-stream identification and pricing, boosting scalability while minimizing capex.

## Market and Competition

Massive, under-digitized waste market, accelerating ESG compliance needs, and increasing landfill scarcity collectively expand its addressable market and harden its moat.

-   Total Addressable Market (TAM): North-American waste & recycling spend of **≈US$200 bn** in 2019, growing with GDP plus regulatory tailwinds.
    
-   Segmentation: ~15 % is controlled by vertically-integrated haulers/landfill operators; **≈85 % (≈US$170 bn)** remains highly fragmented across 18,000 local vendors.
    
-   Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): Quest targets asset-light brokerage and multi-stream waste optimization for national, high-value commercial accounts—segments largely underserved by majors with only ~30 % business overlap.
    

The market has four growth drivers

1.  **ESG & Regulatory Reporting** – Mandatory disclosure regimes and municipal diversion mandates heighten demand for auditable waste data
    
2.  **Rising Landfill Tipping Fees** – Inflationary disposal costs tilt economics toward recycling and brokerage solutions that can arbitrage vendor capacity.
    
3.  **Technological Modernization** – Cloud platforms/IoT enable real-time optimization and transparency, favoring tech-enabled aggregators like Quest.
    
4.  **Continued Market Fragmentation** – New specialty processes (e-waste, organics) proliferate vendor base, enlarging consolidation runway.
    

Quest faces competition from two customer types. The Integrated Majors who operate nationally but are tied to their existing landfill sites and a vast array of local suppliers who make up the majority of this fragmented industry.

## Strategy

Quest’s roadmap is anchored in a “land-and-expand” model underpinned by technology, vertical diversification, and a repeatable tuck-in M&A program. Management asserts that these levers are now better coordinated under its Operational Excellence initiative adopted in early-2025, aimed at standardising processes and improving data visibility across sales, vendor and service delivery teams.

**Organic Growth Levers**

-   Land: the pipeline of national and multi-site prospects continues to grow, but decision cycles lengthened in 2025 amid macro uncertainty. Even so, new accounts added over the past 18 months contributed **$24 mm incremental revenue YTD-25** and are tracking to plan. Initial margins with new customers are low and take several quarters to reach the required level.
    
-   Expand (cross-sell): Quest is systematically broadening waste streams and geography within existing Fortune 1000 customers. Management disclosed that wallet-share opportunities represent **\>50 % of its active funnel and typically carry faster cycles and higher starting margins**.
    

## Q3 Notes

QRHC’s 3Q25 results surpassed expectations across key metrics: revenue, gross margin, and Adjusted EBITDA.

**Operational Excellence:** Initiatives launched earlier in the year are delivering concrete results, evidenced by sequential improvement through cost reductions and stronger cash generation. Traction is also building in areas such as gaining improved visibility into customer needs.

**Organic Growth Focus:** Despite a challenging macroeconomic environment (particularly in the industrial segment) and a slower year-over-year pace of new client acquisition, the company is making internal progress. Efforts are focused on redefining the sales process to boost “share-of-wallet” opportunities by expanding the number of waste streams managed per client and introducing new value-added services for existing customers. These actions are anticipated to sustain ongoing organic growth.

A key strategic priority for the company is to diversify its customer base to reduce reliance on the industrial segment. Management is actively focused on expanding its market presence in the retail, hospitality, and grocery verticals, where significant growth and deeper market penetration are anticipated.

Despite the headwinds, this quarter showed sequential improvement in financial results. Early results from the Operational Excellence initiatives are evident, particularly in better cost control, enhanced visibility into customer needs, and initial benefits from efforts to boost vendor and sales productivity.

Management acknowledges that the turnaround is ongoing, with further opportunities to increase efficiency, expand margins, and strengthen organizational execution. While the macro environment, especially in industrial markets, remains a difficulty, Quest is building a more robust operational foundation.

**Management Guidance and Outlook**

Management’s expectations for gross profit are for it to remain flat or slightly decrease.

Consistent operating cash flow generation is expected as the business continues its overall improvement trajectory. A key priority is the aggressive reduction of debt.

**Key Strategic Focuses:**

The main priorities moving forward are:

1.  **Business Efficiencies:** Streamlining operations.
    
2.  **Driving Margins:** Enhancing profitability.
    
3.  **Debt Reduction:** Lowering financial leverage.
    
4.  **Generating Growth:** Expanding the business.
    

**Margin and Customer Strategy:**

Management acknowledged that margin pressure is still expected as the “land and expand” strategy is executed. Furthermore, volumes from the company’s largest industrial customers are anticipated to remain challenging. These macroeconomic issues are expected to be partially offset by optimization of service levels, increasing the ‘share of wallet’ with existing customers, and continuous operational improvements across the enterprise.

## QRHC 3Q25 Highlights and Turnaround Progress

QRHC demonstrated significant progress in its turnaround during the quarter:

-   **Cash Flow and Debt Reduction:** The company generated $5.7M in operating cash flow by improving its cash cycle. Debt was reduced by $11.2M year-to-date, marking a 14% year-over-year reduction.
    
-   **Gross Profit:** Gross profit dollars of $11.5M exceeded expectations, reflecting the positive impact of growth initiatives gaining traction.
    
-   **Working Capital Management:** Days Sales Outstanding (DSOs) continued their favorable decline, landing in the lower 70s for the quarter. Management is optimizing its payment and collection processes with vendors, aiming to enhance the order-to-cash cycle and overall working capital. The vast majority of vendors are now being paid on time, and invoicing time has been shortened, with further optimization of cash collections planned.
    

## Growth and Client Momentum

**New Client Contribution:** New clients added over the past 18 months have already generated over $24M in incremental year-over-year revenue. The onboarding and progression of these clients are advancing as planned and are already positively impacting financial results.

**Margin Trajectory:** While margins with new clients are initially lower and require time to scale, they have shown consecutive gains for several quarters.

**Sales Pipeline and Market Interest:** The sales pipeline is described as “active and growing.” A major positive sign is that prospective businesses are proactively reaching out to Quest, seeking new business and looking to expand their relationships with the company.

**New Business Wins:**

QRHC secured a new contract with a company in the food products end market, which includes executing “share-of-wallet” initiatives expected to drive incremental organic growth. The company is expanding its relationship with an existing client (won in 2024 in the food segment) by adding cardboard and organic food waste services. A “significant” number of new stores were added from another existing client.

In the previous quarter, QRHC announced two significant wins: one with a major retailer and another with a large, full-service restaurant chain.

## Following The Release

I have increased my revenue and Adjusted EBITDA estimates for 2025 to $255 million and $10 million, respectively, up from previous estimates of $240 M and $8 M. For 2026, I forecast $270 in revenue and $12 in Adjusted EBITDA.

**Model Tailwinds:**

**Process improvements**: Established metrics and KPIs reducing service disruptions

**Portfolio optimization**: Exiting low-profit RWS mall business

**Headcount optimization**: 15% reduction expected to save $3M annually

### **Explicit Breakeven Mathematics and FCF Timing**

**EBITDA Breakeven Calculation:**

-   **SG&A run-rate**: $38-40M annually at steady-state
    
-   **Target gross margin**: 17.0-17.5% normalized range
    
-   **Required revenue for breakeven**: $217-235M (calculation: $38M ÷ 17.5% ≈ $217M; $40M ÷ 17.0% ≈ $235M)
    
-   **Current 3Q25 annualized revenue**: ~$253M, indicating **near-breakeven positioning**
    

**Free Cash Flow Breakeven:**

-   **Interest expense**: Reduced by $1.0M annually post-refinancing
    
-   **Working capital normalization**: 9-day DSO improvement driving cash conversion
    
-   **FCF positive timing**: Expected **2H26** under base case scenario
    

**Operational Sensitivity Analysis:**

-   **Gross margin impact**: Each 100 bps improvement = $2.5-3.0M additional EBITDA
    
-   **SG&A leverage**: Each $1M SG&A reduction flows directly to EBITDA
    
-   **Industrial recovery**: Volume normalization could add $8-12M incremental revenue.  
    

## Conclusion

Quest is a high-risk play; transitioning from a failing waste brokerage into a national waste management company has transformed its potential. Its services will be key to the US recycling industry and is the only company currently pursuing this strategy.

It could all fail, but I think the most likely outcomes are that it gets bought by one of the majors or that it builds a strong, profitable, recurring-revenue service business.

I am going to buy today and look to add when we get positive quarterly updates but I am going to limit my exposure.
