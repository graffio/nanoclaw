## Company Overview

Tempus AI is a technology company advancing precision medicine through AI, operating one of the world's largest healthcare datasets — over 400 petabytes of multimodal clinical data, 45+ million patient records, 8+ million imaging records, and 4+ million sequenced genomic samples. The company is connected to more than two-thirds of all US medical providers, with 7,000 oncologists regularly ordering tests.

**Products / Segments:**

- Genomics (Diagnostics) — Next-Generation Sequencing tests for oncology, hereditary risk, cardiology. Ran over 800,000 tests in 2025 (+28% YoY). Revenue \$955M in FY2025 (+111% YoY).

- Data & Applications — Licenses de-identified multimodal data to biopharma for drug discovery. Revenue \$316M in FY2025 (+31% YoY). Insights sub-segment grew 68% in Q4 2025. Net revenue retention 126%.

- Algos — FDA-cleared AI algorithms trained on the dataset. The ECG-AF algo predicts undiagnosed atrial fibrillation and receives \$128 per algorithm run via CMS reimbursement. Currently minimal revenue but Stephen considers this the transformational growth driver.

**Revenue model:** Diagnostic tests billed to insurance + data licensing subscriptions to biopharma + per-use Algo fees (emerging). The diagnostic business is the data flywheel — tests generate data, data trains better models, better models attract more tests.

- FY2025: ~\$1.27B total revenue (+83% YoY), first positive Adjusted EBITDA achieved in Q3 2025
- FY2026 guidance: ~\$1.59B revenue (+25% growth), ~\$65M Adjusted EBITDA
- Total Contract Value: over \$1.1B as of Dec 2025
- Net revenue retention: 126% (customers increasing spend 20%+ annually)

**Key customers/partnerships:**

- AstraZeneca: over \$200M in agreements
- GSK: \$180M minimum commitment
- Pathos: \$350M agreement for oncology foundational models
- Merck: multi-year strategic collaboration (Mar 2026)
- 19 of the top 20 pharmaceutical companies buy data from Tempus
- Over 70 data licensing customers signed

**Technology / Competitive moat:**

- 400+ petabytes of de-identified multimodal healthcare data (Facebook has ~300PB for comparison)
- Datacenter with 1,000+ H200 AI chips, second datacenter under construction
- 700+ software engineers, 400+ PhDs, 100+ MDs
- January 2026: launched Paige Predict (digital pathology AI) following \$81M Paige acquisition, adding ~7M digitized pathology slides
- Tempus Next deployed in ~150 hospitals, screening 60,000+ patients monthly
- AI co-pilot "David" integrated into Northwestern Medicine's EHR platform

**Algo business potential:** Stephen's key thesis driver. One ECG algo alone could be "worth hundreds of millions of dollars a year" and Tempus expects "thousands of algorithms in the near future." The algo business "could be worth \$10 million, or \$10 billion a year by 2030."

## Competitive Position

Stephen's view: Tempus is a "wide moat" operation (Feb 2026 chat). When comparing Healwell AI (HWAIF), Stephen said they "lacked any technology of note and were trying to cobble together an AI healthcare company without a real technical advantage other than we use AI" — in contrast to TEM's massive data moat and commercial traction.

**vs Healwell AI (HWAIF):** "I looked at Healwell when I bought TEM, I thought they lacked any technology of note" — dismissed as acquisition-driven without core technology.

**vs other AI companies:** Stephen groups TEM with SoundHound (SOUN) and Airship AI (AISP) as his "AI sector." He considers both TEM and SOUN "wide moat operations" that were unfairly sold off alongside generic AI names (Feb 2026). Of the three, Stephen and subscribers consistently prefer TEM as the strongest pick.

**Moat sources:** (1) Scale of dataset is unreplicable — no competitor has this volume of multimodal medical data; (2) Flywheel effect — diagnostics feed data, data improves models, models attract more diagnostics; (3) 7,000 oncologist relationships embedded in workflow; (4) FDA clearances and CMS reimbursement create regulatory barriers; (5) "There is limited, if any, competition in the data sector."

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Jan 2026, Trade Alert #97):** "AI will be transformational in many industries. I have always believed that disease diagnosis and precision medicine will be one of the most positive aspects of this new technology." Stephen had been searching for an AI medical play for over a year and invested in several without success before TEM passed the "Commercial Validation hurdle."

Key thesis points: (1) Industry-leading scale with 400PB dataset creates sustainable competitive advantage; (2) Diagnostics flywheel is commercially proven and profitable; (3) Data licensing business is high-margin recurring revenue with 126% net retention; (4) Algo business is at an inflection point — first CMS reimbursement secured at \$128 per run, potentially transformational; (5) Conservative DCF (no algo success) gives \$16B valuation vs \$12B market cap = 25% minimum upside; (6) Full DCF with algo growth gives \$177 fair value; (7) Technical chart suggests \$300 target.
