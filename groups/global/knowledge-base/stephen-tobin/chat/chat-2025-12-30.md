In the Chinese automotive industry, there is a clear distinction between companies that are State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs) and those that are private. The automakers described as "controlled" by the Chinese government are the SOEs, which are managed either by the central government or by provincial/municipal authorities.
The "Big Four" (Central Government Control)
These companies are directly supervised by the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission (SASAC) of the State Council. They are the most influential and have the direct backing of the central government.
 * SAIC Motor (Shanghai Automotive Industry Corporation): The largest of the four. It owns the MG, Roewe, and Maxus brands and has massive joint ventures with Volkswagen and General Motors.
 * FAW Group (First Automotive Works): Famous for the Hongqi (Red Flag) luxury brand used by high-ranking officials. It also operates JVs with Toyota and Volkswagen.
 * Dongfeng Motor Corporation: Based in Wuhan, it produces brands like Voyah and Aeolus and partners with Honda and Nissan.
 * Changan Automobile: One of the oldest industrial groups in China, it produces brands like Deepal and Avatr (a JV with Huawei and CATL) and has a partnership with Ford.
Regional State-Owned Automakers
These companies are controlled by local or provincial governments. While they operate similarly to the Big Four, their primary reporting line is to municipal authorities.
 * BAIC Group: Controlled by the Beijing Municipal Government. It owns brands like Beijing and Arcfox and is the primary partner for Mercedes-Benz and Hyundai in China.
 * GAC Group: Controlled by the Guangzhou Municipal Government. Its primary self-owned brands are Aion (EVs) and Trumpchi, with major JVs with Toyota and Honda.
 * JAC Motors (Anhui Jianghuai Automobile): Controlled by the Anhui Province government. It is notable for its cooperation with Volkswagen and NIO (though NIO is private, JAC has historically manufactured their cars).
 * Chery Automobile: While it has a more complex "mixed-ownership" structure today, it remains heavily influenced and majority-owned by the Wuhu Municipal Government in Anhui.
