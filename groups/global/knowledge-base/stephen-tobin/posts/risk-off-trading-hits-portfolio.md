# Risk Off Trading Hits Portfolio

*Moving back to cash*

January 17th 2023

# Moving to Cash

One of the key elements of my trading plan is not to lose money. I am not a buy-and-hold investor, nor do I average down. When things look bad, I change my trading to reduce risk; when things look good, I increase the risk. This strategy saved me a lot of money during the credit crunch and during COVID-19, I need to trigger a defensive move now. The portfolio has been hit hard in January, down 11%, and I need to preserve funds.

At the moment, the sentiment looks negative, as a result, I will move some of my holdings to cash, eliminating the holdings I have of the companies furthest away from profit and those without near-term catalysts. New trades will be lower risk and aimed at picking up companies trading at a discount. The aim of a 100% increase remains unchanged, but I will do it from a defensive posture.

I have already closed ABAT, and several more will be closed during the coming days, (NKLA, TSP, BLBD, LIDR and IONQ are the positions I expect to close, but there could well be more) I will be booking losses on these positions, which many people disagree with however, I view it as keeping my powder dry. I will pivot the portfolio to less risky bets with clear near-term catalyst points and companies already generating sales and profits. 

The January stock market is often a prelude to what will come that year. We are only halfway through the month, and much is yet to be decided, but I will always try to act early.

I use the wave chart for the S&P 500 to measure the overall sentiment of the market and make overarching decisions regarding managing my trading account. The long-term weekly chart above is quite concerning, and it suggests that the bull market we have been working with since 2009 may be about to pull back for 2024 before it starts its fifth and final wave higher. This scenario would be dropped if the chart makes a new high. However, the price action so far in January is definitely of the risk off variety. The chart gives a potential target price for the pullback of 3500, which is a drop of just over 25%. My portfolio amplifies the movement of the S & P, representing a drop of 50% to -70 % for the small, high-risk companies I trade in.

It is just conjecture, but the chart agrees with the geo-political backdrop. Armed conflict is escalating at many trouble spots. The Middle East crisis is spreading, with Iran firing missiles into Pakistan and the US and UK being drawn into a conflict in Yemen. A major UK newspaper, the left-leaning Guardian newspaper, published an op-ed suggesting that the leader of North Korea has already decided to go to war with South Korea, and the recent changes to the constitution he announced are preparing for that eventuality. The rise of Nationalism worldwide will inevitably constrain growth as each leader puts his own country first, putting up barriers to international trade and the movement of people. A real threat to democracy is swirling in 2024. What will happen to the conflict in Ukraine after the US presidential elections is unclear, and how a new Trump administration will react to issues overseas is unknown but may be behind the recent surge in rhetoric from North Korea regarding the South and China regarding Taiwan. The EU is somewhat fractured after the loss of the UK, and the Hungarian nationalist leader is siding with Putin on many issues. The rise of right-wing nationalism makes it far more difficult for the EU to act as a unifying voice. 

History students will know that isolationism and pandering to dictators have never turned out well. Still, few of today's voters are old enough to remember past events or are interested in studying the past. 

The chart reflects the market's concern, but we must wait to see how it works.
