In the interview, a few important points were discussed:

## The Zapata Balance sheet

Before the merger, they did not expect any significant redemptions; however, that proved not to be the case, and over 90% of redemptions did take place. They had indicated a closing merger cash balance in the region of $250 million, which turned out to be $7 million. Since then, they have raised a further $ 5 million, and it was clear from the discussion that further funds will be needed in the coming quarters. The redemptions were a real blow and probably caused the share price collapse we saw immediately after trading began. It was a significant paper loss for investors; some will have exited. I remain long because I do not think the value proposition Zapata offers has changed.

## Zapata Is a Software Company

Christopher explained the offering at length. Zapata will train a language learning model on customer data; language is not the right term as it will be numbers, not words; we are not talking about ChatGPT.

Time series data is a measurement repeatedly taken at a specific time; for a telecom company, it could be voltage at a specific cellular tower. The amount of data will be vast, perhaps hundreds of different measurements for each tower on a network of a thousand towers. Zapata will use the historical data to train a mathematical model of the network's behavior; they will then use mathematics to generate new insights and make predictions about what will happen based on current data as it arrives.

This has enormous potential; the software should be able to predict when and where network failures will occur, allowing the network operator to reroute traffic or send a maintenance team to a specific tower before a problem arises. Zapata’s work with Andretti Racing is a proven use case of their ability.

The technology is not industry-specific; it works wherever historical time series data exists to train the model, and the more data available, the more accurate its predictions will be. Not all of the data has to be from the customer; in the case of a telecom network, it could include weather data, traffic data, and solar activity, to name a few examples.

Zapata will help the companies train the model and calculate the mathematics necessary to make the predictions the customer wants.

## Quantum Computers

Christopher discussed a specific example: they used a 16-qubit IBM quantum computer to design some new to-science molecules that could target a specific cancer cell. Two of the 15 molecules discovered were produced and have proven to be effective in the lab. This has enormous potential; they have partnered with D-Wave to explore it further.

Christopher noted that this work could have been performed on a classical computer simulation rather than a quantum computer. The computer is used to solve mathematical problems on time series data; the mathematics in question is linear algebra in its simplest form, multiplying matrices.

The image above shows how two 2x2 matrices are multiplied together; as the size of the matrices grows, the number of calculations grows, and by the time you have really big matrices, it becomes too difficult for classical computers to solve in a short enough time period.

Christopher suggested that his company will need quantum computers with between 60 and 100 qubits to solve the large-scale problems they are studying. That would seem to agree with IONQ, a Zapata partner who hopes to have a 64-qubit machine available in the next 12 months.

## Commercial Progress

Christopher outlined significant commercial and technical success. They have a large Japanese bank as a customer and a large European insurance company. They are designing stress-testing models and answering questions like, if the Fed puts interest rates up and the Europeans don’t, where in my portfolio is stress most likely, and advising trading strategies for different future scenarios.

They have proven the ability to design molecules for Pharma quicker than classical methods with better results.

The deal with Tech Mahindra, a global telecom consultancy, has opened many doors, including two US telecoms with which serious negotiations are taking place.

With Andretti, they have proven the ability to use vast amounts of data to make predictions with incredible accuracy. I still do not fully understand how they can predict a yellow flag incident with such accuracy, but the data is clear and they can.

Zapata continues to work with DARPA and other defense agencies; although the work is confidential, they expect it to have commercial applications elsewhere. I have heard it is related to unmanned flight.

# Conclusion

I am long Zapata; I initially bought just under $10 before the merger was confirmed. The redemptions brought about a veritable price collapse, as a pre-market measure of $48 turned into less than a dollar today.

The lack of cash on the balance sheet is an issue; Zapata has had to raise cash and will need to do so again in the coming quarters, and dilution is inevitable.

The technical success Zapata has had in banking, insurance, motor racing, defense, and pharma outlines the broad utility of its technology.

Zapata is a collection of extremely capable mathematicians. If you bring them the data, they can work out the mathematics necessary to use the data to generate new information. They can also predict with a high degree of certainty what will happen based on what has happened before.

At the moment, competition is limited; they have a first-mover advantage. Only time will tell if they can capitalize on this, but so far, it looks extremely promising.

I have averaged down on the stock, and my cost is now $3.40; I will continue to follow the story and update readers as things progress.
