# Trade Alert: Buying D-Wave

*Sold Centrus for a 104% gain*

I have been covering D-Wave (QBTS) for some time and, until now, have held off investing due to concerns about commercial traction. Recent news from D-Wave suggests commercial traction is gathering pace, and D-Wave has significantly advanced its commercialization efforts. Most noticeably, they are moving their offering to a service model and are CHARGING CUSTOMERS to use their machines.

In the past, D-Wave did not charge because they could not guarantee a particular level of service.

I have altered my mathematical model using the cost data I gained when I interviewed the CEO and CFO last month and the recently released data regarding customers and service contracts. The model gives a new target price of $100 per share. (currently priced near $1).

I will add D-Wave to my Strong Buy Portfolio and am currently writing the article for Seeking Alpha, which I hope will be published early next week. I also plan to cover IBM quantum in November, which is relevant to D-Wave as they are trying to produce similar gate computers. (The investment is most certainly not based on gate computers as I do not think they will work) I think D-Wave is wasting money researching this area which will likely be a dead end.

The new nuclear companies Oklo, NuScale and Nano Nuclear are running hot. It looks like a value trap and I am staying away despite my view that this industry is going to grow.

Oklo is significantly overselling itself; it will not meet its own projections and will probably be at least 5 years behind schedule. It said operations would be in 2027, which is a joke. They have not applied for their design certificate yet (which takes 2 years), nor do they have the proposed site inspected or planning permission granted (that has to involve a public hearing) and finally, they have not applied for their combined operator license, which they must have to start operations (that takes 42 months). I do not think we will see them building this decade, never mind operating.

Nano Nuclear: I am not convinced there is a company here. I am not a short seller but if I were this one would be on my list.

Nuscale: this is a real company with an actual product!! they applied for design approval for their newest product in 2023 and were given a 22-month timetable. Their first product got design approval but was too small for commercial operations but was a good proof of concept.

The problem for NuScale is the business model, they intend to sell their Small Modular Reactors to electricity users like datacenter operators, but that means every customer must become a nuclear-approved operator and get their site approved for a Nuclear plant. That is a deal breaker in my view, they need to change the model.
