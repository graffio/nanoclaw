## Company Overview

Metallium Limited is an Australian junior mining company transitioning into a US-based tech stock, commercializing proprietary Flash Joule Heating (FJH) technology for extracting critical minerals from waste streams. Stephen called it an "urban miner" using waste to mine critical minerals.

**Technology — Flash Joule Heating (FJH):**

- Developed at Rice University; MTM has global rights
- Works by using a material's own resistance to create heat — a massive DC surge reaches 3,000 degrees C within one second, breaking chemical bonds
- Extracts high-value metals from e-waste (printed circuit boards, semiconductor waste) and red mud (aluminum byproduct)
- Advantages over hydrometallurgy/pyrometallurgy: higher recoveries, lower costs, shorter lead times, improved ESG, no chemicals, relatively low energy, no water

**Business model:** Build-own-operate for e-waste processing, plus licensing technology to other recyclers.

**Key partnerships:**

- ElementUSA: New agreement (Dec 2025) to extract gallium and scandium from 30 million tonnes of mineral-rich red mud in Louisiana. Element providing \$10M non-dilutive funding. ElementUSA backed by DoD (\$30M grant), planning \$850M facility. Phase 1 completing 2026, Phase 2 in 2027
- Glencore: MOU for long-term offtake — Glencore to supply majority of e-waste feedstock and take 75% of output. Deal expected to be finalized; would place MTM near major e-waste collection hubs across the US
- Jan 2026: Completed A\$75M strategic capital raise from US investors focused on critical minerals, supporting Gator Point Technology Campus in Texas and planned NASDAQ listing

**Mining reserves:** Pomme Project in Canada (7km from world-class Montviel Deposit with 266M+ tonnes REE), Montviel South, and four Australian deposits (West Arunta, East Claverton, Ravensthorpe, Mukinbudin). FJH technology can extract from these deposits long-term.

**Critical minerals context:** US has no domestic gallium supply and imports 50% of germanium needs. DoD designated both as critical minerals. Gallium prices up 60% in 12 months. China controls over 90% of refined rare earths.

**Finances:** Positive shareholder equity AUD\$26M, zero debt, cash AUD\$7M (pre A\$75M raise). Less than a year of cash runway pre-raise. 50% dilution in 2025, more expected in 2026. CEO pay AUD\$18M total but only AUD\$400K salary. Stephen tentatively predicts US NASDAQ listing in 2026.
