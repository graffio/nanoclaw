# Trade Alert: Buying Aris

*Realignment of Portfolio*

I bought shares in Aris Water Systems yesterday and have sent an article to seeking alpha for publication.

Despite the recent slow trading, I have taken the opportunity to conduct a thorough evaluation of my performance. I firmly believe that transparency is key in trading. It provides a clear record of my decisions and actions, and it prevents me from hiding from any missteps. This transparency is a constant reminder that I am always striving to do better.

Looking at the ratings I have given companies in 2024, I clearly need to drop the Buy rating from my trading.

As part of my revised strategy, companies that I rate as a buy will now be placed on a watch list rather than immediately added to my portfolio. This will allow me to monitor their performance closely, and when the time is right, I will make the purchase.

The difference between a strong buy and a buy is the balance sheet. I rate a company a buy based on its competitive situation and its products compared to the competition.

Strong Buys are companies rated a buy with a clear path to profitability and the balance sheet to fund that path.

Investing in only Strong Buy ratings will mean less trading but much higher returns, which is the most important thing.

I built a spreadsheet using my Strong Buy ratings from seeking alpha articles written after Jan 1st 2023; I assumed all trades remained open unless I had written a further article with a lower rating. I only allowed one trade per company, even if I had written multiple articles and assumed an investment of $250 per trade (or as close as possible, depending on share price). The results were better than my actual trading results, with a much lower drawdown.

## The First Strong Buy

**Aris Water Solutions** is an independent, growth-oriented company headquartered in Houston, Texas. It is a leader in developing and operating **produced water infrastructure and recycling** for the largest operators in the **Permian Basin**. Its business involves gathering, transporting, disposing of, recycling, and reusing water associated with oil and gas production.

Aris is a dividend-paying, free cash-flow-generating company that has developed significant pipeline infrastructure and water-handling facilities. It is working to recover more from each barrel of water it handles and is investigating various approaches to improving the quality of recycled water in the hope of using it to replenish the water table.

The company meets all of the criteria for a Strong Buy recommendation, and I have added the stock to my portfolio.
