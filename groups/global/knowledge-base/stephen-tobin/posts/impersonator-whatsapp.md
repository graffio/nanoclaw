# Impersonator WhatsApp

*I never Whatsapp*

Subscribers have contacted me to ask if I am behind WhatsApp messages they have received from a Substack account claiming to be me. I have read about subscribers to other services falling for these scams. They are very sophisticated and seem to be able to convince people, and some have lost considerable amounts of money as a result.

So far, I have come across 18 different attempts to impersonate me. They use my name, my photo, and take an image from my posts to make it look familiar. When you look closely at the Substack address, you can tell it’s not me.

**Note:**

I will never contact anyone individually

I do not use WhatsApp

I do not, and will not, offer any other services

I do not sell signals or buy crypto

I will never ask you to purchase anything

I will never ask you to join me in an investment

I will never manage funds for anyone.

My service is straightforward.

I send Trade Alerts to subscribers telling them what I am going to invest in and why I am doing it—absolutely nothing else. If you are offered anything else from someone purporting to be me, it is a scam, and you should not respond. Reporting it to SubStack will allow them to remove the account.

If you'd like to verify anything, please feel free to leave a note in the Substack chat or comment on one of my articles. But I can guarantee in advance it won’t be me if you feel the need to ask.
