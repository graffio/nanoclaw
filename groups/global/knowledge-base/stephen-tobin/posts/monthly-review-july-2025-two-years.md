## The Trading Plan

I invest exclusively in emerging technologies that are likely to be a significant disruptive force in the future.

I search for companies approaching commercialization by performing deep dive investigations of the company's technology, its competitors, and market potential.

This research includes personal research of the company's key executives with interviews and background checks. Most of the companies I invest in are small, often pre-commercialization, and almost always pre-profit.

Selecting the right stocks and avoiding the wrong ones is the key to success, the SmallCap sector as a whole has dropped in value in the last year but careful stock selection has allowed us to outperform all of the indices.

### August Outlook

**Macro View**

I was very positive moving into July, but am far more wary as we start this new month. The actions of the US administration are difficult to decipher. They are pursuing a new set of policies, and the outcomes are having short-term unexpected consequences, leaving it very difficult to predict the longer-term impact.

The effect is noticeable on Imports and exports as they jumped then collapsed around the tariff dates. Companies stocked up before the tariffs in Q1 and then reduced their stock levels to normal in Q2.

[

[Image 7: Image 7](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/3ad7b30c-ca8a-414b-b775-272b34ad043f_1054x614.png)

The net effect is close to normal, but the swings are very large and will have caused problems with supply chains and increased operating costs.

GDP growth looks quite resilient

The GDP figure looks essentially in line with previous years, not as good as 2021, or 2023, but better than 2022.

However, this week's jobs report is harder to interpret; it could be signs of a weakening economy, but it could just as easily have been caused by companies holding off from hiring as they worked to navigate the tariff impacts and felt uncertainty about the future.

The president's decision to sack the head of the department producing the figures is far worse than the figures themselves. These figures have to be independent for us to trust them, but it now looks like if a department reports a negative result, the department head will be fired. How many will be prepared to do this?

The administration and the Fed may, in the future, be making decisions on flawed data, and the inevitable consequence will be a slowdown of the US economy.

The large cash pile in the account provides some insulation to these macro moves. Friday was the first trading day in August, and the equity holdings fell 2.4% but the account value fell by 1.5% whereas the S&P 500 fell by 1.6%. The cash balance ensured we outperformed the markets when it fell, and I will likely move more of the money to cash if the markets look like they are going to continue to drop.

## Trading Outlook

I have three trades under serious consideration in the autonomous vehicle sector. This sector is a when, not if, buy. We already have good exposure to RoboTaxis, but following the closure of Hesai, we are light on the picks and shovels, the technology that makes autonomous driving possible. I think it is possible I will take all three trades this month.

The Nuclear stock I have been tracking for several months has turned lower and may be approaching an area where it offers good value. We have two Nuclear plays, but this is another sector almost certain to do well in the next few years, so additional exposure at this early time would be good.

Finally, the eVTOL/Drone sector, after closing JOBY, we are very light in this area with only one company in the portfolio. Timelines to profitability and quality of technology are the issues here. I am happy with the current holding, but would like additional exposure.

# **Weekly News Digest: 26 July – 2 August 2025**

A busy stretch of late-July headlines featured new contracts, product milestones, and an early wave of second-quarter earnings. **Aptiv, ProPetro, and WeRide** published results, while **Aris Water** and **Ondas** secured notable commercial wins.

### **Aris Water Solutions (ARIS)**

-   Signed a **seven-year extension** of its water gathering and disposal agreement with ConocoPhillips, citing strong activity and produced-water volumes.
    
-   Management said **Q2 adjusted EBITDA should land at the high end of guidance** and reaffirmed full-year outlook.
    

### **EHang Holdings (EH)**

-   Completed a demonstration cargo flight with its VT-20 autonomous eVTOL, moving seafood and medical samples between Zhuhai and Guangzhou (55 nm round-trip) in 55 minutes.
    

### **BAE Systems (BAESF)**

-   Held its **H1-25 earnings call** on 30 July. Management commentary focused on order-book visibility and ongoing programme execution; detailed financials were discussed on the call transcript.
    
-   Tone of management remarks suggested steady performance; no change to outlook was noted. (Overall impression: **neutral-to-positive** )
    

### **ACM Research (ACMR)**

-   Stock outperformed the market on 30 July, closing up 1.2 % while the S&P 500 slipped.
    
-   Confirmed it will report **Q2-25 earnings before the open on 6 August**.
    

### **ProPetro Holding (PUMP)**

-   Posted **Q2-25 revenue of US$326 m, down 9 % QoQ** due to lower fleet utilisation and weather impacts.
    
-   The headline miss was **over-penalised by the market** , a new 10-year, 80 MW PROPWR contract is a strategic positive.
    
-   Earnings snapshot: **Negative** headline trend, long-term positioning seems reasonable.
    

### **Aptiv (APTV)**

-   Delivered record **adj. EPS of US$2.12 (+34 % YoY) on revenue of US$5.20 bn** , beating consensus on both lines.
    
-   Management emphasised strong volumes in North America and Asia and continued margin expansion despite softer operating cash flow.
    
-   Earnings view: **Positive** .
    

### **ASP Isotopes (ASPI)**

-   Announced seed funding for subsidiary IsoBio to advance antibody-isotope conjugates for oncology.
    
-   Competition Commission of South Africa approved ASPI’s pending acquisition of Renergen Ltd., keeping the scheme on track for Q3 completion.
    

### **WeRide (WRD)**

-   Reported **Q2-25 revenue of US$17.8 m, up 60.8 % YoY** , with Robotaxi sales surging 837 % to US$6.4 m.
    
-   Unveiled an HPC 3.0 platform co-developed with Lenovo and powered by NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor to improve performance and cost of Level-4 fleets.
    
-   Earnings view: **Positive** , driven by rapid top-line growth and new tech partnerships.
    

### **Pony.ai (PONY)**

-   Obtained a permit to launch **fully driverless commercial Robotaxi services in Shanghai’s Pudong New Area** , marking a regulatory milestone for large-scale deployment.
    

### **Ondas Holdings (ONDS)**

-   Subsidiary Airobotics secured a **US$14.3 m purchase order for its Optimus autonomous drone system** , expanding the defence and homeland-security backlog to US$28.7 m.
