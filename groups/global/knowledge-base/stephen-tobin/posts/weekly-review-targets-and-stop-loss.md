## **Active Management: The Plan**

The name Strategic Waves comes from the two key parts of my trading plan:

**Strategic Investment**

I invest in emerging technologies likely to be significant disruptive forces. I start with the technology and review its potential, then move to the competition and complete an industry review. From that, I select companies with the best chance of being winners.

Investment selection starts with the possible winners and focuses on competitive advantage, business plan, and potential for **returns in the next two years**. I build mathematical models and run scenarios. I also reach out to companies to see if they will allow an interview (about 20% agree) to confirm my thoughts and try to get a feel for the management team. If everything checks out, I decide to invest, but then have to decide when.

**Wave Trading**

Every trader wants to buy low and sell high; I want to identify good entry and exit points to maximize returns and minimize losses.

Although I am not a technical trader, I do look at the charts to help me choose timing. I want to buy a pullback, or when a stock is depressed, I use Elliot Wave theory to help with this, and pay for automatic EW analysis.

Investing is not linear, stock prices do not move in a straight line and the market is not logical. I am trying to predict the future and that cannot be done with any certainty, I know I could be wrong and am ready to jump out when it looks like I might be and only want to jump in when the price looks low.

I tend not to set stops, preferring to exit on a review rather than a predetermined value, a fall in share price of around 30% will trigger a review.

I will not buy and hold, waiting for things to happen while the stock price keeps falling. Some technologies take decades to become established, and some never make it. I do not wait to find out.

Regular review and potential exit upon declining value or changed outlook aims to prevent significant losses and allow efficient use of our capital.

Investing in disruptive emerging technologies involves small, often pre-profit or pre-revenue companies prone to hype-driven volatility. Guided by the Gartner Hype Cycle, a proprietary wave pattern, I frequently exit trades as hype unfolds.

Re-entry at lower prices or when the original thesis revives is a key part of the plan.

### Arbe Already on the List

I first identified LiDAR as an emerging technology with potential in 2021 and conducted an industry review. Innoviz and Arbe were identified as the two companies likely to be winners within autonomous vehicles.

I wrote my first article on Arbe in 2022. Back then, it was clear that radar technology would play a big part in autonomous driving. In 2023, I wrote a piece about Tesla using Arbe after a parts manual was released from Tesla showing testing of an Arbe chipset. In 2024, I wrote another piece after interviewing the CEO when he was adamant that Arbe would sign a major deal with a European OEM before the end of the year.

Ultimately, that 2024 deal never materialized (I think it went to APTV), and ARBE stock started to fall; I exited for a loss of 37%. This exit aligned with the regular review of trades and the taking losses early policy.

On Friday, a trade magazine ran a story that Tesla was putting Arbe radar into its Juniper Model Y. This confirmed my 2023 article. It was not new news, but it could well drive Arbe shares higher. If it turns out to be true, Arbe will fulfil the potential first identified in 2022.

It also agrees with my view that Tesla will not be able to run a robotaxi without radar. I have stated this many times: Tesla needs radar. Assuming they are not going with LiDAR, they have only two options: Arbe and Mobileye. No doubt they would get a better deal out of Arbe, and they have been testing the product (according to my unconfirmed article) for two years.

The ARBE trades are good examples of my trading plan: Identify an emerging technology, look at all the competition, and choose likely winners. Analyze the target company in detail, and invest at the right time. Be prepared to exit if things deteriorate, but then rebuy if things improve.
