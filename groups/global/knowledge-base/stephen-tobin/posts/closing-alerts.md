# Closing Alerts

The sell off in tech stocks continues and I will move more funds to cash.

I will exit companies that are not meeting the objectives I had expected and will likely put the cash generated into companies that have pulled back despite good earnings. The current pull back, despite being deep, remains within the bounds we have experienced before with this account and the strategy in previous instances to move to cash then buy cheap has proved very successful when the correction ends.

Today I will exit

ARBE: in our last meeting the CEO was clear that he expected to announce a major volume production order with a global auto OEM in H1 this year, we are now well beyond that date and the company seems to be now focussing on smaller more niche applications that make our price target void.

ELVA: Our largest position still seems to have medium term potential but they did raise additional funds having said they did not need too and are expanding into areas that lie outside of our original thesis. These new areas, especially stationary storage, could lead to big revenue growth but they are highly competitive areas that may not be as profitable as expected. It is also worth noting that key USP of cycle life is being eroded by other manufacturers.

I will continue to assess other companies over the day and intend to have a complete list of those I want to hold onto in time for the weekly review when I will also share the stats on previous pullbacks we have experienced
