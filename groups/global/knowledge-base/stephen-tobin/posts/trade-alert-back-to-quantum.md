# Trade Alert: Back to Quantum

*Our most succesful sector*

Quantum computing has been our most successful sector in terms of profit made (that's probably the only measure of interest).

The sector is changing, with multiple new companies entering the market via IPOs and SPAC transactions. Existing companies like IONQ are using their large balance sheets to acquire companies and build a full quantum ecosystem.

Quantum sensing is proving successful in GPS-compromised areas, and quantum clocks are becoming important for navigation.

The technology has alwasy offered the potential for huge disruption but only if it works.

It has always been my view that, in new technologies, a first-mover advantage has enormous value, and in Quantum, I am buying exactly that. The company with the first mover advantage and real operations.

As always, this is another super risky trade. We will likely see whipsaw price action, and given the level of competition, the nature of the technology, and the obvious valuation problems with these companies, I could easily be wrong. If you are going to invest, only invest money you can afford to lose

## Trade Alert #100: Buying D-Wave (QBTS)

Unfortunately, I didn’t manage to get this completed before the markets open, but given today's price action, I have decided to press ahead with the trade.

As I indicated in the weekend review, I am buying Quantum, returning to our most successful company, a proven thesis, so a full $600 investment.

I have written an article for publication on SeekingAlpha about this investment and that article has to be exclusive to SeekingAlpha. Writing on Seeking Alpha is why companies like D-Wave speak to me and invite me to their conferences and investor meetings. Without that kind of access, I would not have a full understanding of what is happening.

The key arguments for the investment are quite simple. Firstly, their existing technology is gaining commercial traction, with 5 computers sold to date, and the Quantum as a service cloud offering is gaining momentum, including a $10 million order from a single Fortune 100 company received in January, which is greater than the entire cumulative sales of this product.

The reason for buying now is the new dual rail qubit technology, which will be a game changer and put QBTS at the front of the race to produce a general-purpose quantum computer not only an annealing machine.

**Key Points**

-   **Transition Beyond NISQ:** D-Wave is moving past the Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) era, aiming to deliver a universal gate quantum computer with error correction ahead of competitors.
    
-   **Commercial Success:** The Advantage 2 Quantum annealer is commercially viable, and the company has secured major contracts, including a $20 million deal with Florida Atlantic University and a $10 million Quantum Computing as a Service (QCaaS) agreement with a Fortune 100 company.
    
-   **Organic Revenue Growth:** Revenue is growing organically through sales of quantum computers and QCaaS, not through acquisitions. Forecasts suggest accelerating growth, with targets of $60 million in 2026 and $150 million in 2027.
    
-   **Strategic Acquisition:** The acquisition of Quantum Circuits, Inc. brings dual rail qubit technology, enabling D-Wave to accelerate its gate-model roadmap and target the release of a gate-model system as early as 2026.
    
-   **Technology Roadmap:** Plans include a 100,000-qubit annealer and a commercial 17-qubit gate machine in 2026, a 181-qubit error-corrected gate system in 2028, and a 1,000-qubit system by 2030.
    
-   **Industry Impact:** Use cases span defence, healthcare, telecoms, and AI, with proven results in missile defence optimisation and pharmaceutical research. The technology has the potential to disrupt multiple sectors.
    
-   **Risks:** The main risk is that the technology’s disruptive potential remains theoretical, and wide-scale applicability is not yet proven. The company is still in its early stages, and large customers have not yet renewed major deals.
    

**Financial Strength:** D-Wave now has a strong balance sheet, significant cash reserves, and improved shareholder equity. Forecasts suggest continued growth, but uncertainties remain.
