## Weekly News Digest

Coverage period: 1 – 6 September 2025

The first trading week of September delivered a steady flow of partnership announcements, product milestones and corporate actions across the mobility, aerospace, energy-transition and quantum-technology themes that dominate our universe.

Company Updates

### EHang (Holdings) (EH)

-   Signed a strategic cooperation agreement with **China Road & Bridge Corp.** to jointly promote and sell pilotless aerial vehicles worldwide, supporting low-altitude-economy growth initiatives.
    
-   Broader news flow noted the partnership within a wider rally in Chinese tech equities on 5 Sep.
    
-   Separately, Bragar Eagel & Squire initiated an investigation into the company following its 26 Aug revenue-guidance reduction; the law-firm announcement on 5 Sep keeps litigation risk in focus.
    

### Electrovaya (ELVA)

-   Completed the first export shipment of newly developed **1.5 kWh NMC battery modules** to a Japan-based construction-equipment OEM. The custom 51 Ah format passed UN38.3 testing and leverages the firm’s Infinity safety technology.
    
-   Management added that its planned Jamestown, NY cell facility will be tooled for both 54 Ah and 51 Ah formats to meet anticipated demand from heavy-duty customers.
    

### Aptiv (APTV)

-   Shares rose 1.6 % on 3 Sep after unveiling a **US$400 m Midwest capacity expansion** and a Level-4 autonomous-driving partnership with a European OEM aimed at 2027 commercialisation. The initiative is intended to shorten ADAS lead times and improve margins.
    

### ASP Isotopes (ASPI)

-   In a 2 Sep shareholder letter, management confirmed its **Renergen merger remains on track to close in 3Q25**, highlighting the creation of an integrated supplier of electronic and isotopically enriched gases with synergy capture expected from 2026.
    
-   Three isotope enrichment facilities now operational, first shipments of Silicon-28 and Ytterbium-176
    
-   Quantum Leap Energy expected to list in Q4 started procurement of first facility and is collaborating with Fermi America for HALEU production in Texas.
    

### Arbe Robotics (ARBE)

-   Won an **Innovation Award at the 2025 Just Auto Excellence Awards**. CEO Kobi Marenko underscored the competitive edge of the company’s ultra-high-resolution imaging radar ahead of demonstrations at IAA Mobility (9-12 Sep).
    

### WeRide (WRD)

-   Launched **24-hour driverless robotaxi operations in Guangzhou** on 5 Sep. August saw the unveiling of WePilot AiDrive ADAS, co-developed with Bosch, expanding the firm’s product stack.
    

### Pony.ai (PONY)

-   Announced a partnership with Qatar’s Mowasalat (Karwa) to **deploy autonomous robotaxis on public roads in Doha**, marking further expansion in the Middle East following activity in Dubai.
    

### D-Wave Quantum (QBTS)

-   Zacks Investment Ideas highlighted the stock, alongside IonQ and Rigetti, in a piece on quantum-computing break-outs published 4 Sep.
    
-   Hired a Chief Information Security Officer to expand into quantum encryption services.
    

### SES AI (SES)

-   Water Tower Research **withdrew coverage** on 3 Sep, citing a realignment of its technology universe and advising investors not to rely on prior estimates. Companies pay Water Tower for coverage, the note means SES are no longer paying likely a reflection of increased Wall Street coverage
    

### Hesai Group (HSAI)

-   Launched a **global offering of 17 m Class B shares** ahead of a dual-primary listing in Hong Kong (stock code 2525); the Hong Kong public tranche opens 8 Sep.
    
-   Confirmed status as the **exclusive short-range lidar supplier for Motional’s IONIQ 5 robotaxi**, formalising a two-year collaboration.
    

### AIRO Group Holdings (AIRO)

-   Will be **added to the Russell 2000 and Russell 3000 indices** effective 22 Sep, reflecting growing market relevance. Management also referenced a new US manufacturing site for its RQ-35 Heidrun ISR drone.
    

### Vertical Aerospace (EVTL)

-   Appointed **former EASA Executive Director Patrick Ky to its board** effective 1 Sep, strengthening certification expertise as the company targets 2028 authorisation of its VX4 eVTOL aircraft.
    
-   $100 million ATM agreement with Jefferies LLC, the first offering of 2025.
    

### NuScale Power (SMR)

-   Shares fell 10.9 % on 4 Sep despite announcing a **landmark 6-GW SMR deployment programme** with TVA and ENTRA1 Energy, the largest small-modular-reactor pact in the US to date. Liquidity stood at US$489.9 m at quarter-end, but investors focused on execution and capital-intensity risks.
    

## Key Themes to Watch

-   **Autonomous Mobility Commercialisation** – Agreements by EHang, WeRide, Pony.ai and Aptiv all indicate accelerating efforts to translate pilot programmes into revenue-generating operations across Asia, the Middle East and Europe.
    
-   **Capital-Markets Activity** – Hesai’s Hong Kong dual-listing and AIRO’s upcoming Russell index inclusion highlight continuing funding-market access for growth hardware platforms.
    
-   **Electrification & Energy-Transition Supply Chain** – Electrovaya’s module exports and ASP Isotopes’ spin off update underscore widening global demand for battery and specialty-gas inputs supporting construction equipment and semiconductor markets.
    
-   **Government Alignment** – NuScale’s 6-GW programme with TVA embed public-sector partnerships as a foundation for scale in defence and clean-energy verticals.
    

## Outlook

The coming week features IAA Mobility in Munich, where Arbe and other ADAS vendors will showcase next-generation sensing solutions, and the opening of Hesai’s Hong Kong book-build. Investors will also monitor follow-through orders tied to NuScale’s SMR framework and regulatory feedback on EHang’s global UAV push. While broader macro concerns remain, the period under review shows companies continuing to execute on strategic partnerships, certification milestones and capital-raising initiatives that will underpin long-term growth.
