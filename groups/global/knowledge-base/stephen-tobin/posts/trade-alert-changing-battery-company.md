## Switching to Amprius (AMPX)

SES and AMPX have ended up targeting the same market for their battery cells; neither appears to have a cell for EVs at the moment, and both have moved to target the Drone and Light vehicles (scooters, electric bikes, etc). The eVTOL market is also targeted by both.

AMPX is focused on developing Silicon Anodes, whereas SES is exploring new electrolytes; they both share the same goal: improved battery energy density and cycle life.

I think AMPX is the better bet, they are much farther down the commercialization road, they have developed a broad international customer base, and the quality of customers seems to be much higher.

In Drones, SES has announced Data Blanket as its first customer, an American start-up that will use drones made by Ascent Aerosystems to monitor wildfires. Their funding round raised $4 million.

In contrast, AMPX has announced a contract with a Fortune 500 company.

SES will be making cells on its former A sample lines, and AMPX has multiple worldwide contract manufacturers.

The lithium ion cell of SES has an energy density of 417 Wh/Kg, the Amprius Si-Core battery cell has an energy density of 450 Wh/Kg; they both quote cycle life around 800.

AMPX may become a customer of SES and its electrolyte discovery tool as they continue to advance the Si-Core product but for the time being I think AMPX is a better bet.

**Amprius History**

Amprius was a US startup with two research lines: Silicon Nanowires and Silicon Particles. The company initially split into three divisions: Amprius US (nanowires), Amprius Nanjing (particles, later rebranded as Berzilius), and Amprius Wuxi (battery manufacturing). Only Amprius US became the public company.

Si-Maxx, AMPX's first battery cell based on its proprietary nanowire architecture, has a complex and expensive manufacturing process, limiting its scalability and cost-effectiveness. It also struggled with integration into standard cylindrical cells. While Si-Maxx offered excellent energy density, I believe it has reached a dead end.

**Change of product**

The particle research of Berzilius (Amprius Nanjing) has delivered an anode material capable of matching the performance of the nanowires under development by AMPX. The Berzilius product is easy to scale and does not use the hazardous silane gas required by the nanowire product.

AMPX has transitioned to its Si-Core product, utilizing more cost-effective silicon anode materials purchased from Berzilius. This allows for manufacturing by existing contract manufacturers using standard cell formats. AMPX has re-focused its R&D on improving cells with the Berzilius product and has halted the expansion of Si-Maxx facilities, shifting customers to Si-Core.

**The New Business Model**

The old strategy was to develop Si-Maxx, build manufacturing facilities in Fremont and Colorado and sell Si-Maxx cells into demanding applications. It was going quite well with orders signed from Airbus and the US military. But it does not scale, it costs too much and has no real performance advantage over Si-Core.

The new strategy involves buying anode material from Berzilius, developing new cells that are drop-in replacements for existing lithium-ion cells, and targeting specific vertical markets (e.g., micro mobility, drones, eVTOL) that will pay a premium for increased energy density despite a higher cost and slightly lower cycle life (800 vs. +1,000 cycles).

**Commercial Traction**

The new plan is working, with sales growing and profitability on the near-term horizon. Q1 2025 revenue increased 380% year-over-year to $11 million, with 102 customers and 65% of revenue from outside the US. AMPX now offers over a dozen batteries, including a new 21700 cylindrical cell with a 25% energy boost.

In Q1, AMPX announced a $15 million order from an unmanned aerial system manufacturer for H2 2025 shipment. 25% of Q1 revenue came from the light electric vehicle market. AMPX added $34 million in new customer purchase orders to its backlog in Q1, securing large-scale manufacturing contracts for high revenue visibility in 2025.
