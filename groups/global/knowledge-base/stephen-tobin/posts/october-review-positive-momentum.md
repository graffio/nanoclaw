# October Review: Positive momentum and Profits Booked

*Two trades for November already identified*

October continued the volatile trading we saw in September, the portfolio saw an all-time high mid-month and was up 25% at one point. We managed to book some big profits and opened some trades that seem to have high potential. We closed the month up more than 100% YTD for the first time in 2025.

Once again, we outperformed all the major indices by a considerable margin.

The S&P finished up 2% thanks to upbeat earnings from Big Tech in the final days of the month and optimism around a US China trade deal. The gains were powered by the US mega caps with the S&P top 50 up 4%.

Japan's S&P 500 gained 6.8% in October, led by large caps and IT (Japan’s small caps rose 2%). The Australian ASX is now up 90% YTD, I will continue to evaluate these countries and I hope to find a Japanese company to take advantage of the momentum being seen. I opened positions in two Australian companies this month but have no other Australian prospects at the moment.

The Hong Kong indices fell 3.5% in October over fears of a renewed US-China trade war; likely, they will see a rise in November as that concern wanes. Although we do not own any stocks on the exchange we have several US stocks that are companies based in China with a dual listing in Hong Kong and may see a benefit as a result.

Our small caps portfolio continues to power ahead of the indices delivering its seventh consecutive positive month.

Historically, November has been a positive month.

There is a general pessimism about the US economy; many forecasters are suggesting a top is already here or is close by. However, in my 30+ years of trading, they have always been predicting a pullback is near by, but it rarely seems to happen.

I will be placing two trades at the beginning of the week, one of them is in the retail industry, increasing the diversity of the portfolio.

Both companies have delivered good results for us in the past and have made excellent business progress, so I will be looking to buy again, aiming for a 100% return in the medium term.

I also expect to continue diversifying the portfolio; currently, 22% of the portfolio is in Aerospace and Defence, which is likely too concentrated. In addition to the two trades I identified above, which will bring us back into the retail market, I expect to acquire an environmental company and continue exploring the electricity generation market in the Permian Basin, with several potential targets.

An investment in Quantum remains elusive, but I continue to watch new entrants. Current prices are likely to keep me out of the sector for the time being despite it being our most profitable sector to date.
