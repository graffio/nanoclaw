# October Outlook

In 2024, October was one of the two months that delivered a negative return, but the trades made that month went on to provide record-breaking returns in November and December.

Once again, this October, we have a solid prospect list significantly longer than it was in September, comprising 22 companies, which typically results in 7 or 8 trades per month.

I hope to add to our Drone holdings. We have held ONDS and DRO, making good money, but missed out on the large gains those stocks have made in the last couple of weeks. Currently, all the military drone companies appear slightly overvalued; this is often the case when the sector receives extensive press coverage. I continue to evaluate the civilian drone suppliers, but will wait before buying back into the military. I expect one civilian drone investment next week.

We are currently underexposed to the space sector, with only one holding, and I will be making my second investment in the coming days. The target company meets all of our criteria, just entering the commercialization phase with a technological lead. I don’t expect the sudden growth we saw with the recycling stock in September but I think a 100% return in 12 months is quite feasible. I did expect to buy a different space stock this week, but the final DD check, an interview with an insider, convinced me to stay away.

I do not hold a quantum stock; unfortunately, I am not prepared to pay the current sky-high prices for these stocks. I continue to look for adjacent sectors and companies that will benefit from the slow commercialization of quantum.

The environmental sector appears to be picking up, and I expect to make one investment in this area during October. I have identified two companies that meet the criteria, and I am conducting due diligence by speaking with their management.

The pullback in our stocks has several looking like excellent value. I expect to add two positions in the coming days, increasing our exposure to the nuclear industry. I am also actively researching electricity generation in the Permian basin and the prospects for siting data centers there. It is an exciting prospect, but I have not yet slimmed down my list of prospects.
