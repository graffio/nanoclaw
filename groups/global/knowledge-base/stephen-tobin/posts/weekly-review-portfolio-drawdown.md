# Weekly Review: Portfolio Drawdown

*Normal or something different?*

Investing in small-cap, early-stage companies is inherently risky; inevitably, there will be ups and downs. However, it is essential to monitor performance and adjust the strategy if needed. As the project to reach $100k continues, it is now in month 27, we generate more data than can be used for this task.

So is the current pullback normal or not?

The total account balance decreased from $18,282 to $17,299 last week, representing a decline of $983, or 5.4%. The account has now fallen 11% in the first two weeks of November.

The account remains above its target, but the pullback is clear on this chart.

Data so far suggests that this pullback is similar to the three previous significant pullbacks. As the plan has worked well in the past, I intend to stick with it. Average gains in the three months following a similar pullback are 31%.

The first part of the plan is to move to cash, which has been implemented as I have exited multiple positions so far.

It means the cash balance is now at an all-time high of $8,994, which is 52% of the account balance.

The pullback may not be over, and I have made decisions about which companies I want to hold and which I am prepared to exit to preserve capital. Companies I intend to keep are those I believe have a strong probability of moving significantly higher over the coming months.

I have also identified the previously held companies I would like to re-buy if the opportunity presents itself. Some have delivered excellent earnings reports but have still pulled back as much as those who have not done so well. Additionally, several companies on the prospect list are performing well and will present excellent opportunities as soon as the pullback is complete. Hopefully, this will be sooner rather than later. Still, with our substantial cash balance, we are somewhat insulated from any further adverse developments and are well-positioned to capitalize on opportunities as they arise.

## DroneShield

The collapse in DroneShield drove much of last week's losses, and I was unable to exit in time. The drop appears, as far as I can determine, entirely due to the CEO and other senior managers selling their performance-related stock options as soon as they vested.

Of course, they have the right to do this, and I understand that this component of their salary accounts for 80% of the total, so their monthly pay is relatively low compared to the size of the company. However, it shows a disregard for shareholders or ignorance of the effect of their actions. Neither is good, and it has seriously dented the credibility of this management team in my eyes. It contradicts the CEO’s comments in the first part of my interview, where he discussed how critical retail shareholders are to the company.

It does not however have any impact on the fundamental business case, if they sign the deals they are expecting then the price will recover and this episode will be viewed as a dilutive event rather than management insensitivity. If they do not secure the deals, then the market will make accusations that go beyond insensitivity.

As I am stuck in this trade and it is below 50% my standard stop rules cannot be applied; this stock requires a unique solution to a unique situation. I will not make a decision for a few days or perhaps even weeks, but if the stock holds its low and shows signs of recovery, I will buy additional shares and average down to help the position get back to break even quickly.

### Virgin Galactic

**Headline results (US$):**

-   Revenue **$0.4 m** (future astronaut deposits)
    
-   Operating expenses **$67 m**, -19 % y/y; Net loss **$64 m**, narrowed from $75 m LY
    

**Management commentary & guidance:**

-   Flight-test programme to begin **3Q 2026**, first commercial spaceflight slated for **4Q 2026**; timelines unchanged from prior quarter.
    
-   Long-term economic model targets **125 flights/year** from initial two ships, implying ~$450 m annual revenue at high margins and ~$100 m adjusted EBITDA; expansion to four ships + second launch vehicle seen lifting revenue potential to ~$1 bn.
    
-   Cash discipline continues; ATM equity programme remains available to bolster liquidity.
    

This is a high-risk play, can they get the first space flights running in H2 2026? It is a tall ask, and they will need almost perfect execution.
