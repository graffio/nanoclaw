### QSI: A Quick Review

Quantum-SI Inc. (QSI) is pioneering next-generation protein sequencing technology, aiming to transform the field of proteomics. Their flagship product, Platinum, is designed for single molecule detection and next-generation protein sequencing, traditionally performed on expensive mass spectrometry devices. The Platinum device, retailing at $70,000, is a desktop machine based around a proprietary silicon chip known as the M2. The process involves loading a sample into the device, splitting proteins into peptides, and allowing these peptides to fall into millions of nanoscale reaction chambers on the silicon chip. Recognizers, which are short-lived fluorescent molecules, bind to specific amino acid sequences. Lasers are then fired at these luminescent recognizers to measure the light emitted. The AI software then uses these measurements to determine the nature of the protein. This innovative approach provides a thorough understanding of which protein is present, making it a crucial tool for cancer research and other proteome-related studies.

Quantum-SI's technology is significant because it addresses the complex nature of the proteome, which has hindered research requiring the sensitivity of DNA sequencing techniques. While mass spectrometry is the current standard method for studying the proteome, it requires high protein levels for detection. Quantum-SI's single molecule-based sequencing method has the potential to revolutionize protein science and medicine. The company's mission is to bring next-generation sequencing to every lab, everywhere, with the long-term goal of developing a machine that can sequence all proteins and identify errors in the sequencing code.

**Investment Case Update**

After my previous write-ups, I did not invest. Commercial take-up seemed slow and uneven; there was no commercial validation of the machine, and industry insiders were sceptical. I could not forecast a breakeven date within an acceptable timeframe or with a sufficient degree of certainty.

### Things have Changed

Quantum-Si is the first company to commercialise next-generation protein sequencing (NGPS) on a benchtop instrument. Rapid kit iterations, a clear multi-year platform road-map, and expanding distribution underpin early but accelerating revenue growth. A recent capital raise extends the cash runway into 2H-27, mitigating funding risk while the high-throughput Proteus system is finalized.

**Competitive landscape and technology comparison**

**1.1 QSI’s differentiation**

-   **Only commercial NGPS platform:** Management states that Platinum® is the sole instrument on the market capable of single-molecule peptide sequencing across millions of nano-wells, a technology “invented” by the founder.
    
-   **Bottom-up single-molecule chemistry:** Semiconductor chip with 2 m reaction chambers and optical waveguides allows parallel interrogation of peptide fragments, generating kinetic information not accessible by bulk methods
    
-   **Scalable architecture:** Proteus™ will shift to an optical architecture, add on-board automation and is expected to deliver an **order-of-magnitude increase in reads per sample** at launch in 2H-26.

## Commercial traction

The technology looks promising, representing a true emerging technology with real disruptive potential and an enormous addressable market. The business model is enhanced by the possibility of ongoing consumable sales developing into recurring revenue streams.

Revenue grew by 183% in 2024, beating my forecast, and Q1 2025 came in at $0.83 million, up 84% YoY. This is despite the funding uncertainty being caused by the new US administration. Academic research had been the primary sales funnel for QSI, but that has changed.

Avantor (AVTR) has signed on as a distributor for QSI; results from this should be seen in the next earnings report (early August). AVTR has a global footprint and is recognised as a leader in the supply of mission-critical research equipment for laboratories. With $7 billion in annual sales and a market capitalization of over $9 billion, it has established relationships with every major research institution.

Avantor represents an upgrading of the sales funnel and validation of the value of this product.

The geographical mix has also changed, shifting from 90% US sales prior to Trump to 85% outside the US post-Trump. This is the result of changes to research funding enacted in the US and the growing international distributor base focusing on commercial sales.

### Balance Sheet

QSI has consistently maintained a strong balance sheet, which was further strengthened with this month's equity raise. I estimate that QSI will have $250 million of cash on the balance sheet at the next earnings call and that the cash runway has been extended into at least late 2027, and possibly mid-2028, depending on sales.

The updated model suggests that this cash balance might be sufficient to achieve positive cash flow, or at least break-even operations. A further capital raise is likely, but it should not be excessively dilutive.
