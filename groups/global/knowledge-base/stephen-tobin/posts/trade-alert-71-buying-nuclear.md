# Trade Alert #71: Buying NuScale (SMR)

I will be buying NuScale (SMR) before the market opens today, targeting a position size of $650.

I will also take a position on my experimental margin account using £60 of margin.

Two significant developments have occurred in the last two weeks, making this a high-potential opportunity.

1.  The 77MWe reactor has been approved in the US
    
2.  Entra1 has signed its first deal.
    

## Key Takeaway

● NuScale Power represents the first and only company to achieve comprehensive regulatory approval for small modular reactor (SMR) technology in the United States

● NuScale's differentiation centers on its proven light water reactor platform rather than experimental advanced reactor designs, reducing technological risk

● Nuscale has strategic partnerships with major industrial players, including Fluor, Doosan Enerbility, and Samsung C&T Corporation

● Proven light water reactor platform with enhanced safety features, passive safety systems, and scalable modular design from single modules to 12-module plants

● Comprehensive engineering and licensing services with 87% gross margins, supporting customers throughout plant development and operation lifecycle.

● Doosan in Korea is already building reactors; 12 are being manufactured at the moment

I first wrote about NuScale last year, rating them a hold.

## SMR and Fluor

Fluor is a $6.5 billion engineering and procurement corporation. They offer project management solutions in three divisions: Urban, Energy, and Mission. They focus on bringing advanced technologies to market, while also providing project management and staffing services. They are a global force with $16 billion in revenue and over $4 billion in annual income.

Fluor has invested +$600 million in SMR and has held as much as 60% of NuScale shares.

Entra1 is a JV between NuScale and Flour. I had doubts about it and wanted to wait to see how Entra1 would operate and if anybody would buy from it.

Last year's short report into NuScale questioned Fluor's commitment. During the recent earnings call, Fluor management made a couple of statements that may fuel this speculation and lead to additional volatility in the stock.

Referring to Nuscale, they said

· We see it as increasingly difficult for the strategic buying community to consummate a transaction at fair value (they are talking about buying SMR shares not products)

· We expect to complete a 15 million share conversion of NuScale shares

· And unveil our monetization plan over the next quarter

Here is the quote from the Fluor earnings call

_For several quarters, we've described our efforts to monetize our ownership of NuScale. With NuScale stock performance in the last few months, we see it as increasingly difficult for the strategic buying community to consummate a transaction at fair value. Nuscale managers were asked about this and this was their response.

_Fluor owns class B units and so they're a class B holder. Class B units may be exchangeable to class B shares. And periodically, we offer the opportunity to engage in exchange....The last time we approached the Class B unitholders... \[they decided to\] exchange. I have no idea. I don't know if the strategy has changed is to be a street shareholder._

**What it Means**

Fluor wants some of its cash back and to make a profit on its investment. It will convert some of its Class B shares into Class A shares, allowing it to sell them on the open market. This is generally a good thing. Currently, Fluor holding Class B shares gives it control of the company and will prevent some investment firms from buying. It is likely to pave the way for NuScale shares to become a less volatile asset in the future.

**NuScale Power SMR: Investment Case Update**

NuScale Power remains the only small modular reactor (SMR) developer with **U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) design approval**. It has just secured a landmark 6 GW development agreement through its strategic partner ENTRA1 Energy. The combination of regulatory precedence, an exclusive global commercialization platform, and an expanding pipeline positions NuScale as a front-runner in the race to deliver near-term advanced nuclear capacity.

**ENTRA1 Alliance – Scale and Exclusivity**

-   In September 2025, **Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) and ENTRA1 Energy signed a collaborative agreement targeting up to 6 GW of NuScale SMR capacity** , enough to power 4.5 million homes or roughly 60 hyperscale data centres.
    
-   ENTRA1 Energy holds the **global exclusive rights** to commercialize, distribute, and deploy NuScale products and services and operates a 50/50 joint venture (ENTRA1 NuScale LLC) focused on project execution and financing. Fluor owns the other 50% of Entra1.
    
-   The agreement validates a private-public development model and offers NuScale access to TVA’s established nuclear operating expertise and credit profile, mitigating first-of-a-kind risk.
    

**Regulatory Leadership**

-   NuScale’s original 50 MWe design received Part 52 certification in 2020, and in **May 2025 the NRC granted Standard Design Approval for the uprated 77 MWe module**.
    
-   NuScale is currently the **sole SMR vendor with NRC design approval**, giving it a multi-year licensing lead that its competitors will find difficult to overcome.
    

**Commercial Pipeline Beyond TVA**

-   Two-site 1,848 MWe programme with Standard Power in Ohio & Pennsylvania targeting 2029 COD, a six-module 462 MWe project in Romania (RoPower) aiming at 2030, and memoranda of understanding in the Czech Republic and Jordan.
    
-   Manufacturing readiness is progressing; management reports 12 power modules already in production to support a 2030 commercial start-up schedule.
    

**Competitive Situation**

The competition in this market is all about certification. Without certification, these companies are just science experiments.

After certification comes the ability to manufacture, and then the business model. NuScale is already manufacturing reactors for deployment in 2030. They could press ahead with this after the smaller reactor was approved.

### Technology and Risks

NuScale has a technological advantage; the company utilizes the same basic technology as the majority of large-scale nuclear reactors, and crucially, it employs LEU fuel, a readily available fuel with multiple suppliers worldwide. The competition uses HALEU and Triso fuel, neither of which has an established supply chain.

Using more standard technology will make certification much easier worldwide, and potential customers will feel more comfortable purchasing a technology they understand and have possibly used before, powered by a fuel they can purchase.

NuScale's approved design lowers risks. The first firm order, expected in 2025, will be a key milestone; a delay could lead to price fluctuations.

A nuclear accident, while not directly NuScale's fault, could harm the stock and slow nuclear adoption.

The upside is enormous. NuScale's reactors can power data centers, industrial units, and provide grid stability. They also highlight the ability to produce 200 tonnes of Hydrogen and 150 million gallons of clean water daily, seeing desalination and hydrogen production as significant markets.

## NuScale Finances

The balance sheet of SMR has improved since my first article; they now have shareholder equity of $500 million and zero debt. Short-term assets of $450 million give them a three-year cash runway.

Revenue is very bumpy; they are selling engineering services to prospective customers, especially RoPower, and not selling the final product. I expect this to continue for several years.

Q2 2025 revenue was $8 million, down from $13 million in Q1, but up from $1 million in Q2 2024.

The net loss was $38 million, which was worse than the forecasted $33 million.

Nuscale has three years of cash, which means the next capital raise will be in 2027, but by then, they should have a pretty long order book and have broken ground on the first sites with the Doosan-manufactured reactors due for delivery in 2030.
