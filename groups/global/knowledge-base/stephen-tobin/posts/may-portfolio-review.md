### Notes

**Ehang's** recent results were disappointing, with revenue and deliveries falling short of expectations (11 aircraft delivered versus an anticipated 25). Management attributed this to order delays pending the final operating license for passenger flights, which was granted at the end of the quarter. Consequently, deliveries and orders have reportedly returned to expected levels. Ehang announced its upcoming VT-35, a longer-range and more conventional aircraft, scheduled for a Q3 unveiling. Production capacity at the original Yunfu site has increased to 1,000 units annually, and a second manufacturing base is under development in collaboration with an automotive partner. While international expansion is underway, there has been little news regarding international type certification. Despite this month's setback, Ehang remains a promising investment, and consideration may be given to increasing the position.

**Hesai** released another excellent earnings report with shipments up 200% and revenue up 50%. Guidance for 2025 was confirmed along with positive EBITDA. Hesai remains the market leader in LiDAR, with a dominant global market share. I will hold onto it and add if an opportunity arises. Both Robosense and Xiaomi have shown notable improvements in their share of the LiDAR market, and I will be focusing on Robosense this month. Xiaomi has already been identified as a target company, but it is currently too highly priced.

**ARIS and PUMP** both continue to struggle with oil prices, and OPEC has announced further production increases, which will not help. ARIS announced a dividend this month, as expected, and PUMP continues to make good progress with its electricity generation business.

When analyzing these companies, I aim to answer only one question. Will the price increase in the next 12-24 months?

To answer that question, I look at four things

-   Technology: Do they have a technological advantage
    
-   Competition: Does the technology lead to a sustainable competitive advantage
    
-   Finances: Can they afford to turn the technological advantage into a commercial one
    
-   Fair Value: What is the likely long-term value of the shares
    
-   Price: Is the share price in a hype cycle, or is it currently low
