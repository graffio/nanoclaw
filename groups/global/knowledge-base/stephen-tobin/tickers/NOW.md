## Company Overview

ServiceNow is an enterprise software platform that automates and digitizes complex business workflows across entire organizations. Often described as an "AI Control Tower for Business," it provides a unified architecture connecting IT, HR, and customer service departments through a "system of action."

**Products:**
- IT Service Management (ITSM)
- Customer Service Management (CSM)
- HR Service Delivery
- Creator Workflows (App Engine — low-code development)
- Pro Plus (AI agents tier — autonomous request resolution)
- Workflow Data Fabric (connects cloud environments and internal data silos)

**Financial profile (as of 2026):**
- Market cap: >\$90 billion
- Revenue: +20% YoY growth; another 20% forecast for 2026
- Free cash flow: 36% margin
- Cash: \$6 billion; Debt: \$1.5 billion (down 60% in 5 years)
- Shareholder equity: \$13 billion
- Customers: ~8,700, including 85% of Fortune 500
- 88% institutional ownership; ~11% retail

**AI / Agentic capabilities (2026):**
- Transitioned from "tracking work" to "doing work" — AI agents resolve IT/HR issues autonomously
- Partnerships: Anthropic (Claude as default model for Build Agents; deployed to 29,000+ employees) and OpenAI (preferred model for agentic use cases and voice capabilities in CRM)
- Gartner named Leader for Business Orchestration and Automation Technologies ("Slope of Enlightenment")
- Pro Plus (AI agents tier): ~50% of new deals up from 30% last year; ~100% of pilots convert to full rollouts

## Investment Thesis (Apr 2026)

Stephen's thesis: "Smart money buying what retail is dumping." Key points:

1. **Retail panic, institutional buying**: Robinhood cut holdings 54% (retail panic). IFP Advisors +146%, Yarborough Capital +155%, Prospera +131%. CEO Bill McDermott personally bought \$3M in shares Feb 27, then signed a new 5-year contract. Company is 88% institutionally owned.

2. **Price dislocation**: Stock fell 66% from \$240 (Jan 2025) to \$82 low (Apr 2026). Stephen views this as temporary mispricing driven by AI fears and general market pullback.

3. **Bear case rebuttal**: (a) "AI will kill software" — wrong; ServiceNow *uses* AI, it's an orchestration layer that AI depends on; (b) Seat-based pricing headwind — NOW pivoting to hybrid model including AI agents, devices, and AI assists (TAM expands from millions of seats to billions of devices/agents); (c) M&A concerns — acquisitions are complementary "tuck-in" tech, CEO says no further substantial deals needed.

4. **Technology moat**: Owns 80 billion annual workflows giving operational context that generic LLMs cannot replicate. Gartner "Slope of Enlightenment" positioning. 30% efficiency gains for customers using AI virtual agents.

5. **Competitive position**: Three-way battle with Salesforce and Microsoft for AI agent platform dominance. ServiceNow's advantage is workflow ownership and governance; starting to erode Salesforce's mid-market CRM share.

**Out of character for Stephen:** "It is out of my normal comfort zone, it's a big company with positive cash flow and a fortress balance sheet." Comparison to META/MSFT positions in 2023.
