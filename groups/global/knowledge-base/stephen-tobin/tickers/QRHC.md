## Company Overview

Quest Resource Holding operates an asset-light, end-to-end waste, recycling and sustainability platform positioned between vertically-integrated landfill majors and thousands of single-stream local haulers. Stephen described it as "a picks and shovels play for new recycling technology."

**Business model:**

- Uses a network of 3,500 vendors, 25,000 trucks, and 1,000 recycling facilities — on-demand capacity without owning hard assets
- Handles 100+ solid and liquid waste streams (cardboard, metals, used oil, hazardous waste, etc.)
- Offers seven bundled "national solutions" covering every US zip code plus Canada and Puerto Rico
- Designs bespoke waste management systems for large multi-site corporations, guaranteeing maximum recycling
- Cloud ERP and portal provides zero-touch ESG reporting meeting EPA standards

**Market position:** Market cap under \$50 million at purchase. Serves 90,000 client locations. Revenue ~\$253M annualized (Q3 2025). Targeting Fortune 1000 customers with multi-site, complex waste footprints.

**Revenue and financial data:**

- Q3 2025: Gross profit \$11.5M, operating cash flow \$5.7M
- Debt reduced by \$11.2M YTD (14% YoY reduction)
- New clients contributed over \$24M incremental YoY revenue
- Stephen's forecasts: 2025 revenue \$255M, Adj. EBITDA \$10M; 2026 revenue \$270M, Adj. EBITDA \$12M
- FCF breakeven expected 2H 2026

**Transformation:** Underwent fundamental operational transformation 2023-2025, evolving from a struggling waste broker into a technology-enabled, nationally-scaled managed-waste platform. New CEO Perry Moss (30+ years industry experience) appointed March 2025. Debt refinanced December 2024, reducing blended interest rate by 150bps.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Dec 2025, Trade Alert #88):** "Picks and shovels" play for the recycling sector. Large multi-site corporations face an administrative nightmare getting each waste stream to suitable recyclers — Quest bridges this problem with technology. "Land-and-expand" strategy proven effective: 8 new customer wins expected to generate 7+ figures each annually, plus 5 expansion agreements with largest existing clients. Near EBITDA breakeven. Could be acquired by one of the integrated majors, or build a strong, profitable, recurring-revenue service business.

**Concerns noted at purchase:** Market cap under \$50M (true micro cap), large drawdown possible. Half-size position (\$250) due to risk profile. Management guidance for gross profit "flat or slightly decrease."

**Exit thesis (Mar 2026):** Identified as "too exposed" in oil-price-shock review. "Fully dependent on consumer spending at retail parks, costs linked to oil." Lackluster operating report — volumes soft across all verticals. Closed at -24% loss as part of portfolio rotation away from stocks vulnerable to Middle East crisis.
