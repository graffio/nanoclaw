**An Investment Thesis for MDA Space**

MDA Space presents a compelling, albeit volatile, investment opportunity as a vertically integrated leader in the high-growth space, defense, and communications markets. While the stock has recently pulled back due to contract uncertainty, the core business fundamentals—driven by proven technology, a strong backlog, and a unique competitive position—remain robust, offering an attractive entry point.

**Compelling Investment Strengths**

**1\. Diverse, High-Growth Product Portfolio**

MDA’s revenue is generated across three synergistic and growing segments:

-   **Satellite Systems:** Manufacturing advanced, software-defined satellites for Low, Medium, and Geostationary Earth Orbits. This division is pioneering high-volume contract manufacturing, a unique capability that differentiates MDA from its competition and is critical in the current “space race to revenue.” MDA is building new facilities to give it capacity to build 2 satellites a day, that is unheard of and unmatched capacity.
    
-   **Space Robotics (Canadarm):** The company holds an indispensable legacy in space, having built the robotic arms for the Space Shuttle and International Space Station (Canadarm1 and Canadarm2, plus the Dextre robot). Its next-generation Canadarm3, an autonomous AI-driven robot funded by NASA for the Gateway program ($1.8 billion contract), cements its long-term role in space exploration.
    
-   **Geointelligence:** Operates the RADARSAT-2 and is launching the CHORUS system in 2026, providing high-precision radar imagery for defense and intelligence customers. This division is expanding into satellite tracking and counter-space defense systems, leveraging MDA’s 40 years of operational history.
    

**2\. Strong Financial and Operational Progress**

-   **Financial Outlook:** Management forecasts $1.6 billion in revenue for 2025 (48% year-over-year growth) and EBITDA forecast to rise 45% to $312 million. Cash flow is expected to be neutral to positive despite significant investment in infrastructure.
    
-   **Order Backlog and Capacity:** MDA has a solid current backlog of **$4 billion**, which is enough to keep its operations fully utilized for approximately three years. The company is actively doubling the capacity of its advanced satellite manufacturing site in Montreal to meet the anticipated demand for large constellations, driven by new investment cycles from NATO countries viewing space as a critical defense domain.
    
-   **Technological Milestones:** The company is progressing on major projects, including the detailed engineering and manufacturing preparation for the Telesat Lightspeed program. In the crucial Globalstar contract, the engineering team has achieved key, industry-first milestones, demonstrating “digital beam forming” and multiple beam steering on the MDA Aurora satellite, affirming a technological advantage. Furthermore, MDA is providing antennas for a 126-satellite LEO constellation for the US military.
    

**Balanced View and Contract Risks**

The share price has been spooked by two recent contract-related events, both involving a competitor, SpaceX. While concerning, these risks appear to be either mitigated or overstated:

-   **EchoStar Contract Cancellation:** The $1.3 billion EchoStar LEO constellation contract was canceled after EchoStar sold its spectrum rights to SpaceX. While a hit to the order book, the early cancellation is arguably a **blessing**. Given EchoStar’s SECD filing reported “Substantial doubt... about our ability to continue as a going concern,” a late cancellation would have been far more financially damaging to MDA. The company will be compensated as per the contract terms. It has led to accusations of insider trading around the news release, but these class actions rarely lead to anything.
    
-   **Globalstar Acquisition Rumors:** Rumors of Globalstar actively exploring a sale to SpaceX caused the second leg down in the stock. However, I consider a cancellation of MDA’s contract unlikely:
    
    -   The contract is financially backed by **Apple**, who has invested approximately $2 billion in Globalstar’s infrastructure and holds 20% of its equity.
        
    -   The Globalstar project is in an advanced stage, with engineering work complete and 9 spacecraft on the shop floor, approaching launch dates—a far more committed stage than the EchoStar contract was at cancellation.
        

## Other News

This month, MDA announced its first contract to deploy RADARSAT+, the next-generation radar-imaging satellite technology. It could be worth an additional $500 million in the long term, but the first confirmed phase is $50m. I don’t think this has been priced in at all. This is part of the geointelligence division, MDA’s highest margin segment providing radar imaging for defence and inteligence customers.

## Forecast

I have started a mathematical model, but we need to see the pace of new satellite system acquisitions to model growth from 2028 onward. The recent RADARSAT+ award is important, and more deals like this, which I consider very likely, would provide a material uplift to the forecast.

**Conclusion**

The recent stock pullback, driven by perceived risk of further SpaceX contract losses, presents a buying opportunity at a discount. The underlying company is exhibiting high growth (48% YoY revenue expected), holds a deep backlog, is expanding its unique manufacturing capacity, and continues to make critical technological progress on its major programs. The investment thesis is that the market’s reaction to the contract issues has been overblown, failing to account for the company’s financial compensation, its critical technology portfolio, and the unlikelihood of Apple allowing the Globalstar contract to fail. MDA is poised for significant future revenue and EBITDA expansion.
