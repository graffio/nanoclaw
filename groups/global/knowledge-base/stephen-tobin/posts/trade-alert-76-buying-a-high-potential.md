**Core Business Model & Value Proposition**

Volatus is a drone integrator, operator, and manufacturer. It provides drones as a service using its in-house manufactured drones as well as those bought from other OEMs. It provides comprehensive solutions for inspecting everything from pipelines to border walls, offering the correct hardware in conjunction with its own software, operational expertise, and regulatory approval. Volatus has a control center in Toronto with 20 operators controlling remote operations throughout North America with the majority of drone missions flying autonomously.

Unlike standard aerospace companies that focus exclusively on either traditional aviation or drone operations, Volatus employs a **dual-aircraft strategy**. The company maintains both Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems (RPAS) Operating Certificates for commercial drone operations and two Air Operator Certificates for piloted airplanes and helicopters.

The company has shifted from a manufacturer model, where equipment sales accounted for approximately 75% of revenue two years ago, to a services-first approach, with services now contributing 60-70% of total revenue.

This transformation, brought about by a 2024 merger, has diversified the revenue base and improved gross margins, as services typically generate margins of 40-50%.

Volatus’s five primary business lines include **Aerial Intelligence Services** (inspection, mapping, surveillance, data collection with GIS, data management, and machine learning analytics), **Cargo and Logistics Solutions** (RPAS-based cargo transportation), **Equipment Sales and Maintenance** (value-added reseller with integration, customization, and post-sales support), **Training and Certification** (a high-margin revenue stream with over 114,000 students trained worldwide), and **Consulting Services** (advisory support for RPAS program development and scaling).

The competitive environment varies significantly across Volatus’s business segments. In **Commercial Distribution and Drones-as-a-Service**, the market remains **highly fragmented, with multiple smaller players** dominated by regional operators that have limited geographic presence and expertise. This will be the key market in the mid-term and the main reason for my investment.

### Key Recent News

In June, Volatus announced a contract to provide a bi-weekly aerial inspection of a gas pipeline that extends from Texas to the northern states of the US. Guidance was given for revenue of $0.5 million per year. This recurring revenue drone-as-a-service contract is key for Volatus in the future. It has very little competition in this market, with the scale and operational capability to cover this type of distance, as well as the certification to fly in the required geographies.

This contract builds on several that have established Volatus as the leader in this market sector.

It has a multi-year contract with a power utility to provide mapping and inspection services along 100,000 miles of distribution lines (contract size $15 million over 3 years, 2-year extension option in place). I view this as a real proof point and opens the door to multi-year high-margin, high-value recurring revenue.

In Canada, it has a contract to deploy nationwide and inspect agricultural land for plant health and crop performance.

Volatos has been approved for Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations across Canada, a unique achievement, the approval is for multiple aircraft types and includes night time flying and the ability to fly within 100 feet of towers and other vertical structures.

This means Volatus can scale its operations by using a single hub to direct flights, as it does not require operators on the ground, thereby unlocking key markets such as border security, environmental monitoring, inspection services, and cargo delivery. It significantly reduces cost and means larger companies can access nationwide services.

Volatus intends to supply nested drones in a box to maximize the full potential of this market. Volatus manufactures its own drone-in-a-box but has signed important agreements with companies like Ondas, which manufacture this type of system. Ondas will receive a one-time purchase price, but Volatus will receive years' worth of repeat service business on top of the margin from the sale.

### **Proprietary Technology Portfolio**

The Canary RPAS is a next-generation small cargo drone for efficient delivery operations, approved for Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations. DroneSpot™ is a proprietary and patented infrastructure solution for efficient and secure drone delivery operations, with commercialization as a standalone product beginning in Q3 2025.

The AIRS 3 (Advanced Integrity Reporting System) is a proprietary software platform for data gathering, analysis, and reporting for oil and gas client infrastructures, integrating aerial inspection data with advanced reporting and analytics tools. Additional proprietary technologies being commercialized include FLYTE and Aeriport systems.

Volatus’s “Vetted by Volatus” program integrates complementary drone technologies to offer comprehensive solutions, making the company a one-stop provider. They integrate technologies from over 60 OEMs. Recent partnerships include **Ondas** Holdings for border surveillance, **RigiTech** for a long-range delivery drone (100 km range, 3 kg payload), and **DroneUp** for advanced cargo services in North America. Volatus also integrates drones from the world's leading low-cost Chinese manufacturers like DJI Although this may not be advantageous in military applications, it can make a huge difference in the commercial world, where price beats politics.

### **Operational Differentiators**

Volatus’s Operations Control Center (OCC) is a key differentiator, providing secure, global BVLOS oversight for scalable remote operations. Management noted its role in responding to RFPs, calling it a “game changer.”

With over 75,000 cumulative flight hours, the company demonstrates operational maturity and client trust. Volatus has also secured multiple Special Flight Operating Certificates (SFOCs) from Transport Canada for complex BVLOS operations in urban environments, enabling scalable operations without visual observers.

### Growth Plans

Geographic and market expansion plans have been announced, with a focus on Africa, and Volatus Aerospace Europe S.A. has been launched.

Management is primarily focused on converting its $600 million sales pipeline into recurring, long-term contracts. CEO Glen Lynch emphasized this, highlighting a strategy that prioritizes recurring revenue streams.

Volatus is leveraging its abilities and scale to target market sectors where autonomy, security, and reliability are key differentiators. They are having the greatest success in remote, difficult to access areas where their certifications and drone-in-a-box technology are of great value.

Volatus maintains an active acquisition strategy. Lynch stated they are actively reviewing opportunities where acquisitions could add technology, customer channels, or geographic reach.

## Born By Merger

Volatus’s biggest transaction was the $31.5 million merger of equals with Drone Delivery Canada Corp. on August 30, 2024. Under the arrangement, Drone Delivery Canada issued 1.785 shares for each Volatus Aerospace Corp. share, with 224.2 million shares issued at $0.14 per share.

Management achieved $3.77 million in annualized cost synergies ahead of the original 12-month timeline. The strategic rationale centered on unlocking Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations and expanding the addressable market to $4 billion by 2030.

**Complementary Acquisitions**

**UAV Hub and Drone Mentor** were acquired in January 2024, Volatus has increased its stake in **Synergy Aviation Ltd. to 58.47%**, bringing UK-based drone capabilities.

### Finances

A red flag was the Ontario Securities Commission cease trade order (CTO) against Volatus for failure to file its 2023 annual filings. This was pre-merger, and it was revoked a few days later.

Volatus does not have any institutional investors, with the general public owning 77% of the stock and insiders 23%. This will lead to greater volatility but might reduce management’s appetite for dilution.

Volatus reported $10.59 million in revenue for Q2 2025, representing a 49% year-over-year increase.

H1 2025 revenue was $16.3 million, up 19% year-over-year. I think $45 million of revenue is likely in 2025, and $35 million of that will be repeat revenue, allowing Volatus to scale quickly in the coming years

A key driver of recent performance has been the strategic shift in revenue composition. Sevices are repeat high margin revenue, equipment is a lower margin one of sales.

Large military orders have made the mix less favourable. Equipment sales surged 104% quarter-over-quarter and 114% year-over-year in Q2 2025, driven by defense market demand and NATO-related procurement activities. This resulted in a revenue mix of 48% equipment sales and 52% services in Q2 2025, compared to 33% equipment and 67% services in the prior year period.

Volatus maintained gross margins at 32% in Q2 2025, consistent with Q1 2025 levels. The company achieved an 85% improvement in Adjusted EBITDA, reducing the loss from $1.85 million in Q2 2024 to $0.3 million in Q2 2025.

I believe domestic and NATO military contracts will show meaningful growth in 2026. Volatus recently delivered tactical “Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance” (ISR) drones to NATO countries under two contracts totalling more than $4 million, it positions Volatus for upcoming military spending growth.

The Balance Sheet

Volatus has shareholder equity of $30 million but total debt of $20 million. Cash on hand is $6 million, providing a roughly 6-month cash runway.

The company’s financial position improved materially during the first half of 2025. Total assets increased to $60.6 million as of June 30, 2025, from $57.8 million at December 31, 2024.

Shareholder equity transformed from a deficit of ($2.5 million) at year-end 2024 to a positive $30 million by June 2025.

Cash and cash equivalents strengthened to $6.1 million from $1.6 million end 2024. This improvement was helkped by $10 million in equity and convertible debt financing completed during Q2.

A large Canadian government order would anchor the balance sheet and make raising non dilutive capital a viable option. The Canadian government has a priority for spending on domestic suppliers and is expected to invest heavily in drone technology. I am forecasting a significant Canadian government order in Q1 2026

The current liquidity might be sufficient to fund the company through 2025 and it might hit breakeven in 2026.

## Conclusion

Finding a drone company offering good value is difficult but Volatus appears to be that, we will have to watch the rest of the industry. The sector could fall back if some of the overextended moves start to falter.

I like the Drone As A Service model for long-term recurring revenue and Volatus has achieved a scale that few others can match (if any).

The military sales will likely generate additional revenue over the next couple of years, and the extra profit will help them expand the DAAS infrastructure and reduce costs through economies of scale.

I also like the integration of other manufacturers into their offering, ensuring they can design systems to meet the needs of customers. No drone company can hope to build every type of drone.

Volatus has concentrated on manufacturing drones in the part of the sector with the least competition. Its drone delivery service in hard to reach areas will likely be a growth area in the future with medical supplies the most immediate use case.
