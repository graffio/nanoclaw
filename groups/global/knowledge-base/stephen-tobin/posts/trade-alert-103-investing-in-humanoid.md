# Trade Alert #103: Investing In Humanoid Robots

*Possibly the largest emerging technology of the decade*

I published a primer on [this investment in the weekly review,](https://open.substack.com/pub/stephentobin/p/sw-weekly-humanoid-robotic-opportunites?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web) it is worth reading if you have not done so already.

It is important to understand what we mean by a Humanoid; it's not a two-legged, two-armed human look-alike, but a robot that can perform jobs currently only available to humans.

Amazon packing is a good example: each order must be packed into a suitable delivery box, with the remaining space filled with filler. At the moment, this is done by humans who stand at a packing station with all the different boxes, and each order arrives already picked on a conveyor belt. Amazon is going to replace these people with an 8-armed machine fixed to the floor, powered by a constant supply of electricity. It won’t walk or talk but it will use its eight arms to quickly and efficiently put orders into boxes, affix the delivery label, tape up the box and do it 24 hours a day 7 days a week. It will never need a break, and its 8 arms will enable it to work far quicker than a human.

It is the actuators that make these arms capable of picking and not breaking the products going into packing boxes, and they will represent 50% of the BOM of these robots.

One technology dominates actuators: Strain Weave gearing, often called Harmonic Drive and first brought to market by the Japanese company Harmonic Drive Systems \[HDS\]. No other technology has a proven ability to do this, and Strain Weave Gearing has a near 100% share of the market. The gearing has extreme precision, high torque, and zero backlash (that’s wiggle to you and me).

Harmonic Drive Systems had a monopoly on this technology and is still the world leader; its products are by far the best available. In a chat with a Tesla robot engineer, I learned that with Harmonic Drive Systems, you get exactly what is expected; the product is perfect, made with the best materials to the highest standards. If you are building a robot to perform surgery, then you have no other option but to buy from Harmonic Drive Systems.

In the latest earnings report, Harmonic Drive's management revealed that sales to China had dropped 26%, and they forecast further declines as Chinese robot manufacturers turned to a domestic supplier. China is currently the largest market for these robots with multiple companies manufacturing them and widescale adoption of the technology for manufacturing.

That domestic Chinese supplier is Leader Harmonious Drive Systems, commonly called LeaderDrive. Unfortunately for HDS, its patents have long expired, and LeaderDrive has copied its devices and developed sufficient manufacturing capacity to produce products good enough for most use cases, especially for the Amazon packing example.

This is a typical Incumbent v Challenger story. HDS offers the best strain-weave gearing available, and they are expanding their product line. LeaderDrive makes products that are good enough in most instances, but crucially, they are between 30% and 50% cheaper.

So, which is the most sensible company to Buy? Obviously, the one that will make us the most cash with the smallest risk.

The short answer is neither; both HDS and LeaderDrive are incredibly expensive right now. The dominance they have in actuators is no secret and the market has already built future growth into their share prices.

There are issues with execution. HDS will continue to slowly grow its reach and market, along with its manufacturing base, it cannot scale up quickly as the knowledge base does not exist. LeaderDrive is based in China, and with current geopolitical risks, that is more than a headache. The winner needs the American market as well as the big markets in China and Europe.

## Minth is Glocal

Every few years, a manufacturing strategy emerges that gives companies a notable competitive advantage. When I was in business school, it was Just-In-Time manufacturing. Since then, there have been many others. Today, it is Glocal- Globally producing products local to customers. The strategy removes supply chain risk, tariff risk, and benefits from the wave of buy-local government initiatives sweeping the world. Minth has built local manufacturing footprints in North America, Europe and throughout Asia. 78 factories in total, every one of them is local and together they are global.

Minth Group operates as a global Tier-1 automotive supplier, primarily engaged in the research, development, production, and sales of auto parts, toolings, and moulds. The company’s core auto part operations are structured into four distinct product business units: metal and trim products, plastic products, aluminum products, and battery housing products. Hence, the reason to close APTV.

## Minth The future

Minth is actively trying to generate a second growth wave by moving into growing markets. They have targeted battery housing, eVTOL, AI liquid cooling, and most importantly for this investment, robotics.

## The Robot JVs

1.  **Leader Drive**
    

In February, Minth announced a JV with leaderDrive to use its manufacturing base to produce gears and actuators for the North American market. The JV will be 60% owned by Minth and produce all LeaderDrive products for North and South America.

2.  **AgiBot**
    

AgiBot is a Chinese robot manufacturer and the first to achieve scale, with 5,000 humanoids produced last year. Last week, Minth announced it will be AgiBots' OEM partner in Europe, assembling, manufacturing, and selling AgiBot robots in the European market. AgiBot robots will be deployed in all 78 Minth factories (and they use leaderDrive actuators that will be made by Minth).

**Minth Competitive advantages**

Minth’s competitive edge stems from proprietary material formulations in its aluminum and plastics divisions. Key developments include high-performance aluminum alloys: Minal-S748 (500Mpa ultra-high yield strength, superior collision resistance), Minal-S636 (360Mpa yield strength), and Minal-S632 (320MPa yield strength). The latter two passed advanced global vehicle crash performance tests.

Minth focuses on lightweighting and carbon reduction in polymer and composite materials. Using proprietary VarinTech, they create composite materials for many areas of an automobile.

They have also successfully mass-produced green materials like EcoSupElast elastomers and EcoOleCom green plastics, achieving a total carbon emission reduction exceeding 36.00%.

Minth leverages its materials expertise to develop intelligent exterior decorative products, including highly integrated front modules for L4+ autonomous driving that incorporate illumination, heating, wave transparency, and automatic cleaning. The company also mass-produces LiDAR-compatible radomes, flush door handles, and illuminated emblems for top technology and NEV companies.

**Battery Housing**

Minth is a market leader in battery housing for Electric Vehicles (EVs), with its battery housing division emerging as a core business segment. This growth is underpinned by key innovations in processes such as roll-forming, high-frequency welding, and aluminum extrusion for battery cell housings. The segment grew 50% YoY and now accounts for almost 30% of total revenue. Management has guided this sector for enormous growth increasing to 1 million units by 2030.

Minth’s solutions utilize multi-materials to adhere to rigorous “cell-to-body” (CTB) standards and advanced battery safety specifications.

Management is targeting a 33% Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) for this unit and already supplies 8 of Europe’s top 15 selling EV models. By 2030, management projects that battery housing will account for 40% of Minth’s total sales.

The company plans to leverage this expertise and product knowledge to develop specialized, high-protection “torsos” for humanoid robots.

**AI Liquid Cooling**

There is a strong technological similarity between NEV battery thermal management and AI liquid cooling systems and the Minth advanced technology is directly applicable. The migration of this technology is already showing success, with Minth receiving orders from a Taiwan-based AI server manufacturer for immersion liquid cooling cabinets and from a liquid cooling system provider for water distributors, thereby integrating Minth into a global semiconductor end user.

**Lightweight Structures**

Minth has world-leading knowledge of materials science and advanced manufacturing capabilities. It has signed an agreement with Ehang to produce lightweight, high-safety airframes and propellers.

In robots, Minth has announced plans to produce electronic skin, intelligent masks, limb structures, and wireless charging systems for humanoid robots using its advanced material abilities.

**Technological Moat**

Unlike HDS, Minth has carefully designed its patent protection. Its products are heavily protected by 60 core patent families related to material formulations and processing technologies. In H2 2024 it filled an additional 168 new global patents, currently it has licensed 242 patents and has established a patent pool where it frequently conducts patent right defense analysis to protect its interests.

**Finances**

Unlike most of my investments, Minth has rock-solid financials. Shareholder equity is over $3 billion, net debt to equity is around 20% and with peak CAPEX passed (reduced 17% last year) we can expect this figure to fall. The company generated positive free cash flow of $270 million last year, and my model did not turn up a single financial concern.

The current Auto OEM business is doing very well despite a challenging environment.

## Conclusion

Minth has the factories, the cash, and the technical knowledge to provide a large part of the mechanical and physical components of future humanoid robots. The management team has built a successful, profitable business by applying its materials science expertise and has shown foresight in implementing a Glocal policy.

Even more impressive, this management team has identified an opportunity to create a second growth curve and has moved quickly to establish agreements with dominant robot companies.

The share price has risen a lot over the last year but has pulled back, presenting this entry opportunity. Of course, it could fall back much more; I would not be particularly concerned if it fell to HK$30.

The potential for a substantial re-rating if the thesis plays out, and the current market cap of US$5.7 bn, make this a compelling investment in my view.
