## Company Overview

Minth Group is a global Tier-1 automotive supplier headquartered in China, primarily engaged in research, development, production, and sales of auto parts, toolings, and moulds. The company operates 78 factories worldwide under a "Glocal" strategy — globally producing products local to customers, removing supply chain risk, tariff risk, and benefiting from buy-local government initiatives.

**Core business units:**

- Metal and trim products (21.6% of H1 2025 revenue)
- Plastic products (23.3%)
- Aluminum products (20.1%)
- Battery housing products (29.2%, up from 21.6% YoY — growing at 50% YoY, targeting 33% CAGR)

**Revenue:** Total H1 2025 revenue RMB 12.3 billion, up from RMB 11.1 billion in H1 2024.

**Battery housing:** Market leader for EV battery housings, supplying 8 of Europe's top 15 selling EV models. Management targets 1 million units by 2030 and projects battery housing will account for 40% of total sales. Revenue roadmap: RMB 0.3B (2021) to RMB 5.3B (2024) to RMB 30B (2030 target).

**New growth verticals (the "second growth curve"):**

- **Humanoid robotics** — JVs with LeaderDrive (actuators/gearing for North America, 60% Minth ownership) and AgiBot (OEM partner for European assembly/sales of humanoid robots)
- **AI liquid cooling** — Technology transfer from EV battery thermal management; orders from Taiwan-based AI server manufacturer
- **eVTOL** — Agreement with EHang to produce lightweight airframes and propellers
- **Robot components** — Electronic skin, intelligent masks, limb structures, wireless charging systems

**Financials:** Rock-solid balance sheet. Shareholder equity over \$3 billion. Net debt to equity ~20%. Peak CAPEX passed (reduced 17% last year). Positive free cash flow of \$270 million in latest year.

**Technology moat:** 60 core patent families related to material formulations and processing. 168 new global patents filed in H2 2024; 242 licensed patents total. Proprietary high-performance aluminum alloys (Minal-S748 at 500MPa, S636 at 360MPa, S632 at 320MPa). Proprietary VarinTech composite materials. Green materials including EcoSupElast elastomers and EcoOleCom plastics achieving 36% carbon emission reduction.

## Competitive Position

Stephen chose Minth over the direct actuator/gearing companies (Harmonic Drive Systems, LeaderDrive, Sanhua Intelligent, Nabtesco) because those are already priced for the robot boom.

**P/E comparison:**

- Harmonic Drive Systems (TYO: 6324): ~86x-296x
- LeaderDrive (SHA: 688017): ~115x-440x
- Sanhua Intelligent (SHE: 002050): ~49x-55x
- Nabtesco Corp (TYO: 6268): ~33x-43x
- **Minth Group (HK: 0425): ~13x-15x**

**Key insight:** Minth is not yet priced as a robotics company. If the robot deals generate substantial revenue, a re-rating from ~13x P/E toward the 40-100x range of pure-play actuator companies could deliver enormous upside. Management has described 2026 as "the inflection year when they break out of their traditional business into an AI/Robot infrastructure play."

**LeaderDrive relationship:** LeaderDrive is the low-cost Chinese alternative to Harmonic Drive Systems. Products are 30-50% cheaper, "good enough" for most use cases. Minth's JV will manufacture all LeaderDrive products for North and South America.

**AgiBot relationship:** AgiBot is the first Chinese robot manufacturer to achieve scale (5,000 humanoids produced in 2025). Minth will be AgiBot's OEM partner in Europe, and AgiBot robots use LeaderDrive actuators that will be made by Minth. AgiBot robots will also be deployed across all 78 Minth factories.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Mar 2026, Trade Alert #103):** The humanoid robotics market (TAM \$15B by 2030, \$9 trillion by 2050) hinges on actuators, which represent 50% of robot BOM. Strain wave gearing dominates actuators with near 100% market share. Minth has established JVs with both the leading Chinese gearing manufacturer (LeaderDrive) and the leading Chinese robot producer (AgiBot), giving it a unique position to supply critical components globally — all while trading at a fraction of the P/E of pure-play competitors.

**Financial model:** Stephen's initial forecast projects revenue from RMB 23.1B (2024 actual) to RMB 62.1B by 2030, with profit before tax rising from RMB 2.8B to RMB 13.2B. He notes this is "very conservative" — it excludes AgiBot partnership revenue and uses only RMB 10M from LeaderDrive by 2030. "There is a real possibility that this revenue forecast could be off by a factor of 5 if the robot market takes off as predicted."

**Rotation rationale:** Stephen closed his APTV (Aptiv PLC) position to fund Minth, as both serve the auto parts market. "Minth has the Robot market, whereas APTV appears to have lost the Radar advantage, which was the thesis for the trade."

**Oil risk assessment (Mar 2026):** Short-term risk: Medium. Medium-term risk: Low. "Hesai and Minth both supply the auto makers, but so far their home markets in China seem to be holding up well, and both have substantial shares in EVs, which might see a boost."
