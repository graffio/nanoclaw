## Company Overview

Airship AI Holdings, originally JDL Digital Systems (founded 2003), went public in December 2023 via a SPAC merger. The company specializes in edge AI data structuring — using machine learning on sensor data at the source to create searchable databases, eliminating the need for central data centers.

**Products:**

- Outpost AI — purpose-built edge computing hardware that structures unstructured video data (vehicle images, license plates, make/model/colour, locations) locally, even with low bandwidth
- Acropolis — enterprise video and data management platform (Commercial and Law variants) supporting hundreds of thousands of devices through a single GUI
- Airship Command — visualization and control platform for security operations centers and field personnel ("single pane of glass")
- Supporting services — custom AI model training, on-site training, engineering, remote monitoring

**Technology position:** AISP is sensor agnostic — works with any input from any sensor (existing security cams, traffic cams, Ring doorbells, robot-mounted LiDAR, drone-mounted radar). The system enables natural language queries against video data: "show me all red cars passing this point" or "alert me if a human approaches this fence." Edge processing means it works without internet connectivity, making it cost-effective to scale.

**Revenue model:** Hardware product sales (Outpost AI) plus recurring software/support subscriptions. The bull case rests on growing the recurring support revenue over time.

- FY2024: \$23.1M revenue (+87% YoY from \$12.3M)
- FY2025: revenue fell ~33% YoY due to US government headwinds (shutdown, budget uncertainty)
- Q4 FY2025: \$6.5M revenue (+102% YoY), 51.2% gross margin, cash-flow positive
- Q2 FY2025 (reported Aug 2025): \$2.1M revenue, 71% gross margin — significant miss
- Cash: ~\$8.8M (Mar 2025), expected over \$10M at year-end 2025
- No debt on balance sheet (as of mid-2025)
- Validated pipeline: \$128-166M (record), expected conversion over 18-24 months

**Key customers/deals:**

- US Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and Customs and Border Protection (CBP) — approved vendor
- 16 individual contracts from DOJ and DHS agencies
- Fortune 100 transportation and e-commerce company (seven-figure follow-on contracts, Oct 2024 and Jan 2025)
- Historical commercial customers include FedEx and Home Depot
- Working with prime contractors on CBP Smart Wall (\$4.5B in new contracts, per Mar 2026 earnings)

**Government spending tailwinds:** Over \$70B allocated for DHS/CBP over four years, including \$6.2B for border security technology and \$51M for autonomous towers in 2025 appropriations.

## Competitive Position

Stephen's view evolved from bullish to increasingly concerned over the holding period.

**Initial assessment (Jun 2025):** "A clear, strong buy; it has a wide technical and commercial moat, enough cash to reach break-even, and strong visibility around its revenue trajectory." Operating within a \$7B edge AI hardware and software addressable market, differentiated from traditional surveillance competitors (Avigilon, Milestone Systems, Verkada) through specialized edge AI data management.

**Key competitive advantages:** 19-year operational history, deep domain expertise, established relationships with federal/military/intelligence agencies creating barriers to entry (security clearances, compliance requirements, track record).

**Erosion of moat (Mar 2026):** "I worry they have lost their technical advantage. I have been looking again and a couple of companies having competing tech being sold to integrators so I think the opportunity may have been missed by AISP." Stephen identified silicon chip manufacturers putting similar features onto chips, allowing integrators to develop comparable feature sets at a fraction of the cost.

**Market concerns:** AI in video surveillance market valued at \$3.9B in 2024, projected to reach \$12.46B by 2030 (21.3% CAGR). However, AISP's customer concentration remained high — in 2023, three customers accounted for 67% of sales, with the top customer at 34%.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Jun 2025, Trade Alert #54):** First investment in the robot/AI security sector. AISP had great technology in a market with huge potential, first-mover advantage, good financial position, break-even in the forecast horizon, proven repeat business, and new product development opening new verticals. Stephen's Seeking Alpha coverage from March 2025 rated it a Buy at \$3.36 with fair value of \$5.96. By June, fair value had nearly tripled to \$17, driven by faster-than-anticipated growth, approaching cash generation two years earlier than forecast. 80 shares, \$405 position (full-size at the time). Target: 100% return within 12 months.

**Q2 2025 earnings reassessment (Aug 2025, chat):** Revenue miss at \$2.1M — product sales dropped from \$5M to \$0.7M YoY, but recurring support revenue grew 40%. Pipeline at \$128M (record). "The revenue figure missed expectations... looks like some seasonality." Created first fair value of "a little over \$10." Decided NOT to add due to "serious negative equity situation with assets of \$10M and liabilities of \$60M. Cash and equivalents stand at \$6M, but cash burn is around \$2M a quarter. Less than a year of cash and negative equity means one thing: dilution."

**Add thesis (Jan 2026, Trade Alert #95):** Added half-size (\$300) after Q4 2025 showed 102% YoY revenue growth and 15% price jump. "The product was making real progress with the US government before the shutdown and reassessment of priorities. With these issues now resolved, I think AISP is set for a breakout year." Updated DCF valuation: \$12/share (10% discount rate, Gordon Growth terminal value). Insider buying noted — President Paul Allen purchased 100,000 shares at \$2.74, CEO Victor Huang purchased 20,000 warrants.

**Thesis deterioration (Feb-Mar 2026):** "Fantastic product but the company has not made enough progress." Stephen identified on-chip AI competitors eroding AISP's technological moat. The US administration's overseas military actions (Iran war) went against the isolationist/border-security thesis. Government agency budgets not confirmed for 2026, with "enormous spending on overseas military action likely reduces the possibility of large spending on domestic security."

**Exit thesis (Mar 2026):** Exited as part of broader portfolio repositioning due to Middle East escalation (Israel-Iran strikes on gas infrastructure). AISP identified as one of four stocks with "major concern" — demand likely to decline as both commercial customers slow purchases and US government redirects spending from border to war.

**Risks that materialized:**

- Customer concentration on US government agencies whose budgets were redirected
- Competitive moat erosion from on-chip AI solutions
- Failure to convert pipeline into revenue
- Opacity of government contracts making thesis validation difficult
- Dilution risk from negative equity position
