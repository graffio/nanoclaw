## Company Overview

ASP Isotopes is a South African-based isotope enrichment company developing proprietary technology to displace legacy gas-centrifuge incumbents on cost and flexibility. The company operates a dual-technology enrichment architecture: Aerodynamic Separation Process (ASP) for lighter isotopes and laser-based Quantum Enrichment (QE) for heavier ones.

**Technology:**

- ASP technology — derived from South Africa's 1980s enrichment program via acquisition of Klydon. Modular units built locally for under \$3M each vs \$1B+ for centrifuge facilities. No moving parts. Enriches Carbon-14, Silicon-28, and other lighter isotopes.
- Quantum Enrichment (QE) — updated version of older AVLIS laser method using new-specification lasers and enhanced beam-forming. Currently enriching Ytterbium-176. Lab-scale tests promising for uranium enrichment.

**Products / isotopes in production:**

- Ytterbium-176 — precursor for Lutetium-177 used in Pluvicto (prostate cancer radiotherapy). Order for 1kg/year at \$20,000/gram. Rosatom has been the dominant supplier.
- Carbon-14 — multi-year deal with Canadian company, minimum \$2.5M/year. Radioactive isotope used in pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals.
- Silicon-28 — used in semiconductor industry. Purchase orders from a US semiconductor manufacturer and a European industrial supplier.
- Barium-137 — purchase order from US customer for quantum computing applications (likely IonQ).

**Revenue model:** Enrichment-as-a-service (tolling) plus direct isotope sales. Management guided to \$50-70M revenue from Yb-176 and Si-28 alone during 2026-2027, and \$300M EBITDA by 2030.

**Subsidiaries:**

- PET Labs — radiopharmaceutical formulation and dispensing
- IsoBio — therapeutics
- Quantum Leap Energy (QLE) — HALEU nuclear fuel division, intended for spin-out. Raised \$60M in convertible notes (Nov 2025) with investment from Eric Trump and Donald Trump Jr. via American Ventures LLC
- Renergen (acquired Jan 2026) — helium and LNG production in South Africa

**Key partnerships:**

- TerraPower (Bill Gates-funded) — signed term sheet to co-fund HALEU enrichment facility, targeting 2029 supply
- Fermi America — MOU for HALEU research/production facility at Texas HyperGrid campus near Amarillo
- NECSA (South African Nuclear Energy Corp) — MOU for HALEU R&D at Pelindaba nuclear center
- UK Office for Nuclear Regulation — QLE cleared for Early Engagement to become first commercial HALEU producer in UK

**Balance sheet (Q1 2025):** \$48M cash, \$35M debt. October 2025 equity raise of \$210M strengthened balance sheet to over \$260M pro-forma cash. Institutional ownership rose from near zero to over 40% (later 50%).

## Competitive Position

Stephen's view: ASPI is "probably the only company in the world with working low-cost enrichment technology" (Nov 2025 chat). Plants cost under \$3M and take 18 months to build vs multi-year greenfield centrifuge projects costing \$1B+. Claimed cost advantage of under \$50/SWU vs ~\$140/SWU for legacy gas centrifuge.

**vs Russia:** Russia controls 85% of the global supply of stable isotopes. Geopolitical risks around Russian supply create structural shortage, positioning ASPI as a Western alternative across medical, semiconductor, and nuclear fuel markets.

**vs Centrifuge incumbents:** Established centrifuge technology is 40+ years old. ASPI's modular approach offers claimed 10x cost advantage. IP moat from 20+ years of Klydon-derived trade secrets and proprietary flow-tube geometries.

**Risks:** South African base could become a geopolitical issue. CEO Paul Mann took medical leave in Sep 2025 (returned by Feb 2026), creating key-man risk during critical commercial ramp. 75% dilution in last year. Plants operating at less than 5% capacity utilization. Timeline delays on commercial shipments have been persistent. By Feb 2027, Stephen expressed frustration: "Great idea but they have not made the most of it and other companies have managed to deliver the same product for less."

**Short seller attack:** Fuzzy Panda report in early 2025 alleged SEC-sanctioned investors, lack of NCA approval, old technology, and CEO bad history. Stephen investigated all claims, contacted CEO directly and had a friend in South Africa verify operational sites. Concluded most claims were a "mixture of some truth, some speculation, a lot of irrelevant conjecture, and errors." Delayed his investment by two weeks but ultimately bought.
