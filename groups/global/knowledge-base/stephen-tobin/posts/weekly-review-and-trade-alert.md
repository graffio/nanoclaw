### **RoboTaxis**

In my last update, I discussed the two Robotaxis, Pony and WeRide, and my concerns regarding how their respective share prices had deviated in recent weeks in a way that did not reflect changes in the prospects for the two companies.

The RoboTaxi space is one I am keen to stay exposed to, as it is forecast to grow almost exponentially in the next few years. Much of that growth will be swept up by large established companies like Google, Amazon, Baidu, Hyundai, UBER, and Lyft.

Pony and WeRide are the only pure-play companies we can invest in, and recent developments are very positive for both companies.

They are pursuing different go-to-market strategies: Pony is more asset-light with its China-first robotaxi-first mantra and JVs with established EV makers. WeRide has set larger goals with its more capital-intensive global multi-vehicle strategy.

I have summarized the developments announced by both companies in the last 30 days below.

Pony AI (PONY) Developments

1.  Partnership with Xihu Group in Shenzhen Pony AI announced a strategic partnership with Shenzhen Xihu Corporation Limited to deploy over 1,000 Gen 7 Robotaxis in Shenzhen. This collaboration adopts an "asset-light + AI-empowered model," where Xihu Group handles fleet operations, safety, and services, while Pony AI focuses on AI technology and scalable Robotaxi solutions.
    
2.  Public Road Tests of Gen 7 Robotaxis Pony AI began public road tests of its seventh-generation Robotaxis in Guangzhou and Shenzhen. These vehicles, based on Guangzhou Auto's Aion V model, feature a new autonomous driving system with 100% automotive-grade components, reducing costs by 70%.
    
3.  Expansion into Dubai Pony AI partnered with Dubai's Road Traffic Authority (RTA) to deploy its Robotaxis, with supervised trials starting in 2025 and fully driverless operations planned for 2026. The partnership aligns with Dubai's "2030 Smart City Vision" to convert 25% of its transportation to autonomous mode by 2030.
    
4.  Financial Performance: Pony AI reported Q1 2025 revenue of $14 million, a 12% year-over-year increase, with a net loss of $37.4 million.
    

WeRide (WRD) Developments

1.  Expansion with Uber in 15 New Cities WeRide expanded its strategic partnership with Uber to deploy Robotaxis in 15 additional cities globally over the next five years, including locations in Europe and the Middle East.
    
2.  Launch in Saudi Arabia WeRide commenced Robotaxi trial operations in Saudi Arabia, supported by the Transport General Authority. The service is expected to launch on Uber's platform in the coming months, with full commercial operations by late 2025. This deployment supports Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 goals for smart city development.
    
3.  Deployment of Robobus and Robosweeper In addition to Robotaxis, WeRide deployed its Robobus in key areas across Saudi Arabia, including King Fahad Medical City and Aramco residential communities, to address last-mile transportation gaps. The company also introduced the Robosweeper S1 , Saudi Arabia's first monetized autonomous sanitation project.
    
4.  Financial Performance: WeRide reported Q1 2025 revenue of $10 million, a 1.8% year-over-year increase, with a net loss of $53.1 million. The company also secured a $100 million equity investment from Tencent Cloud to accelerate its global Robotaxi expansion.
    
5.  Analyst Update Morgan Stanley maintained an Overweight rating on WeRide stock but lowered its price target to $13 , citing high market volatility and rising competition in the autonomous driving sector.
    

### Take Away

I do not want to miss the exponential growth forecast for this market sector. WeRide and Pony are the pure plays in this market; I still see them as market leaders and that view was only compounded by the recent articles discussing the problems of Teslas FSD software.
