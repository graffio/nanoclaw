## Company Overview

Vertical Aerospace is a UK-based eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing) aircraft developer building the Valo aircraft (previously VX4). The company is targeting certification and commercial operations, positioning itself as a manufacturer-only business (will not operate planes).

**Products:**

- Valo eVTOL aircraft (next-generation of VX4)
- 70% new components vs first prototype, faster and quieter

**Technology position:** Design organization approval received. First prototype crashed due to single faulty propeller. Second and third aircraft built for piloted testing. Targeting certification by end of 2026 (as stated in March 2024, later revised to 2028).

**Order book:** 11,500 aircraft or approximately \$6 billion at time of initial purchase (March 2024).

**Funding:** Company spent \$93M in 2023, had \$60M in cash. CEO committed additional \$25M. Forecast \$80M spend in 2024. Ongoing concern about capital needs — two years from operations as of 2026.

## Competitive Position

Stephen holds three eVTOL companies in the portfolio. EVTL is the UK-based play alongside Chinese EH (EHang) and other eVTOL names (JOBY, ACHR).

**Key competitive dynamics:** EVTL is a manufacturer-only model. Archer Aviation (ACHR) filed a patent infringement lawsuit against Vertical in early 2026, which EVTL dismissed as "without merit."

**Insider activity:** Significant insider buying — founder bought \$5.8M in shares on Nov 19, 2025. Insiders increased shareholding by 50% in November 2025.
