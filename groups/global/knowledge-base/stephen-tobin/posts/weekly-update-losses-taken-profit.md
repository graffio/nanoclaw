We exited several positions last week, and they highlight a key part of the trading plan.

**Taking A Loss:** The road to profitability can be a long one for companies with new technology; once the initial hype fades, they can languish with low share prices for years. 30-40% drops have in the past signified this type of pre-commercialization doldrums, and barring good reason, I will take a loss when they drop this far.

It does not mean I no longer believe the thesis, just that experience has taught me that I may be in too early, and rather than let my capital stay tied up for an extended period, I will put it into a better performing stock.

All of my trading rules are based on my previous trading experience. I have kept accurate spreadsheet records of every trade taken and regularly analyse them, looking for patterns and similarities that help improve my profitability in the future.
