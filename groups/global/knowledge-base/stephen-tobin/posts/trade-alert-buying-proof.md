# Trade Alert: Buying Proof

*Market Turmoil, Indices down Adding to a Loser*

The portfolio is down over 3% in February, and we have only had 3 days of trading so far. The sell-off is broad-based, but our AI sector and REE holdings are bearing the brunt of the losses.

It is earnings time, and so far, the companies we have owned have delivered good results. Today I will add to one that has beaten top and bottom lines and whose business seems to be expanding.

As with all of our holdings, they are an emerging small cap,s so volatility is part of holding this stock, but the thesis is playing out roughly in line with the forecast, and most importantly, the future continues to look positive.

Market conditions indicate this is not the ideal time to be buying, but hopefully, fortune will favor the brave.

## Trade Alert #99: Adding to Byrna Technologies (BYRN).

This will be my fifth trade in Byrna. Before opening, the position was underwater by more than 40%, it jumped 15% at market, but has since moved lower. Overall, the stock has returned abround 20% from my investments.

The price action is far from ideal; many people would say this is trying to catch a falling knife. I believe these shares are worth more than $40 each and that this is a solid company, but there is no escaping the downtrend that is in play.

Most people would stay well clear in this situation, but I am going to buy.

## Earnings release

I have been waiting for this morning's earnings release to decide on this stock, and the results were excellent and continued to position the company for short-term growth.

The company is moving from a niche idea, with revenue based on right-wing influencers, to an established self-defence product stocked by some of the largest gun and self-defence dealers in the country.

The store-in-a-store model is making excellent progress and is driving sales forward. This is key for longer-term success, the influencer model can only go so far in helping develop a brand name, and Byrna is now moving to more traditional marketing with a more traditional business plan of selling their non-lethal guns through the main dealers in the US.

International sales continue to grow, the ammo factory has been relocated to the US, and the new products are selling well. the company continues to make signature hires in key roles with the new marketing director a former VP at Nike.

The balance sheet remains strong, bolstered by a new line of credit. Stock levels are much higher to allow for unexpectedly large repeat orders from dealers in January, suggesting another beat may be delivered next month. The company continues to buy shares with XS cash, which should support the share price.

-   **Fiscal Fourth Quarter 2025 Performance**
    
    -   Net revenue was $35.2 million, a 26% year-over-year increase, driven by growth in dealer, chain store, Amazon, and international sales.
        
    -   Gross profit rose to $21.1 million (60% of net revenue), but the gross margin declined slightly due to the sales mix and amortization related to the introduction of the CL launcher and the move of the ammunition factory.
        
    -   Operating expenses increased by 27% to $17.1 million, reflecting higher investment in advertising, marketing, and payroll for growth and innovation.
        
    -   Net income was $3.4 million, down from $4.1 (excluding the $5.6 million income tax benefit.)
        
    -   Adjusted EBITDA improved to $6.0 million from $5.0 million in the comparable prior-year quarter.
        
-   **Fiscal Year 2025 Performance**
    
    -   Net revenue climbed 38% to $118.1 million, up from $85.8 million, driven by the Byrna CL launch and retail expansion (over 600 new chain store locations).
        
    -   Gross profit totaled $71.5 million (61% of net revenue), an $18.7 million increase.
        
    -   Gross margin dipped modestly by 1% due to one-time amortization of CL launcher production costs and startup costs for the new Fort Wayne ammunition facility.
        
    -   Total operating expenses increased by 29% to $59.6 million, reflecting strategic investment in consumer awareness and product development.
        
    -   The Company anticipates margin improvement in fiscal 2026 as one-time startup costs conclude and benefits from a recent 4% to 5% price increase take effect.
        
-   **Business Progress and Operational Highlights**
    
    -   The Company surpassed 750,000 launchers sold since inception, with over 250,000 units sold in FY 2025 alone.
        
    -   Monthly production capacity expanded to 20,000 units to meet demand.
        
    -   Two new members—Adam Roth and TJ Kennedy—were appointed to the Board of Directors.
        
    -   Secured a $20.0 million credit facility with Texas Capital Bank subsequent to quarter-end for potential acquisitions.
        
    -   The product line was expanded with the introduction of the new Byrna CL XL launcher.
        
    -   Marketing investment is expanding, including testing a local market advertisement during Super Bowl LX.
        

**Conclusion**

The BYRN thesis is playing out nicely, even if the share price is collapsing. There is still a long way to go, but BYRN is profitable, has a solid balance sheet, is growing sales, and has a unique technological advantage over all competitors in the non-lethal gun market. A market that they have generated from nothing that has great potential to grow tenfold over the next decade.
