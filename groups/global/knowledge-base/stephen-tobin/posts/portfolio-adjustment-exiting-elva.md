# Portfolio Adjustment: Exiting ELVA and NPWR

*The Strong Buy portfolio continues to make excellent progress*

I am exiting ELVA and NPWR for different reasons.

ELVA: The last quarter's results were below expectations. Management said that customers had delayed orders from 2024 to 2025, accounting for the miss in Q2, and lowered guidance for the rest of the year. This means that ELVA is no longer expected to break even this year or next, and positive cash flow will likely not arrive before 2027. My mathematical model is now giving a fair value 80% above the current share price, which is below my minimum for opening a new trade. The good news was the signing of a second material handling OEM and a Japanese trading house. However, it may take time for revenue to be generated from these customers.

ELVA continue to talk about bus applications and minig trucks, they have been doing this for three years and nothing ever seems to develop.

The bad news was twofold. Firstly, the New York site did not make any real progress. Management said it was because they are awaiting financing, but I think it is more likely that they will not need additional capacity next year or in 2026, so they have no desire to press on with the capex of a new building when it is not really needed.

Secondly, at the battery day, they discussed the progress in solid-state batteries and tried to put a positive spin on things, but it seems to me that things have gone backward since I took this position. ELVA appears to have ditched its previous line of research and adopted a new one. It is now a long way behind the leaders in this space. Last year they appeared to be close in terms of performance.

I feel it is likely that ELVA will experience downward price pressure for at least a year, so I am exiting now at a loss just short of 30%.

NPWR: I remain a big believer in Net Power and will keep a holding in my never sell long-term portfolio but will exit the position I have in the Strong Buy Portfolio.

I have incrementally improved the criteria for taking trades in the Strong-Buy portfolio based on past performance, and NPWR does not meet the new criteria. It fails the mathematical model test as I cannot work out a break-even date or a fair value for the shares.

The first utility-scale site is due to start operations in 2028; testing of the turbo expander will begin next year. The growth from 2028 is unclear, it could be 3 or 4 years of meandering and falling share price with little news to lift sentiment.

Analyst target prices released lately have been around $11 when I took the position they were closer to $40. Greenlight capital has exited its position and likely more will follow putting downward pressure on the shares.

The mathematical model rule was brought in to avoid losses that I have had in the past on companies like ARBE, where I bought too early and suffered a significant loss before buying back in at a much lower price. The Strong Buy portfolio is designed for wealth creation for people who are not already wealthy, so the opportunity cost of capital is important. I cannot allow money to be tied up in stocks that may make little progress for a few years when that capital could be better deployed elsewhere. My never sell portfolio has a different outlook. I inherited it from my father and will leave it to my children. For that portfolio, only the long term counts.

I will exit NPWR for a loss near 18%.
