# Trade Alert: Buying Graphene

*The first time of many I suspect*

The Graphene market is forecasted to see near-[exponential growth between now and 2032](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/graphene-market-102930). A CAGR of 36.5% is exceptionally high and implies the market will grow more than eightfold in eight years, reaching a staggering $8.3 billion.

This growth represents the commercial adoption of the material and its extraordinary properties, [which I covered in this Graphene primer](https://stephentobin.substack.com/p/graphene-investment-opportunities?r=nh85d).

Over the next two years, **I expect to make multiple investments** in this industry as different companies establish themselves in each of the identified vertical markets.

Many of these companies are too small and early-stage to invest right now, but they are firmly on my watch list and I will be monitoring their progress closely.

Some have astonishing products awaiting government approval or in testing with large OEMs, others have unique proprietary production techniques, but are still at pilot scale.

As they reach commercialization, some of these companies will become **multibaggers,** while others will become **millionaire makers.**

Experienced emerging technology investors will know, however, that many will not succeed; they might not have the necessary capital or end up being second to market. A big multinational might out spend them on R and D or that one big customer they are relying on might decide to go elsewhere. Often, these new technologies are challenging to scale, and new products cannot justify the price premium required.

We have to choose carefully, buy the right company at the right time, and today is the right time for my first Graphene investment. I will purchase on my Interactive Brokers Account today.

## Trade Alert: Buying NanoXplore.

**Top Line:** I will buy NanoXplore today. It is a Canadian company with the ticker symbol TSE:GRA or GRA.TO, depending on the broker. It has a US-traded ticker NNXPF.

I will be buying the Canadian company and will attempt to invest US$500. The US Stock Trading account currently has $4,396 in cash and total equity of $14,067, making the new investment around 3.5% of equity.

I have already bought on the UK leveraged account. I used £35 of margin last night and made a video on how to use the platform, something requested by a subscriber.

## Introduction

Being first is not everything, but it is a lot, and in the Graphene industry, one company is light years ahead of the competition. It has scale, it has customers, it has revenue, and it has cash.

Of all the companies I examined, this is the only one that is a bona fide business; the rest are science experiments, R&D shops, pilot-scale Incubators, and a lot of hope.

**NanoXplore is the largest global producer** of low-cost, high-quality graphene. It is aggressively developing end-use markets by using graphene as a performance enhancer for a variety of different plastics with many different end products and has developed a new proprietary production method that has opened entirely new vertical markets closed to the competition because of the price point needed.

They have **two new high-volume vertical markets** that will take thousands of tonnes of Graphene per year, beginning in late 2025: the drilling fluid and spray insulation market.

I expect NanoXplore to increase its graphene production from 4,000 tpa to 20,000 tpa plus 5,000 tpa of Sheet Moulding Compound by 2026. (tpa = tons per annum)

The next largest manufacturer, Black Swan, is at 40 tpa and aims for 100. This means NanoXplore is 40 times larger than its nearest competitor; they are producing what will eventually become a commodity product, so scale really matters.

## NanoXplore and Li Batteries

NanoXplore aims to address the Li-ion battery market by offering high-performance battery materials, including Si anode solutions. The battery subsidiary VoltaXplore has been a bit of a disaster, a drag on results, and one of the two reasons the share price is currently low.

The battery market is a tough one, with long lead times and intense competition.

Currently, I do not see a commercial route for VoltaXplore; it had grand plans to be a gigafactory and had an enormous order. However, all of this has been shelved, management has reduced overheads to a minimum, and turned the project into a longer-term R&D operation. To enable this, they had to buy out their 50% JD partner, which cost them $10 million, but that cost has been paid and is in the rearview mirror, along with the huge drain on resources that the JD had become.

## NanoXplore overview

The company has two reporting segments: **Advanced Materials, Plastics, and Composites**, which provides both standard and custom graphene-enhanced products. **Battery Cells and Materials** provide silicon/graphene-enhanced Li-ion battery cells and anodes.

I will not cover the battery segment, except in the financials section of this piece.

## Advanced materials (SMC)

NanoXplore has developed a growing market for its Sheet Moulding Compound \[SMC\]. It combines Graphene with recycled plastic for use in large-area material production for the automotive industry. The compound is stronger, lighter, and dries to a ready-to-paint smooth surface.

NanoXplore has its own plastic extrusion facilities and sells completed parts to vehicle OEM customers.

NanoXplore has several contracts to supply body and engine parts for vehicles, which are used to reduce vehicle weight. Particularly important in EVs, but also important in the trucking industry, the existing business has slowed along with the EV market and the general auto market in North America (NanoXplore’s main market).

The vehicle slowdown resulted in lower than expected revenue last quarter, and management reduced guidance for the remainder of 2025.

NanoXplore has three new SOP awards for the supply of exterior parts for vehicles. The first has been delayed until mid-2025 (previously it was guided to Q1), the other two major awards are due to begin in 2026 and 2027. The programs are expected to last 10 years and generate $24 million in annual revenue.

An existing OEM customer requested a significant increase in production, necessitating an $8 million expansion of a NanoXplore facility in Quebec. The facility expansion was completed in 2024, and equipment installation was completed in April 2025. Production was scheduled to begin in Q3 2025.

The customer has paid $5 million towards the expansion cost, demonstrating their commitment and reducing NanoXplore's capital expenditure guidance by $5 million.

The customer has delayed the launch of its new model to Q1 2026. This is an ongoing trend with new vehicle models; further delays are likely, and we should expect them.

## Two new Verticals

The new graphene production process has opened two new verticals: drilling lubrication and spray foam insulation.

In the latest earnings call, Management confirmed that shipments to the first drilling customer had begun and that insulation foam is on track for first deliveries this month.

The potential here is staggering; an average well is expected to require 3 tons of graphene during the drilling phase, and tens of thousands of wells are drilled every year.

Every insulation customer is expected to require 1,000 tons a year, making the total addressable market significantly larger than the drilling lubricant industry.

In these two verticals, NanoXplore has no direct competition; no competitor can produce graphene on the required scale or at the price necessary to enter these markets, and graphene has significant advantages over the incumbent technology.

Graphene performs better, is more stable, and less toxic, with a price comparable to legacy technology. It will be an enormously disruptive force in these industries, pushing graphene towards a commodity price-elastic product.

## Manufacturing

NanoXplore has a five-year strategic plan to build capacity, CAPEX for the plan is now estimated to be $140 million, having originally been set at $170 million in 2023 when the plan began.

The newer manufacturing process requires less CAPEX than the previous one, saving $30 million for the same amount of capacity.

The CAPEX is being funded by long-term debt, management has guided to minimal dilution, but it is always a risk.

## Facilities

NanoXplore has manufacturing sites in Canada, the United States, and Europe. The two sites in Quebec are undergoing significant expansion, as is the North Carolina facility.

NanoXplore signed a new lease agreement for a manufacturing plant in Statesville NC in early 2025. Part of its plan to significantly increase manufacturing in the US of its composite parts and has a Q1 2026 SOP (newly awarded, unnamed customer) with a value of $10 million per year. Most of the equipment had arrived by the time of the last earnings call, allowing for the completion of the fit-out.

## Manufacturing

NanoXplore is moving to a proprietary dry exfoliation production process. It has previously been producing with a more traditional liquid exfoliation technique. The new method reduces capital investment in equipment by 50% and requires 75% less square footage. 8,000 metric tonnes will cost $20 million in CAPEX and the equipment for 1,000 tonnes will arrive later this year. (spot prices for graphene average $100,000 per tonne, so the $20 million investment will deliver revenue in the region of $800 million a year, quite extraordinary).

The new technology, which uses waste graphite as feedstock, is a continuous process and is highly scalable.

The new process is patent-protected (at least 20 patents in seven jurisdictions, including Europe, the US, and Korea) and, due to the reduction in graphene cost, opens up a wide range of new verticals.

A problem has arisen with the first large-scale site, as the project requires 7MW of electricity and was planned to be built in Quebec this year. Verbal approval has been given, but no official contract has been received. Despite government intervention, electricity constraints in Quebec are causing issues. The production machinery for the site was ordered 12 months ago and is expected to arrive shortly.

Management said in the last earnings call that they are approaching decision time on the project, if they don't get the Quebec approval they have two options, either relocate (the US is the preferred relocation site) or chop the facility into smaller chunks, fortunately it is a modular manufacturing system so that is possible but would likely increase costs.

The large-scale machines must be running next year to meet demand, so management or the Quebec electricity company needs to “get their arse in gear,” as my father would have said.

### Carbon Black

The new process will enable price parity with carbon black (currently selling at $1,500 per ton), a competing crystalline form of carbon that has broad adoption but significantly inferior properties compared to graphene.

If they can sell Graphene at $1,500 per ton, no one else will be able to get near that price, it is less than 2% of the current price of graphene, meaning a 98% margin, just extraordinary.

The vast majority of carbon black is used in tires (approximately 90% of the total), where it has become an indispensable ingredient for enhancing tensile strength, tear strength, abrasion resistance, and thermal resistance. **The market in 2024 was 14 million tonnes**.

## Finances

As the sale of Graphene increases as a percentage of total sales so does the margin. Gross margin has risen for 11 consecutive quarters, despite revenue fluctuations that are typical with new technologies. I expect margins to continue improving over the medium term as economies of scale and the latest production techniques begin to take effect.

I do not expect NanoXplore to sell Graphene at carbon black prices, even though they could. It has multiple technical and performance advantages, but I do expect the product to become a commodity after 2035, resulting in a substantial drop in unit sales price, accompanied by a significant rise in unit sales. The margin will come down, but profits will remain the same.

Notes

As with all these early-stage companies, we have to watch closely:

-   The new manufacturing process needs its electricity.
    
-   The vehicle parts SOP dates need to firm up
    
-   Existing customers need to return to growth
    
-   The new verticals have to scale up
    

Investing in emerging technologies is inherently risky. NanoXplore looks like a great bet, but with experience, we know it could all go wrong very quickly, yet it may go right very slowly. The key to success is controlling position size and accepting that every individual company could be a bust. That is why we spread the total investment over a portfolio.
