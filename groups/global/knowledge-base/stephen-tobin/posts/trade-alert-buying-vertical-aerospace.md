# Trade Alert: Buying Vertical Aerospace

*MULN hit stop loss.*

I am currently writing a series of articles on the eVTOL market. It looks clear that EVTL is undervalued; I rated them a sell in 2022 when they were priced at $3.94. They are at $0.80 today, and I will buy them at market open.

# Fundamental Changes

The company has received design organization approval for its new aircraft, VX4. The first one crashed, and the review identified a single faulty propeller as the cause.

The new aircraft will have 70% new components and be faster and quieter than its previous version. After certification, the company intends to fly the aircraft at Farnborough this summer, this will likely lead to more orders and a hike in prices at which point I may exit.

The CEO has confirmed that he believes the company is funded through 2025 and will only manufacture, not operate planes. The order book for the company remains at 11,500 aircraft or $6 billion. In Q1, the company will complete the assembly of its second plane. In 2024 H2, the company will build a third plane, and both new planes will be piloted. The company has completed 70% of the required documents for the permit to fly and is targeting certification by the end of 2026.

The company spent $93 million in 2023 and has $60 million in cash. The CEO has committed a further $25 million. The company forecasts spending $80 million in 2024.
