# Reviewing Stocks

*Being vigilant with the portfolio*

The portfolio is suffering from the ongoing geopolitical tensions. So far, losses are not too dramatic, but it is important to stay vigilant and take action where needed.

I have highlighted several companies under review, and in this piece, I will begin sharing concerns. I never take any trading action without publishing it in advance, so it is useful to keep subscribers updated so they understand my thinking. Of course, they have to do their own research and make their own decisions, but a review often helps people decide how much risk they want to take and where they would rather trim positions.

Over the three years I have been publishing results, we have had 6 previous occasions with back-to-back monthly losses. The largest drawdowns were in February and March 2025, when we saw an 8% drop followed by a 4% drop. However, February and March 2026 come on the back of relatively weak previous months, unlike last year.

I currently hold positions in 26 companies. In the last 7 days, only 4 have shown a profit; the best performer is up 18%, and the worst is down 13%.

I have not made any trades so far this month and it is unlikely I will buy anything this week, risks of a greater pullback remain high.

I am reviewing the portfolio's stocks to ensure the thesis remains intact. I do not currently intend to have a fire sale of assets due to the ongoing geopolitical issues, but to continue to exit when the thesis of the initial investment fails.

Rarely do we get a clear signal from these reviews, but they do help frame the concerns and choose a path forward.

If I decide to exit any stocks, I will send a trade alert in advance. This is a review of my thinking about specific stocks of concern.

## Ehang (EH)

I have made two investments in Ehang both quite some time ago and the stock has just drifted lower since.

[

![Image 2](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/d389f545-d3c7-48d3-9f30-03194c0a757e_1315x216.png)

](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HPQA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd389f545-d3c7-48d3-9f30-03194c0a757e_1315x216.png)
Ehang's stock often trades in hype cycles; the underlying business has rarely been reflected in the stock price, but we have been lacking any catalysts of late. They remain the only certified eVTOL but face ongoing regulatory uncertainty and a very skeptical Western market. Geopolitical tensions always play on ADR stocks as concerns rise about how the US authorities will treat these foreign stocks and the threat of de-listing has been held over Chinese stocks for decades.

**Slowing indicators**

Revenue fell 28% in Q3 2025 YoY and 37% QoQ. Deliveries also fell by 42 aircraft in Q3, down from 68 in Q2, and profitability fell with a loss of US$2.9 million following a first profit in Q2.

**Guidance**

Management reiterated full-year guidance of RMB500 million, citing the backlog of nearly 1,000 units. They also reduced expense growth to 15% YoY from 30% previous.

**The Problem**

It was expected that when the aircraft was approved Ehang would soon see revenue shift up a gear as operators started to generate cash from their existing fleet and ordered more aircraft beyond the initial test ones but that shift has not happened.

The bottleneck is a lack of licensed ground crew and certified operators. Although the aircraft is approved for flight conditions, the approval requires licensed ground craft and certified operators. The granting of licences and certifications is at the discretion of the Chinese regulator, so there is limited visibility on timing.

**The Solution**

In Q3, management said it had shifted its focus away from new aircraft sales to helping existing customers with the approval process. 100 operators are due for certification in the first half of this year and if that happens (or perhaps is happening) then likely the stock will see a significant lift and orders will resume their growth strategy.

There is a risk that these approvals do not arrive; if so, this thesis could be broken.

Brokers remain split on Ehang, Jefferies has a target of $24 but JPMorgan (who have always been negative) still hold it at $12

**Conclusion**

Earnings are due on March 11th, I will wait to see what progress has been made and if the operator issue is not going well I will exit.

## Aptiv PLC (APTV)

I have made two trades in APTV the holding is currently in the money 14%

[

![Image 3](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/c32b0485-4f0b-4aa6-ab94-a9130151f1f6_1207x252.png)

](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BM8H!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc32b0485-4f0b-4aa6-ab94-a9130151f1f6_1207x252.png)
APTV does not fit the general makeup of the portfolio, being a large, well-established company.

The original thesis was that orders expected to go to ARBE and its radar were actually going to APTV. Likely that particular thesis was wrong and it is probably LiDAR that is being adopted instead of Radar. AEVA recently reported that it has won the Mercedes deal with its 4D LiDAR solution. Mercedes had been a key Arbe target.

APTV has a multi-year Radar program with Indie Semiconductor as the exclusive supplier, supporting 6-8 OEMs through 2026 and 2027. No indication of volumes.

APTV is in the process of a strategic pivot, spinning out its Electrical Distribution Systems division (Versigent). That spin-out is due to take effect on April 1st, 2026.

Q4 2025 revenue increased 3% and the new APTIV guidance was for an increase in 4% in 2026. APTV secured $27 billion in new business awards last year with approximately 75% coming from China.

APTV is highly exposed to global EV sales, so it is actually performing quite well, increasing sales as its overall market contracts in its main markets, the US and Europe.

Management is guiding to 2026 EPS of $8.15 - $8.75 if they hit this, the company is fundamentally undervalued on any metric, with a share price of $71. On average, for the last three years, APTV has traded at 17xEPS, but that fell to 9xEPS in 2025.

Recent broker targets range from $84 to $110, all above current prices

**Conclusion**

The spin-off may cause a re-rating, as the new APTV will have sold off its lower-margin division and may be seen as more of an autonomous driving/connected vehicle-enabling company. The ADAS systems will all be housed in the new APTV and the traditional parts division will be in Versignet.

The call here is nuanced; we have a bit of profit we could book, which might be useful in the current situation, and the overall holding at $714 represents 3.3% of equity. It might be a good place to reduce drawdown and free up some cash for trading later.

## AirShip AI

I have taken two positions in AISP

[

![Image 4](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/89d9fd47-770e-49b7-ba86-593a73d5c770_1200x251.png)

](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4hjH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89d9fd47-770e-49b7-ba86-593a73d5c770_1200x251.png)
The technology and the thesis looked very solid but it is clearly not going according to plan.

Following 87% revenue growth in 2024, AISP was hit by US government headwinds in 2025. I had expected them to benefit from the administration’s desire to strengthen their borders but spending does not appear to have followed the rhetoric.

2025 revenues fell 33% which is a major concern but Q4 showed a possible return to growth with revenue up 102% YoY, that result triggered an additional buy from me as I took it to mean that the original thesis was playing out.

The two brokers covering AISP share a similar view, with both Roth Capital and the benchmark expecting significant spending on border security this year. Management are not as convinced as they said specific agency budgets are still to be confirmed for 2026, the enormous spending on overseas military action likely reduces the possibility of large spending on domestic security.

The US administration's overseas actions go against my thesis. I had assumed they would become more isolationist and increase spending with companies like AISP but I was clearly wrong in that regard.

AISP technology is also becoming increasingly under threat, they have not managed to cement their position as a supplier to the US border wall and I have recently reviewed several silicon chip manufacturers who are putting similar features on to their chips, allowing integrators to develop a similar feature set to AISP at a fraction of the cost and bundle it as part of other products.

**Conclusion**

The 100% growth in Q4 and the large validate pipeline are major positives but pipelines are not orders. Competition is growing and the largest customer, US government agencies, appear not to have finalized budgets and are perhaps focused elsewhere.

The next earnings are not due until early May, so it is probably not sensible to wait for those results. If I exit, then I could well be selling at a bottom, and with a 39% loss already taken, the holding is at $438, equal to 2% of equity.

Will the pipeline turn into revenue? Or will the new on-chip erode their market share? With government agencies, it is difficult to predict. AISP are a known and successful technological solution but budget pressures and a focus on other areas could well have the border agencies looking to save money.

It may be wise to take the hit, but I hate selling near a low.

## ASP Isotopes

I have made three investments in ASPI, all underwater

[

![Image 5](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/4ecdc65e-1dbf-437d-ac3b-cb496219ff93_1208x305.png)

](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ddjF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ecdc65e-1dbf-437d-ac3b-cb496219ff93_1208x305.png)
ASPI is a speculative bet that they can move their enrichment technology from the lab and into commercial production.

In Q3 2025, timeline delays led to a significant miss in revenue and margins. In an interview on Feb 12th, the CEO, Paul Mann, who has recently returned to work following a medical issue, was very clear that he believed the timeline extensions will end this year and that commercial shipments of several isotopes are imminent. He maintained that initial batches have passed customer evaluation and that the problems with feedstock have been solved. He also said that when the 4 isotopes that have passed customer approval start shipping later this year ASPI will become cash generating.

Russia controls 85% of the supply of stable isotopes, and they are involved in all of the most important emerging technologies, including nuclear medicine (where ASPI is growing in double digits). ASPI is expanding this medical business into other countries, including the US. ASPI has also moved into Helium production, a chemical in short supply, with demand growing as supply drops.

For HALEU (nuclear fuel) ASPI intends to spin off their division involved with production. This is really about generating enough cash to build the facilities needed to enrich fuel.

The risks around ASPI are enormous, will its base in South Africa become a geopolitical issue? It is quite possible that the bus is more of a risk than Russia and China ASPIs only competitors?

We have seen 75% dilution in the last year and that has change the balance sheet (which is now solid) and the nature of shareholders (now 50% institutional holdings). Although we don’t like the dilution the changes it has brought are positive. Dilution is an ongoing concern with ASPI and I do expect more.

**Conclusion**

This is a very high-risk play, and it could easily all come to nothing, but they have proven technology, they can enrich isotopes, and their products are in high demand. If they can begin commercial shipments, despite all the regulatory concerns, and get paid, I think they remain a multibagger opportunity, so I am going to hold this one.

I will be reviewing other companies this week and will share in the weekend review. Companies far from profitability are generally hit hardest in a risk-off scenario, so SPCE and EVTL are of pressing concern. PONY and WRD are suffering but I do not think there is any justification in those moves.

The other area of concern is the nano caps I have bought; these were very high-risk bets and hence half-size. They will undoubtedly suffer, but I will hold on until the thesis breaks.
