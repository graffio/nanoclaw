My Father was a great stock picker; every year, he would spend hours poring over the end-of-year figures in the FT. We didn't have computers at the time, so he would go line by line with a pencil and ruler, looking to identify the stocks that had fallen the most in the thousands the FT covered. He said they were “on sale for a limited period.” He would then analyze each company and buy the best in January. It worked almost every year, and I still have some of those stocks today.

Markets are more volatile these days, and over the last year, we have seen a couple of themes play out, leaving some stocks “on sale for a limited period”.

I use models to analyze many things. I have mathematical models for valuing companies and waves for modeling technology's path.

I also have a model for the Stock Market. It is a way of moving money from the poor to the rich. No different from the medieval feudal lords taxing their peasants so they could live a lavish life.

Today, the rich invest their funds with smart-money hedge funds that use that money  to transfer wealth away from retail investors.

If retail investors are dumping a stock at the same time as successful hedge funds are buying, then it sends a clear signal.

Today, I am investing in a stock that has been dumped by retail investors, acquired by hedge funds, and has suffered a brutal pullback, and will be on sale for a limited period.

It is out of my normal comfort zone, it's a big company with positive cash flow and a fortress balance sheet, but I know a bargain when I see one.

## What do they Do?
ServiceNow is a leading enterprise software provider that delivers a cloud-based platform to automate and digitize complex business workflows across the entire organization.

Often described as an “AI control tower for business,” the company provides a unified architecture that connects disparate departments—such as IT, HR, and customer service—to a single “system of action” that orchestrates how work flows between people, data sources, and AI agents. By providing a “single pane of glass” for enterprise operations, ServiceNow helps organizations move away from fragmented legacy tools toward automated, intelligent processes that increase productivity and reduce operational costs.

The company’s product portfolio is organized into four primary areas: Technology, Customer Relationship Management, Core Business (Employee Workflows), and Creator Workflows.

Key offerings include IT Service Management, which helps manage IT infrastructure; Customer Service Management, which resolves issues through proactive support; and HR Service Delivery, which streamlines employee requests for facilities or legal services. Additionally, the “Creator” suite provides low-code development tools such as App Engine, enabling customers to build custom applications on the core ServiceNow AI platform. Customers typically integrate these products through a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) model, often using ServiceNow’s “Workflow Data Fabric” to seamlessly connect intelligence from various cloud environments and internal data silos.

ServiceNow primarily serves large enterprise customers across a wide array of sectors, including financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, and telecommunications. As of late 2025, the company had approximately 8,700 total customers, including 85% of the Fortune 500.

Its target market focuses on organizations with more than 1,000 employees and at least $500.00 million in annual revenue, and it is increasingly expanding its footprint in government and public-sector agencies.

**AI Agents:** In 2026, ServiceNow transitioned from just “tracking” work to “doing” work. Their autonomous AI agents can now resolve common IT and HR issues without a human ever having to touch the keyboard.


## Why the Stock Sell Off?
The bear case has three elements.


- **AI is going to kill software companies**


This comes from a misunderstanding of AI. It is the case that some software companies will suffer or even be replaced, but people should remember that AI is a probability function. That is why they hallucinate, and you can not be certain of the results. Company workflows need to be deterministic; they must give the right answer. Retail traders have this the wrong way round; they think AI will replace enterprise-wide orchestration, when the truth is that AI depends on it.

The CEO addressed this point during the latest earnings call, announcing two partnerships: one with Anthropic and one with OpenAI. ServiceNow is using Anthropic’s Claude as “the default model powering ServiceNow Build Agents,” which helps developers “build and deploy agentic workflows.” Internally, the company “deploys Claude and Claude Code to more than 29,000 employees.” Additionally, on the call, the Chief Product Officer [CPO] commented, “we use Claude as the underlying technology to generate some of the code, and then we provide the context, the security, the governance on top of that, using “build agent” to run those workflows on top of ServiceNow.” Regarding ServiceNow’s OpenAI partnership, the CEO commented, “OpenAI models will be a preferred intelligence capability for several agentic use cases offered to ServiceNow enterprise customers.” With the CPO adding,  our CRM products can now have voice capabilities with OpenAI, as a preferred model, so that we can have a much more differentiated offering using what we know from domains perspective as well as context, and adding the OpenAI speech capabilities into our products.”


- **Seat Based Pricing**


For many years, SaaS has made great returns because companies pay by seat- 10,000 staff using the system is 10,000 seats and 10,000 payments. AI will reduce the number of staff, hence the number of seats and the size of payments.

In the latest earnings call, the CEO pointed out that on the seat-based model, their potential market is 1.3 billion seats, so they have only ever scratched the surface, but he also presented this chart showing how their subscription model is changing depending on the application.

It has been reported that NOW has begun M&A activity to increase revenue and meet its guidance. The suggestion is that the business model is failing, so the acquisition spree is trying to cover it up.

## Competition
Competitive threats do remain. While ServiceNow maintains a stronghold in the large enterprise segment, there is mounting pressure from Microsoft, Salesforce, and Atlassian. This is particularly evident in the mid-market, where customers are often more price-sensitive and may opt for “lighter” or more specialized solutions like Jira or Microsoft Power Automate.

Analysts emphasize that the competition has shifted from a features-based comparison to a “platform-level battleground” focused on which vendor can best orchestrate autonomous agents across the entire enterprise. ServiceNow, Salesforce, and Microsoft are currently locked in a “three-way battle” to become the dominant AI agent platform for enterprises, with each leveraging its respective strengths in workflows, customer data, and productivity ecosystems.

Summarising the view of brokers

**ServiceNow** has solidified its position as the “AI Control Tower” of the modern corporation. By acting as the “semantic layer” and governance backbone for workflows, it has monetized AI at a blistering pace. Its financial velocity is undeniable; growth is fueled by massive “Pro Plus” deal momentum, where high-value licenses exceeding $1 million tripled quarter-over-quarter. Industry experts note that ServiceNow’s primary moat is its ownership of over 80 billion annual workflows, providing an operational context that generic LLMs cannot replicate. Consequently, large enterprises are increasingly consolidating fragmented tasks onto the ServiceNow platform to gain a centralized “command center” for managing and securing their AI agents.

In contrast, Salesforce is vying to be the “Front Door” to the agentic enterprise. Its strategy centers on an agent-centric model where Slack serves as the primary conversational interface for executing tasks across various applications. This approach has seen explosive traction, with *Agentforce* ARR growing 169% year-over-year to $800 million. Salesforce has also pioneered a shift in monetization through “Agentic Work Units,” which measure value based on AI consumption rather than just seats—a metric that grew 57% sequentially in early 2026. However, the path to scaling remains bumpy; field research indicates that while pilot programs are numerous, many customers struggle to move into full production due to “dirty data” and the immense complexity of their existing metadata.

Meanwhile, Microsoft remains the “Full-Stack Backbone,” leveraging its structural advantage across the cloud, application, and developer layers. With over 80% of Fortune 500 companies already using its low-code tools like *Copilot Studio*, Microsoft’s pervasive footprint is unmatched. By bundling AI, security, and governance into the premium M365 E7 SKU, they have effectively driven up ARPU while reducing deployment friction for IT departments. Furthermore, with 4.7 million paid GitHub Copilot subscribers, Microsoft has locked in the developer community, ensuring its role as the primary orchestrator of the underlying compute platform. Partners suggest this “all-in-one” Azure-driven approach gives Microsoft a durability that is difficult for pure-play software competitors to disrupt.

The industry is moving from “hype” to “implementation reality.” Some experts report that ServiceNow is starting to erode Salesforce’s core Sales and Service Cloud opportunities in the mid-market due to its superior AI orchestration capabilities.


## Conclusion
I think NOW is a great company with great products and a defensible moat. It has leading technology that is rapidly being adopted by large organizations, and its recent acquisitions have enabled it to expand its core offerings. It faces competition, but in an industry this big, they can all do well.
