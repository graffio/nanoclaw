## Company Overview

Kodiak AI is an autonomous vehicle technology company that merged with Ares Acquisition Corp II via SPAC in 2024. Unlike Aurora's highway-focused approach, Kodiak targets off-road industrial, defence, and commercial trucking verticals with modular, map-less autonomous driving technology.

**Technology — Kodiak Driver:**

- Proprietary "SensorPods" — modular hardware units (cameras, radar, LiDAR) that can be swapped or retrofitted onto various truck models
- AI-based, map-less system — does not use HD maps, making it suitable for off-road environments
- Vehicle-agnostic: operational on Class 8 trucks, Ford F-150s, and treaded military vehicles
- Expanded to pulling doubles (two trailers) — advanced technique not supported by Aurora

**Three verticals:**

1. Industrial (off-road/private roads): Safety case complete. 5,200 hours of paid driverless operations (+166% in Q3). First-mover in estimated over \$50B worldwide market
2. Defence: \$30M US Army contract. Fitted to multiple military vehicle types. Hired VP/MD of Defense (ex-Detroit Defense). US Marine Corps contract for ROGUE-Fires carrier
3. Highway: Safety case 78% complete, targeting 100% in 2026. Shipping freight Dallas-Houston with safety driver

**Key partnerships:**

- Atlas Energy: Expanded from 30 to 100 trucks in Permian Basin — largest order for any autonomous trucking company. Operating 24/7 on private roads
- Roush: Dedicated retrofit line (same Roush that built Bluebird engines)
- Bosch: Manufacturing sensor pods in volume for OEM use
- ZF: Steering and actuation systems with redundancy
- ARK Invest: Buying up to 60,000 shares per week; major institutional supporter

**SPAC disaster:** Expected \$562M in cash, received only \$62M after massive shareholder redemptions. 180% dilution in first year. Shareholder equity only \$3M, debt \$33M. Insiders own 29%, Wall Street 43%.

## Competitive Position

**vs Aurora (AUR):** "For the time being, it is AUR v KDK." Aurora leads highway with completed safety cases and active driverless operations. Kodiak leads in industrial/off-road with complete safety validation. Key difference: Aurora deep OEM integration with HD mapping; Kodiak modular, map-less approach enabling multi-vertical flexibility.

**Capital situation:** Kodiak needs ~\$750M total (\$500M this year). Aurora has \$1.6B runway through H2 2027. Kodiak's survival depends on successful capital raises — institutional support from ARK and others suggests this is achievable.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Jan 2026, Trade Alert #94):** Half-size position (\$300, ~1.5% of equity) reflecting high-risk nature. "For risk-tolerant investors, Kodiak AI appears to offer a better risk/reward profile for 2026." Key drivers: (1) Atlas Energy deal provides commercial validation — 100 trucks, largest order in the industry; (2) off-road/defence verticals provide revenue cushion while highway builds out; (3) capital-efficient, asset-light model with Tier 1 partners (Bosch, ZF, Roush); (4) ARK institutional support suggests ability to raise needed capital. Stephen's model: 1,500 trucks at \$250K/truck at 30% gross margin delivers profitability. Target \$19 (average of scenarios), could upgrade to ~\$44 if 2026 goes well.

**Concerns:** Precarious balance sheet. Needs \$750M in capital. SPAC redemption disaster echoes Zapata (total loss). 180% dilution already. Oil crisis assessment (Mar 2026): Medium short-term, Medium medium-term — "Defence business may counteract commercial problems."
