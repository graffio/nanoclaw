## Decided I cant add to AISP. I am going to put the review here rather than sendin
*stephen Tobin at 10:28 UTC*

**Stephen Tobin:**
Decided I cant add to AISP. I am going to put the review here rather than sending by email. Not sure if that is the best approach. Perhaps people could give their views?
AISP released their earnings report on Aug 5, 2025.
Key line items were
Revenue $2.1 million
Gross Profit $1.5 million
Gross Margin 71%
The revenue figure missed expectations in the previous 4 quarters revenue had been $6m, $3m, $3m, $6m. It looks like some seasonality going on here but the print of $2m was low.
Gross margin came in above historical, last 4 quarters were 70%, 75%, 39%, 41% so 71% was in line with Q2 and Q3 2023 and well above the last two quarters. Likely this is product mix. They sell hardware products and then ongoing service support after that, support is effectively SaaS and covers update to the AI model and is high margin.

Product sales dropped from $5 million to $0.7 million YoY and post product support grew from $1.0m to $1.4m a 40% rise which is very good as this is long term recurring revenue but is currently too small to support the company. The bull case for AISP is them growing this support revenue over the years.

Expenses changed little YoY, which is good news however the loss figures were distorted by non cash changes to earnout and warrant liabilities. They booked almost $17m of these charges against total operating expenses of $3.5m The big quarterly loss probably accounts for the drop in shares but as it is none cash it should be ignored.

Guidance 
Management guided to a verified pipeline of $128 million (a record) and 2025 revenue growth of 30% implying 2025 revenue of $29.9 million against my forecast of $30 million.
It does imply significant growth in the last two quarters.

The pipeline of $128m, expected to be recognised over 2026 and 2027 is materially higher than my total revenue forecast for those two years of 39+54 = $93 million.

Management also guided to expense increases with additions to the workforce. Both engineering and sales headcounts are forecast to grow but as yet we do not have figures for the size of the growth.

Using all the new data I have updated my mathematical model, I had to make estimates of the growth in cost base and the increases to the share count.

For the first time, I could create a fair value for the shares of a little over $10; the current price is $5.03.

Conclusion

Price fell after the earnings report, but it did not dent the medium-term view for this company; indeed, macroeconomic changes suggest the base case remains solid, with growth forecast in the key areas of autonomous and border. The validated pipeline adds some strength to the mathematical model (assuming they are correct) and has allowed me to produce a fair value.

Risks here are too big for me to add to the position.

Despite the equity raise in March the company remains in a serious negative equity situation with assets of $10 million and liabilities of $60 million. Cash and equivalents stand at $6 million, but cash burn is around $2 million a quarter.

Less than a year of cash and negative equity means one thing: dilution.

We can expect significant dilution in the year ahead and I will wait for that dilution before buying. When the finances have a secure footing, I will be ready to increase exposure, but until then, I will keep the position as it is.
