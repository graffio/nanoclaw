# Trade Alert (#72): Adding to Nuclear

*Buliding the position in this emerging company*

Successful emerging technology investing is all about stock picking; you must choose stocks that possess the right technology and a competitive advantage early in their life cycle.

You cannot get it right every time and need to monitor progress, but when the opportunity arises to add to a winning position, I like to take it. Sometimes it involves moving money from a loser, other times it involves redistributing profits, but either way, it puts more money into winning trades and lets them run.

The Nuclear market is a technology I track, expected to see near exponential growth in the coming years. It is a key emerging technology on multiple fronts, from enrichment of fuel, Small Modular Reactors, and advancing nuclear medicine.

I want to ensure I have exposure to all segments of this market and build positions where possible. A crucial part of the plan is to compound our returns by increasing winning trades when opportunities arise, and the companies we are investing in are still under the radar of most investors.

When they go mainstream, it is often too late; the hype cycle can take hold of the stock, and its price starts to deviate from its fundamentals; however, buying early in a company's commercialization presents an additional set of risks.

It is necessary to control position size and limit overall exposure. I will increase my exposure to this company from 3% of my portfolio to 4%, taking an additional position that is half the size of my current holding.

The position is currently up 22% from when I first invested in May.

# **Trade Alert #72: ASP Isotopes ASPI**

**Key Takeaway:** I am adding to my position. I bought $400 of stock in May and will buy an additional $200 today.

The last few months have marked a pivotal transition for **ASPI** from a research-focused organization to a commercial supplier of enriched isotopes.

ASPI has **commissioned three enrichment** plants in South Africa, begun commercial shipments, and joined a JV to build-out U.S. capacity in Texas.

ASPI has laid the foundations that could open multiple addressable markets across semiconductors, nuclear energy and radiopharma.

ASPI's ability to build isotope enrichment plants at a fraction of the anticipated cost and at an unmatched speed gives it a **clear and sustainable competitive advantage.**

Management has guided to 2030 EBITDA of $300 million, a sensible multiplier would be somewhere from x5 to x10, (it could be as high as x30 for an emerging technology company).

It suggests a Market cap between $1.5 billion and $3 billion.

Today the company is at $500 million. **A 300% upside seems likely.**

We do not have the details of this plan yet, but it will be given at ASPI’s first earnings call in November. The call could drive prices higher if the plan gains some traction with investors.

## Investor traction

ASPI has been inviting investment companies to view its operational facilities, and it is having a dramatic effect. Institutional ownership is up from almost nothing to over 40% today.

## **Operational Milestones (September 2025)**

-   **Three enrichment facilities now online.** ASPI brought its first Carbon-14 plant and its multi-isotope Silicon-28 facility into production during 1H25; a Quantum Enrichment plant for Ytterbium-176 completed commissioning in April 25 and is now shipping samples
    
-   **First commercial shipments dispatched.** Pilot lots of **Yb-176** and **Si-28** were sent to customers in August; initial **C-12** shipments are targeted for September
    
-   **Revenue visibility.** Management estimates the first two isotopes alone represent **US$50-70 m** of annual revenue in 2026-27 based on indicated demand.
    
-   **Capacity expansion.** Procurement of long-lead items for **four new laser enrichment plants** is underway, with construction slated to begin 1Q26.
    

The $50-$70 million in revenue over 2026-7 is a big deal. Centrus Energy (LEU) enriched $90 million of LEU in 2024 (2025 target is $105). Centrus has spent $265 million building its enrichment project and started construction in 2019. ASPI spent $4 million, and it took 1 year to bring its Silicon-28 enrichment facility online.

TerraPower is funding ASPI's HALEU production facility in South Africa, a 2-year project with a $20 million price tag.

The difference is the crux of the investment case in ASPI. ASPI's older technology seems cheaper, easier to build, and is capable of producing a wide range of different radioactive isotopes.

## **Building a Vertically Integrated Portfolio**

Medical isotopes are a growing business and will need a decentralized supply chain. These carefully targeted drugs have a half-life of 6-10 hours. They will need to be produced close to the point of use; you can't put them on a 10-hour flight, as they will no longer be effective.

ASPI's ability to build a low-cost enrichment facility quickly could see it selling or installing multiple systems in many countries.

These entities form an ecosystem in which ASPI supplies enriched isotopes, PET Labs formulates and dispenses, IsoBio advances therapeutics, while QLE focuses on nuclear fuel and could become a separately traded security. The **spin-out** aims to allow distinct investor profiles to ascribe value to the higher-risk/higher-return HALEU business while preserving option value for medical and semiconductor isotopes.

### **Texas HyperGrid Collaboration**

-   **MOU with Fermi America (Aug-25).** QLE and Fermi intend to develop a HALEU research and production facility on the 11 GW HyperGrid campus near Amarillo, Texas. Fermi will secure all necessary nuclear licenses, while ASPI also plans a **stand-alone stable-isotope plant** at the same site.
    
-   **Strategic location.** The campus is adjacent to DOE’s Pantex Plant, offering a pre-qualified nuclear site and sovereign institutional control via Texas Tech University.
    

**Implications.** U.S. siting positions QLE to meet Western demand for domestically sourced HALEU, reducing reliance on Russian supply that currently accounts for ~35 % of enriched U.S. fuel, according to DOE data.

Having an ASPI enrichment site co-located will allow for the US supply of all other enriched isotopes for medical and silicon chip production.

## **Risk Considerations**

-   **Execution risk** on building four additional enrichment plants and Texas facilities within announced timelines.
    
-   **Regulatory approvals** outstanding for the Renergen merger and eventual HALEU licensing.
    
-   **Capital intensity.** While recent off-take agreements (e.g., TerraPower loan, not detailed in September letter) help de-risk funding, large-scale enrichment remains capital-heavy.
    
-   **Competitive landscape.** Incumbent centrifuge and laser enrichment players may accelerate their own HALEU and stable-isotope programs.
    

## **Outlook**

Management’s near-term priorities are (1) scaling the South African plants to commercial volumes, (2) achieving first revenue from Si-28, Yb-176 and C-12, (3) closing the Renergen deal, and (4) converting the Texas MOU into binding agreements while preparing QLE for a separate listing. If executed, these steps could position ASPI as a diversified, vertically integrated isotope platform addressing secular growth themes across **AI hardware, clean energy, and precision oncology**.

The four management priorities show what a high-risk play this is; any of the four priorities could fail. So far, they have been good to their word and over-delivered with the enrichment sites coming online ahead of schedule. The base in South Africa seems to be an enormous advantage as the company is deeply embedded in the Nuclear agency there and moves through the approval process far quicker than elsewhere. It helps that the technology they use is South African and well-known to the approving body.

The first position in ASPI, Trade 47, taken in May was for 60 shares at $6.745 average. Total investment $404.70. The trade is showing a profit of $88.

I am going to place a mid-priced order targeting an additional $200 of ASPI shares, bringing my investment to 4% of the portfolio.
