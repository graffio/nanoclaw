## Company Overview

American Resources Corporation is a holding company pivoting from idled metallurgical coal assets toward an integrated, domestic critical-mineral supply chain. The key technology is held in two subsidiaries: ReElement Technologies (AREC owns 19%) and the wholly-owned Electrified Materials (EMCO).

**Technology — ReElement chromatographic separation:**

- Proprietary technology developed at Purdue University, adapted from the pharmaceutical industry
- Solvent-free process claimed to be 100 times more efficient than traditional solvent extraction, generating 80% less waste
- Feedstock agnostic: can refine mined ores, coal waste streams, byproducts, and end-of-life batteries
- Capable of producing 99.9%+ purity across multiple rare earth elements
- Can produce heavy REEs (Dysprosium, Terbium) with no current Western-based refining facilities

**Corporate structure:**

- ReElement Technologies (19% owned): REE refining arm, pursuing late 2026 listing. CEO of AREC stepping down to become Executive Chairman, focusing on ReElement
- Electrified Materials / EMCO (wholly owned): Metals recycling using density-based mechanical separation. Filing S1 for public listing. Expanding capacity for high-purity battery materials
- Kentucky Lithium (ReElement subsidiary): Recycling coal deposits into lithium carbonate
- Royalty Management Corporation: Outsourced restart of AREC-owned mining facilities, providing royalty stream

**Key deals and milestones:**

- \$200M finance facility from Transition Equity Partners (Jan 2026) — key de-risker
- January 20, 2026: Successfully produced 99.9% purity samarium — critical for jet engines and guided missiles (commercial validation trigger)
- POSCO International: Multi-year offtake for over 3,000 tonnes of Dy, Y, Tb and NdPr through 2030
- \$150M EXIM Bank LOI for "nation's largest critical-mineral refinery"
- OSC loans: \$80M direct to ReElement plus \$620M to partner Vulcan
- DoD Office for Strategic Capital funding 10,000-ton-per-annum facility in Indiana
- Initial 2026 shipments of end-of-life lithium-ion batteries commenced (Jan 2026)
- EMCO capacity expansion announced Mar 2026

**Finances:** Balance sheet is a mess — negative shareholder equity of almost \$100M, debt exceeding \$200M. History of covenant breaches. Revenue collapsed when coal mining idled in 2024. February 2025: completed ReElement spin-off (1 share per 3 AREC shares). Auditor dismissed Nov 2025. Nasdaq notices for bid-price and delayed annual meeting.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Jan 2026, Trade Alert #96):** "There are no rare earths, just rare earth refining facilities." The US critical minerals crisis is a processing bottleneck, not a mining crisis. China controls over 90% of refined rare earths. AREC has DoD backing, proven technology, and secured financing for commercial-scale facility. The \$200M TEP facility and 99.9% samarium production were the commercial validation Stephen was waiting for. Half-size position (\$300) due to high risk. Mathematical model suggests fair value over \$88; technical chart suggests \$11 by June 2026. "Such a divergence [between analyst targets] is unique and perhaps reflects a unique company."

**Concerns:** Negative equity, massive debt, governance red flags (material weaknesses, restatements, auditor dismissal, Nasdaq notices). Coal revenue collapse. Stock had jumped 25% on the news, risking pullback. Oil crisis assessment (Mar 2026): Short-term Medium, Medium-term Low — "Still developing, spin-off may be an issue."
