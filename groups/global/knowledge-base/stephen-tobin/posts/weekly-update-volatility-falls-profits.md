## Weekly Digest: (6 – 12 September 2025)

**ACM Research (ACMR) – 7 Sep**

-   Shipped its first **Ultra Lith KrF track system** to a leading Chinese logic wafer fab, expanding into the 90 nm–28 nm mature-node lithography segment and complementing existing wet-clean offerings.
    
-   Management highlights domestic demand for cost-effective legacy chips; DUV lithography is expected to hold **72.5 %** 2025 market share, providing a sizeable revenue pool for ACM’s new tool line.
    

**ASP Isotopes (ASPI) – 9 Sep**

-   Appointed nuclear-industry veteran **Ralph Hunter** to its board to bolster regulatory expertise as the company prepares to apply its Aerodynamic Separation Process to uranium enrichment for next-generation reactors
    

**Vertical Aerospace (EVTL) – 9 & 10 Sep**

-   Completed **Phase 3 wing-borne flight** of the VX4 prototype, achieving 120 knots, 2,000 ft altitude and 250 mi of piloted testing; final transition testing is targeted before year-end, keeping 2028 certification goals intact.
    

**AIRO Group (AIRO) – 12 Sep**

-   Priced an **upsized public offering** of 4.2 m shares at **US $18.50** , for gross proceeds of **US $77.7 m** ; funds will support the firm’s four growth vectors: drones, avionics, training and electric air mobility.
    

**Aurora Innovation (AUR) – 9 & 12 Sep**

-   At two investor conferences, management disclosed that driverless trucks have logged **50,000 miles** on public roads and that routes will expand to Fort Worth, El Paso and Phoenix by year-end; annual mileage targets of 200–250 k and possible robotaxi entry post-2027 were reiterated.
    

**Hesai (HSAI) – 8 & 11 Sep**

-   Announced the plan to raise $475 million in a Hong Kong listing selling 17 million shares at HK228 (US$29.30). On 11th priced a global offering of 20 million class B shares at US27.11, shares rose on the announcements.
    

**NuScale Power (SMR) – 10 Sep**

-   Reported that its supply-chain partner Doosan is forging long-lead materials for 12 Power Modules, and outreach now includes data-center and AI customers seeking zero-carbon baseload power.
    

**SES.AI (SES) – 9 Sep**

-   Announced that the annual general meeting will take place on October 1st
    

**WeRide (WRD) – 11 Sep**

-   Began HD mapping in **Leuven, Belgium** for its autonomous **Robobus** service in partnership with De Lijn; road testing is slated for mid-September and commercial shuttles after January 2026, marking the firm’s first Western European deployment.
    

## Imminent Trades

I have split my Drone sector into three subsectors: Manufacturers, DaaS, and Counter Drone. I would like to have representation in all three. Currently, we have AIRO in the Manufacturing subsector, but have no coverage in the other two.

I have completed my research in the counter-drone sector and have three companies I would like to buy (two we have held before Ondas and DroneShield, the third is Electro Optic Systems). We did very well with both investments, and I think many subscribers still own them, which turned out to be an excellent move in Ondas, as it has put on another 30% since I took profits.

I was considering taking partial positions in all three. They are highly correlated and provide complementary, not competing, technology. Customers would likely build a counter UAV system layering the three technologies.

All three target companies have experienced substantial price increases over the last year and now have share prices above my fair value estimates.

I have decided to wait for a pullback (which may never come) or the arrival of new information that changes my fair value calculations before investing. The risks seem too high at the moment, and the dreaded hype cycle could be at play. I feel it is very likely I will miss out on this sector. Constant news flow will drive prices higher, but it does not really increase the likelihood of new orders or bigger profits.

I am currently evaluating the DaaS companies (five of them), and they have not seen the same price appreciation as the counter drone companies and probably offer a less risky way forward. I hope to invest in one next week. They are priced well below fair values and are not picking up the same press coverage, probably because they are focusing on mundane tasks like inspecting power lines rather than intercepting incoming Russian drones. (Check out the video on the [home page of Electro Optic](https://eos-aus.com/) to see what I mean)

The shares of the DaaS companies are not correlated and are moving independently in line with individual company news rather than the hype surrounding military drones at the moment.

I have completed my research on a recycling company, another area I follow closely, but do not currently have any exposure to. I expect to invest early next week. It meets all of the key criteria for an investment. The company is approaching commercialization, has proprietary emerging technology, and enormous growth potential.

It also has several risk factors. In this case, they are short of cash and have not yet completed their first full-scale recycling plant, so they are at the beginning of the commercialization stage.

The stock price has already undergone a hype cycle-type move and now appears to have settled into a lower range.
