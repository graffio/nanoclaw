Updated portfolio showing the trade in DroneShield. I decided to wait for the markets, cancelled the at-market order, and bought with a limit when the markets were open. 
When doing the DD into Droneshield, one thing struck me, they began as a US company and made the conscious decision to move to Australia. The CEO said this was about the availability and cost of engineering talent, plus the increased marketing of being a "non-US company". Until recently, I had never heard of this; every tech start-up went straight to the US, and being there was a huge selling point. EVTL has said the same thing about the cost of engineering talent and being able to sell outside of the US.
 It is also noticeable that more tech start-ups are choosing to IPO outside of the US, with Hong Kong being a preferred destination. In the past, the only place you could raise money was in the US.
It is a trend I intend to investigate, but we see the Chinese-based companies that we bought after their US IPO, like PONY, WeRide, and Hesai, putting their stock on the HK exchange. You can add NIO, BAIDU, Tencent, Alibaba, and Xiaomi to that list.
It could be the repeated threat of delisting, or the increased liquidity from Chinese citizens who are major investors.
It is also showing up with proposed IPOs Deepexi has launched in HK, and the robot companies I am tracking appear to be going the same way.
I am not sure of the root cause of this move, but it is happening and showing up in my tiny portfolio. Our non-US holdings have gone from 0% to 18% in the last 12 months. I don't have a strong view on the pros and cons of this, but I will follow the money. If founders decide to launch outside the US, I will continue to buy their stock despite the added costs.
I ended up paying nearly US$20 in charges and exchange fees to IBKR for the Droneshield trade, over 3% of the amount invested, and no doubt I will have the same cost on the way out. Another thing to keep an eye on.
