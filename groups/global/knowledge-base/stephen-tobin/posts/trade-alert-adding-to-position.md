### Q1 Highlights

Q1 represented a return to revenue growth after two quarters of contraction. $50.7 million was 21% higher than the Q1 2024 figure.

Revenue increased despite a 36% fall in communications products. Battery products increased 32%.

Management attributed the drop in communications to delays in large orders.

Gross margin is improving, which was a key justification for the Electrochem acquisition, as it provides vertical integration across multiple products. ULBI closed a manufacturing site, reducing costs as they could absorb the site's activities with the expanded footprint.

Electrochem cells can be used in many of the battery packs ULBI produces, helping with some tariff impacts. It also introduces a new line of products tailored to the ULBI business plan.

ULBI has a simple business plan that delivers a complicated business. They develop lithium-ion batteries that fit into existing devices to replace older non-lithium ones. For instance, military radio, rather than replacing a complete radio system to get the advantages of a lithium battery ULBI design, and makes a battery that fits. These are specific to each product and not available off the shelf.

ULBI has expanded this basic model through acquisition, introducing different battery chemistries and military communication devices, as well as server protection. Electrochem provides vertical integration with cell manufacture and new vertical markets in Bouys and sonar devices.

ULBI has been significantly impacted by tariffs due to its large imports of components from China. It is challenging for ULBI to change suppliers, as the majority of sales are to medical and defense customers, and any change would require re-certification. Management has been able to include tariff surcharges for new orders and despite this the order book is growing. It now stands at $95 million. Historically, ULBI has delivered its order book without significant cancellations.

They are expanding their product range beyond lithium into wearable batteries and medical devices. All ULBI products are highly engineered, mission-critical products that the Military and medical markets depend on.

Military electrical supply and ruggedized containers are forecast for significant growth this year. Ruggedized cases will likely begin booking revenue in Q2 but a slow ramp is expected. The new low-wattage amplifier products (the lightest available for warfighters) are expected to be in production later this year, and the new handheld communications product has just received its first orders.

The ThinCell battery for medical wearables is now in production, and management has been guided to a ramp-up later in the year, following the recent FDA approval.

The balance sheet remains strong with $70 million in liquidity (up $3 million over the quarter), and management has said they will be able to pay down the acquisition debt faster than previously expected due to strong forecasted cash generation over the next two years.
