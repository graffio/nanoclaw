## Detailed View

Aptiv PLC (APTV), a European-based global manufacturing company, looks significantly undervalued. I forecast a 100% upside for this supplier of the key enabling technology that will allow autonomous vehicles and robotaxis to dominate the future of the world's vehicle industry.

The 25 largest auto OEMs rely on Aptiv to design, manufacture and assemble the electronic architecture and computing capability that the new autonomous technology depends on.

New auto manufacturers are turning to Aptiv, “made in China for China” is an Aptiv tagline that has led to $7 billion dollars of new business arriving on the Aptiv China order book in Q4 2024 alone. Unlike previous years, this China business is from domestic automakers, not the legacy customers of Aptiv.

Aptiv has the balance sheet and cash flow generation ability to outmaneuver and outspend new technology entrants and the scale to make bolt-on acquisitions like Wind River that expand its capabilities. As the auto industry moves to a more connected, electric, autonomous product, it will increase the dollars per car it spends with Aptiv, leading to substantial revenue growth and a likely revenue/earnings surprise this year.

## Emerging Technology: Our bread and butter

Established legacy companies sometimes see the potential of emerging technology and position their businesses to capitalize on the disruption ahead. Aptiv is one such company.

Emerging technologies often unlock their true potential when they converge. Aptiv has strategically positioned its business to capitalize on the convergence of three automotive mega-trends: Safe, Green, and Connected.

By providing a comprehensive suite of sensors, controllers, wiring, compute capability, at-the-edge software, and advanced electric charging, Aptiv is empowering original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) to accelerate the delivery of new electric vehicle models, advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), and fully autonomous vehicles, solidifying its role as a key player in the evolving automotive landscape.

In the auto industry, price matters, and price needs scale. In this new industry phase, Aptiv has a scale beyond its immediate competition.

With 149 manufacturing sites and 11 technical centers spread over 49 different countries, they provide locally manufactured products to world-leading automotive customers in their three target markets: North America, China, and Europe.

The largest 25 automotive OEMs in the world are repeat customers. In 2024, 17 of the top 20 selling vehicles in the US, 17 of the top 20 in Europe, and 12 of the top 20 in China contained Aptiv products.

## Aptiv Business Lines

Aptiv organizes into two segments:

**Advanced Safety and User Experience**: Provides electronic active safety and in-cabin user experience technology, including sensors (LiDAR, radar, ultrasound), perception systems (full stack point cloud software), control units (HPC capability), vehicle connectivity (over-the-air connectivity and update), and autonomous driving technologies (complete autonomous package custom designed, manufactured, and assembled) to fit any vehicle.

**Signal and Power Solutions**: provides complete design, manufacture, and assembly of a vehicle's electrical architecture, including wiring, connectors, harnesses, electrical power, and distribution systems.

In 2022, **Aptiv acquired** **Wind River Systems** for $3.5 billion, a global leader in delivering software over the air to the edge for critical intelligent systems. This acquisition adds leadership in vehicle-to-vehicle and vehicle-to-infrastructure connectivity to Aptiv and opens new vertical markets in defense and aerospace.

## New Technology New Business

In Q4 2024, Aptiv announced record bookings of $10.1 billion. They also announced two full system awards for the Gen 6 ADAS platforms, which include cabin sensing and Wind River embedded software, in the EMEA region and multiple awards with Mahindra in India.

In Q1 2025, Gen 6 awards were received from a luxury German OEM and two large Japanese OEMs.

Q1 2025 (investor presentation) showed awards from a host of Chinese EV makers, including, including Cherie, Zeeker, GAC, Chang’an, Xiaomi, and Great Wall.

The Gen 6 Platform integrates sensing and perception using radar, LiDAR, cameras, ultrasonic, and interior sensors. A central high-performance computer fuses the data and executes ADAS algorithms. This scalable system supports over-the-air updates and offers supply chain flexibility by being sensor and software-agnostic.

## China a focus for change

Aptiv began selling to global OEMs in China, but revenues dropped as domestic OEMs started to take market share. They reported a significant drop in revenue from one large customer in 2024. Aptiv is progressing with the “made in China for China” policy.

Q1 2025 reports New China business of $7 billion, but most importantly, 70% of that was with domestic manufacturers.

## Growth and Constraints

Aptiv is already at scale, has significant penetration of the market, and as a result, its sales will vary with the global production of automobiles. However, the three trends of Safe, green, and connected mean that Aptiv is receiving more revenue per car sold and is able to deliver growth even when the total market is stagnant.

In Q1 2025, vehicle production was down 6% in North America, but Aptiv revenue dropped only 2%. In Europe, production dropped 7%, and Aptiv was down 6%.

China production was up 10%, whereas Aptiv only rose 2% due to its lack of penetration with domestic manufacturers who have taken share from global OEMs. I expect to see this change in 2025 as the recently announced platform wins with domestic manufacturers begin to be delivered.

## Autonomy and Robotaxis

The autonomous vehicle sector, particularly robotaxis, is poised for substantial expansion in the coming half-decade. The automotive industry is trending towards increased connectivity, enhanced safety technologies, and greater electrification.

The future leaders in the robotaxi market remain uncertain. Key questions include the viability of Tesla's camera-centric approach, the potential for Tesla owners to lease their vehicles, and the competitive landscape between established ride-hailing giants like Uber and emerging players such as Pony.ai.

Alternatively, the existing automotive hierarchy might persist, with major manufacturers like Toyota and Stellantis maintaining their dominance by supplying autonomous fleets to traditional taxi companies.

The eventual winners stand to gain significant financial rewards, offering substantial investment opportunities. Selecting losers carries the risk of total capital loss.

**One thing we do know** is that whichever manufacturers win the robotaxi race, their vehicles will be full of Aptiv parts, components, computing, and software.

Aptiv has products for the entire market. We are already long Hesai for LiDARs and were long Arbe for its radar; however, I think Aptiv is taking the OEM deals Arbe hoped for. (I think the German luxury OEM discussed earlier is the same one Arbe was targeting). Aptiv does not manufacture LiDAR, but they do manufacture radar.

The fully integrated but open solution from Aptiv seems an easier product to buy, it is from a known and proven supplier with local manufacturing and engineering support for all global OEMS.

## Notable Business Developments

**Divesting EDS** in January, Aptiv announced plans to divest its Electrical Distribution Systems division. Aptiv is preparing to float the subsidiary via an IPO. I will watch this story develop.

**Motional:** The JV with Hyundai is progressing towards live testing this year, aiming for a US service launch next year. Aptiv now holds a 15% stake (down from 50% last year) following a restructuring (resulting in a $600 million gain), a strategic move to avoid customer competition and reduce costs.

## Financial Situation

**Aptiv has a rock-solid balance** sheet despite its high debt load. Aptiv ended Q1 2025 with $1.1 billion in cash and liquidity of $3.4 billion. They had an operating cash flow of $273 million and paid down $573 million of their debt. They have accelerated their debt repayment plan, released in 2024, with debt falling from $9.5 billion to $7.8 billion. [Aptiv ran up its debt and suspended](https://www.aptiv.com/en/newsroom/article/aptiv-provides-covid-19-related-business-update) its dividend in 2020 in response to the COVID pandemic.

**Share Buy Backs:** Aptiv did not reinstate its dividend, buying back shares instead. In Q1 2025, Aptiv completed the $3 billion share repurchase program, reducing share count by 18%

## Price Target

**Fair Value**: I have built a 3-statement mathematical model. It has two key drivers, revenue and operating costs. The model gives a fair value for shares between $107 and $151. I will explain the model below.

**Aptiv's 2025 Outlook: Poised for a Potential Revenue Surge**

-   **Initial Guidance vs. Reality:** In Q4 2024, Aptiv's 2025 projections assumed a 3% global decline in vehicle manufacturing. However, early results suggest a significantly different picture.
    
-   **North America:** Aptiv anticipated a 5% drop in North American production but projected a 4% revenue increase due to growth with key customers, particularly the "Detroit 3," which account for 50% of their regional revenue. If production remains flat, Aptiv could see an additional $400 million in revenue.
    
-   **Europe:** While forecasting a 3% decline in production due to the transition to electric vehicles, Aptiv still expected 1% revenue growth.
    
-   **China:** Initially predicting flat production and a 1% revenue decline due to increased competition from domestic OEMs, Aptiv's outlook has changed. Q1 2025 saw a 10% production increase in China, alongside new deals with domestic manufacturers, shifting their customer balance. Q4 2024 China sales grew 4%, with local OEM sales jumping 25%, resulting in a 53% local OEM revenue mix.
    
-   **Q1 2025 Results Exceed Expectations:** Aptiv's Q1 2025 forward guidance was $4.6 billion, while the actual result was $4.8 billion.
    
-   **Full Year Guidance & Potential Beat:** Aptiv's full-year 2025 guidance is $20 billion. However, to meet this, sales would need to drop 7% in the second half, which seems unlikely.
    
-   **Revised Revenue Forecast:** HSI, a respected industry forecaster, predicts a 20% growth in EV sales for 2025, led by China. This suggests Aptiv's revenue could reach $21.5 billion, a 9% increase from 2024.
    
-   **Conservative Projections:** The Mathematical model projects 9% revenue growth for 2025, but this may be conservative, considering Aptiv's increasing content per vehicle and growing presence in the connected and autonomous vehicle market.
    

## **Cost Efficiency**

Aptiv is aggressively improving its cost structures, reducing workforce and debt while rotating manufacturing facilities into the best cost regions.

-   **Debt Reduction:** Aptiv has significantly reduced its debt levels and remains committed to further reductions by utilizing cash generated from operations, thereby lowering interest expenses.
    
-   **Operational Improvements:** Ongoing shifts to more cost-effective operations are driving down manufacturing costs. This is evidenced by an 80 basis point increase in operating income margin in Q1 2025, following a 50 basis point improvement in Q4 2024.
    
-   **Future Margin Potential:** While the financial model does not currently factor in further margin improvements, Aptiv is expected to continue improving its operating costs.
    
-   **Note on Free Cash Flow (FCFF):** The projected drop in FCFF for 2025 is primarily due to assumptions regarding capital expenditures (CAPEX) and working capital needs, both decreased in 2024.
    

**Key Model Assumptions:**

-   **Working Capital:** The net working capital schedule is based on a Days Inventory Outstanding of 52.9 days and a Days Sales Outstanding of 67.1 days (2024 figures).
    
-   **Depreciation & Amortization (D&A) and Capital Expenditure (CAPEX):** D&A is calculated at 14%, and CAPEX at 11%, both as a percentage of opening Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E) using three-year averages.
    
-   **Model Sensitivity:** The model is not significantly sensitive to other changes in assumptions.
    
-   **Tariffs:** Aptiv is not guiding to any significant impact but does serve much of North America from Mexico. They have discussed moving heavily automated production into the US.

## Conclusion

Aptiv stands at the forefront of the evolving automotive landscape, poised to capitalize on the convergence of intelligent vehicles, robotaxis, and edge computing. Their robust financial health, strategic acquisitions like Wind River, and a growing foothold in key markets—especially China—position them for significant revenue growth. With a strong order book, deep relationships with major OEMs, and innovative technology driving the automotive revolution, Aptiv represents a compelling investment opportunity. Our model indicates a fair value range of $107 to $151, suggesting substantial upside potential within the next 12 months and beyond.

I give a "Strong-buy" rating to Aptiv (APTV) and will add it to our portfolio.
