## Trading

I made 5 trades in February, increasing positions in defence stocks and adding three names to the portfolio, one in Quantum Computing and two in Psychedelic Drug therapy. It is the first time I have invested in Psychedelic drugs, despite reporting on the sector for 3 years, and follows the key strategy of investing in emerging technology in the year before commercialisation begins.

Quantum is a staple of the portfolio, and I may well add an additional position next month. Quantum remains our most successful sector, with a 100% success rate and a total return of 124% on invested capital.

The portfolio holds  a record 26 companies. I do expect to make some changes next month, including some adjustments to positions where we hold more than one company in a sector. I similarly expect to make some changes in sectors where our holding no longer looks to be the best bet going forward, and I will rotate some sectors into Europe.

A key part of the plan is to ride the initial wave of commercialisation, and some of our holdings seem to have completed that first wave higher, meaning we should book profits even though they are not as large as we would have liked, and move that money into companies just at the beginning of the wave.
