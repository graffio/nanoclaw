## Reply: DWave leveraged ETF / leverage strategy
*Thread 761f36fb-a927-42ec-9067-c6b15163d6d6*

**Context (Jack Nesje):**
> I found this article for you about DWave Quantum being cut in half — can a leveraged ETF help bulls?

**Stephen Tobin:**
Leverage is a dangerous game, it increases both the up and the downs. I have tried to incorporate it into my trading but never with success. I did report on my most recent attempt in a series of weekly updates. The problem is I trade such risky assets that they often see big moves on either direction. When they go higher leverage is great but when they go lower it's a disaster. Right now we are seeing drawdown and some stocks are down 40%, if they had 3x leverage they would be down 120% and my equity would be gone. Of course this could be the turning point but I am not going to risk my capital on it. Right now I am showing a loss but I have over \$5K in cash and \$12k in stocks, if I had leveraged the trades I might have nothing left!

## Reply: 200-day SMA break — bear market probability
*Thread f255dd42-066a-4e2b-8737-34b09caeda26*

**Context (Jack Nesje):**
> Hey Stephen, any further thoughts on this after seeing your 1 month issue? I would love to hear your opinion on the breaking under average 200 even though you don't believe in TA

**Stephen Tobin:**
You are right that I am not a believer in technical analysis however that doesn't mean I don't follow it. It is definitely true that many people use it, including institutions to gain an insight into the market conditions and a break of the 200 day SMA shows the market is not advancing. The fact that so many people depend on technicals makes them important whether I think they are true or not. The reason I don't use technical analysis in my trading is there is not a direct relationship between a technical event and what comes next. For instance the S&P has broken below the average more than 150 times but we have probably only had a single figure number of bear markets. So one thing does not necessarily imply the other. Of course that does not mean that I discount the prospect of a bear market, as I said in the piece the probability is with a pullback at the moment. There have only been 3 significant bear markets since the S and P began and the other 150+ breaks have been short whipsaw events. It is up to everyone to make there own decisions, but my view is the drop below the 200 adds weight to the potential for a bear market along with the other evidence we have, oil problem private credit and war, but on its own it is not significant.
