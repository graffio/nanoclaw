## Notes

I am reviewing a couple of holdings in light of news from last week and will decide on Monday if I need to take any action. What happens between Iran and Israel over the weekend will be important; however, wars are rarely bad for business after the initial drops in share price.

**ARBE** announced a $50 million shelf registration, putting pressure on shares.

**ASPI** announced a 4-year supply agreement for Gadolinium-160, starting in 2026, with a minimum contract value of $1 million a year. I will take an opportunity to add to ASPI when it arrives and following the 20% fall in the last couple of days that opportunity could be close.

**BYRN** had its price target increased by Roth and Freedom when it announced revenue above expectations for Q2.

**CDRE** shares fell when BoA reduced its rating from Buy to neutral citing organic growth limitations and pricing constraints. BoA uses a multiple of EBITDA for valuation and changed CDRE's multiple from x17 to x14, but did not alter their revenue or EBITDA forecasts. It always surprises me that Wall Street creates these multiples and then changes them to another made-up number, implying that the company is worth less than it was.

**EH** began test flights in Abu Dhabi in collaboration with the Abu Dhabi Investment Office.

**PONY** entered a partnership with Xihu group to deploy 1,000 robotaxis in Shenzen.

**SES** was in the news with an article in The Elec (Korea) suggesting they were withdrawing from EV batteries. Still, the CEO was interviewed by Deutsche Bank later in the week and clarified that they would contract manufacture EV batteries if they received the expected orders later in the year.

It was a confusing situation that led to a drop in share price. Still, I will hold for the time being, in the interview with Deutsche the CEO discussed plans for a new marketing department and the plans for expansion into robotics with the 2170 battery plus the plans for the drone market using cells made from the manufacturing lines that were destined to produce EV batteries. He also said the changes to the manufacturing model would extend their cash runway to 2028.

**WRD** announced on the 10th that they are bidding to run autonomous buses in partnership with Handal Indah. Although not well known outside of Malaysia/Singapore, Handal are the major supplier of crossborder bus routes into Singapore.

**Potential Adjustments**

The sharp sell-off on Thursday and Friday has broken the pattern in several of the stocks I am holding. I am reluctant to close trades after a short-term drop, but am even more reluctant to take a substantial hit to the portfolio. I will watch closely but not act hastily.

**QBTS** is heading back towards a previously identified buying area. I have shared the chart below before. If the price gets down towards $13 I will look again but news from IBM last week has me a little concerned for QBTS. My bullish case for QBTS has always hinged on my view that they will be the only quantum computing company for the next decade.

I don't believe a word the CEO of IONQ says about their progress (but as he sold $100 million in shares this week, he has made infinitely more money telling his story than I have, so all credit to him). RGTI has been quite open, stating that they have 7-10 years of research to complete, and MSFT and GOOG both provide similar timelines.

This week, IBM announced that it will have a fault-tolerant computer by 2029, and it has already begun building it. IBM has not published any papers or scientific studies suggesting a breakthrough that would enable this to happen, and some of their quantum claims have previously been disproven. However, they are the largest investor in this field, with more quantum engineers spending a larger budget than anyone else.

IBM has been working on low-density parity check error correction. They have released some of the work they were doing, suggesting they were outperforming the more standard surface code error correction in certain circumstances, but a significant breakthrough would have been needed to make the machine they are proposing.

IBM says the “Starling” will be built in Poughkeepsie, New York, and will be capable of performing more operations than 20,000 of today's supercomputers.

If that is true, QBTS is heading to zero, as the IBM machine will be able to do everything the D-Wave can, plus a great deal more. We already knew IONQ was heading to zero; its tech is a dead end, and I was worried about RGTI. However, I thought QBTS had at least a decade-long first-mover advantage.

This is another example of why I never become a believer in a company or show any loyalty. Investing is about making money, not supporting a company or believing in one.
