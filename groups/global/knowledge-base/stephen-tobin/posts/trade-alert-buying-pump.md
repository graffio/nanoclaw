# Trade Alert: Buying PUMP

*Bottom Fishing Old Energy*

I will place a mid-price order on IBKR for 48 shares. The portfolio currently has a total equity of $9,500 so it represents about 4% of the total. Following the closures of HESAI and QBTS, the portfolio is very cash-heavy, with almost 47% of the total in cash.

I discovered ProPetro (PUMP) while researching Aris Water (ARIS) and decided to invest after recognizing its potential. Although I don't support fracking due to its environmental impact, I focus on investment rewards rather than moral considerations.

PUMP services major oil and gas companies in the Permian Basin, specializing in fracking fleets. Their new electric-powered fleets, particularly the FORCE lineup, are gaining traction, with over 75% of their fleet now electric. This innovative approach includes a recent lease agreement with Exxon for additional FORCE fleets that generate electricity from natural gas.

What truly interests me is PUMP's pivot to electricity generation. They plan to leverage their knowledge to provide reliable energy to remote drilling stations and have established a new division, PROPWR, to sell electricity. PUMP has ordered 140 Megawatts of generating equipment, with plans to increase capacity to 200 Megawatts by 2026, backed by $104 million in financing.

With management guiding to $350,000 of EBITDA per megawatt annually, the expected EBITDA for the initial order is $49 million. The timeline for order to revenue is promising—operational by H2 2025—and the equipment's mobile nature allows flexible deployment across the Permian.

For context, an OKLO nuclear SMR will produce only 15 Megawatts, has a 4-year permitting cycle, and the first approval documents have not yet been submitted.

PUMP said they are engaging with data centers and industrial sites to further grow the division. The potential for growth in this energy sector aligns with industry demand for reliable, low-cost electricity.

After a poor year in which revenue and margins dropped due to the underutilization of its fracking assets, PUMP is currently at a low point.

The price will move with oil and gas but there are positives on the horizon with the new fracking units and LNG exports from the US.

My mathematical model suggests this might be a ten-bagger and the chart looks very promising with a potential low in place.
