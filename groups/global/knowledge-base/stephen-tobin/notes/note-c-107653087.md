# Note -- April 10, 2025

I am rethinking my view on renewable energy. I initially thought the election of Mr Trump would slow the adoption of clean alternatives but following the Trump tariff shock and his unpredictable nature I am changing my mind.

Trump's tariffs and renewable energy:

President Trump's recent tariff announcements and the subsequent international responses have injected significant instability into the global economic landscape. While a 90-day pause has been implemented for some tariff increases, a universal 10% tariff remains, creating uncertainty that is particularly detrimental to the clean energy sector, which relies on long-term investments and stable market conditions.

These tariffs pose immediate challenges for the U.S. renewable energy industry but they may accelerate the global transition to renewable energy sources outside of the United States. 

The U.S. has destabilized the existing global energy order, nations may seek greater energy independence as a result. A drive for energy security wil lead to increased investment in domestic renewable energy sources.

This shift is not driven by climate concerns but by a desire to mitigate geopolitical and macroeconomic risks. Countries are realizing that while importing renewable energy components like solar panels requires initial investment, it offers long-term energy security, unlike the continuous dependence on fossil fuel imports. 

However, the U.S. faces a different scenario. The tariffs significantly increase costs for U.S. renewable energy projects, potentially delaying or halting development. 

The U.S. will likely experience a slower and more challenging path towards renewable energy adoption compared to other nations. The tariffs create an unfavorable economic environment for U.S. renewable energy businesses, making projects less financially viable.  Whilst making their adoption almost mandatory for companies lacking a domestic supply of oil. 

The U.S. risks being left behind, resulting in economic losses and accelerated global warming.
