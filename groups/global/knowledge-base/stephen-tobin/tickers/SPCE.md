## Company Overview

Virgin Galactic is a space tourism company developing sub-orbital and orbital spaceplanes. The company aims to offer commercial space flights for tourists but remains far from profitability and has faced repeated delays and dilution events.

**Business model:** Future revenue from commercial space tourism flights (sub-orbital and eventually orbital). Currently pre-revenue with ongoing capital needs.

**Key concerns:** The company has diluted shareholders repeatedly — in December 2025, SPCE dropped 16% on news of retiring debt by selling shares, pushing out maturity of existing notes. Stephen's view: "far from profitability" and requiring ongoing funding.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis:** Space tourism is a long-term growth sector. SPCE was purchased as part of Stephen's broader space portfolio alongside LUNR and MDA. Target price \$6 (from ~\$3.21 entry).

**Thesis deterioration:** SPCE was consistently listed as a "pressing concern" due to distance from profitability. In Dec 2025, a 16% drop on dilution news was noted but Stephen held. By March 2026, the stock was classified among those "falling on no news" but also among positions of "pressing concern" due to funding needs.

**Exit thesis (Mar 2026):** Oil crisis / Middle East escalation. "Requires funding, dependent on discretionary spending." SPCE was one of four positions sold on 2026-03-19 as part of portfolio repositioning for the changed economic environment.
