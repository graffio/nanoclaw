## Company Overview

NRX Pharmaceuticals is a nano-cap pharma company founded in 2015 by the Javitt brothers pursuing a dual strategy: FDA approval of ketamine-based drugs (NRX-100, NRX-101) and building a healthcare services business (Hope Therapeutics) providing neuroplastic treatments including TMS.

**Drug pipeline:**

- NRX-100 (Ketafree) — preservative-free ketamine IV drip:
  - Generic pathway (ANDA): Targeting the \$750M US ketamine market. FDA set July 29 date for acceptance of Generic Drug User Fee Amendment. No new phase trials required. Capacity to scale immediately to 1 million vials/month. Commercial batches already manufactured. Only preservative-free ketamine on market (no BZT toxicity concerns)
  - New Drug Application (NDA): First FDA-approved pharmaceutical labelled for acute suicidal ideation. Fast-track designation awarded 2025. FDA accepted NDA in January 2026 — "ahead of CMPS" and "the only company with an accepted NDA in the psychedelic space." FDA accepting real-world data (70,000 patients from Osmind) in lieu of randomized trials

- NRX-101 — oral tablet (D-cycloserine + Lurasidone):
  - NMDA receptor agonist enhancing brain plasticity. Phase 1 showed similar efficacy to lurasidone but statistically significant reduction in akathisia (dangerous side effect linked to suicide)
  - ONE-D protocol: NRX-101 + TMS achieves similar results to Stanford SAINT protocol but in a single day vs 5 days, without needing expensive fMRI scanner
  - Patents: composition of matter to 2033

- NRX-100 patents: preservative-free formulation to 2045

**Hope Therapeutics (clinic business):**

- Following DaVita business model — integrated national chain of clinics
- Acquired Dura Medical and Cohen & Associates in Q3 2025 (first revenue: \$242K from 3 weeks)
- January 2026: Strategic partnership with Neurocare Group AG — over 400 clinics with TMS machines already in place, aiming to place treatment center within driving distance of every American by year-end 2026
- Will administer NRX-101, TMS, and other neuroplastic treatments

**Finances — extremely precarious:**

- Negative shareholder equity. Going-concern notice on accounts
- \$7.2M cash (Sept 2025), operating burn ~\$1M/month — less than 6 months runway
- \$11M short-term assets vs \$40M short-term liabilities
- Related-party transactions: CEO's brother (Daniel Javitt) owns IP to NRX-101 via Glytech; CEO's son receives \$200K for IT services
- Major \$27M financing package collapsed when investor failed to pay (catalyst for Neurocare deal)
- Auditors dismissed late 2025 without explanation
- Nasdaq non-compliance letter for failing to hold 2025 annual meeting
- Anson Funds providing critical financing via senior secured notes and equity conversion rights

**History:** Originally developing NRX-101, pivoted to COVID-19 treatment in 2020 (Zyesami failed FDA approval, stock crashed from \$600 to \$1.75), pivoted back to NMDA research, then added ketamine and Hope Therapeutics pivots.
