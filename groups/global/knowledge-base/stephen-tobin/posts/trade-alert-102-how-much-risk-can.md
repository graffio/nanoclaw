# Trade Alert #102: How Much Risk Can You Bear?

*Potential Twenty Baggers are never a Sure Thing, Risk and Reward are always linked.*

Risk and reward go together; we all love it when we get the big win, a huge return in hardly any time at all. These are the top four short-term winners we have had, and all subscribers love these gains.

These are our 6 biggest losses, you can see that some of these losses came quickly, DroneShield lost nearly 60% in three weeks and LUNR over 40% in ten days.

That is the nature of my trading plan; it can and does deliver exceptional results, but it can also deliver big losses, and it can deliver both very quickly.

I am happy with risk; you can see this from the tables. Note Trade 29 in the loser table. I lost 41% in two months, then shortly after bought the same company again, and this time it delivered nearly 200%.

I buy small companies with new technology. I am hoping to ride the first wave of commercialization, but in doing so, I am trying to pick winners amongst unproven technology, unproven management teams, and companies often existing on the edge of a precipice, no cash no revenue and in the majority of the markets view no future.

I have seen from the churn in subscribers after each loss that some people just cannot handle the downside of risk. I see that after each big win, people are desperate for another one as subscriber numbers soar.

If you are going to pursue a strategy like mine that actively seeks out risk you have to be able to cope with the losses more than you must delight in the wins and this next trade is going to test everyone’s resolve.

I am going to invest in a Nano Cap company that is in negative equity and has an active going-concern notice on its accounts. Those accounts are full of related-party transactions with the founders’ families, and I have seen the company flip-flop between business models. They do not have enough cash to last the next six months and I forecast dilution of 400%.

Despite all this, I think these NanoCap shares are going to go up 2,000%, but I know there is a chance it could go to zero. In fact, since I started writing this article, its shares have dropped from $2.50 to $1.75, yet I think the shares are worth $50 each.

I am buying for the upside, but I am happy with the risk, you should think long and hard if you decide to follow this trade and the most important question is “will I be OK if I lose this money?”

## Trade Alert 102: Buying NRX Pharmaceuticals (NRXP)

**NOTES:** NRX is a mess; its balance sheet is in tatters, the accounts include a going-concern note, and it has negative shareholder equity. It has $11 million in short-term assets and $40 million in short-term liabilities.

The company, founded in 2015 by the Javitt brothers, has already undergone three business pivots. Originally, they were looking at NRX-101, a drug the brothers developed. Jonathon Javit, the CEO, is a long-term healthcare veteran, and Daniel Javitt is a recognized leader in NMDA receptor research.

In 2020, they tried to pivot to treating COVID-19 patients with respiratory problems. They launched via a SPAC in 2021, promising a Covid cure, and the stock price reached a high of $600 (today it is at $1.75). Their COVID drug, Zyesami, failed to gain FDA approval, sending the stock crashing and forcing the brothers back to the original NMDA research.

In 2023, they secured some funds to help them move NRX-101 towards approval, and in 2025 began another pivot, launching Hope Therapeutics, which they hope will become the clinics where people receive NRX-101.

Last year, they pivoted to a psychedelic drug, ketamine, with their own formulation (NRX-100), undergoing FDA consideration 

On top of all of this, they have insufficient funds, a 10-K full of related-party transactions, a market cap of only $50 million, and are competing with some of the most well-funded companies in the world.

I think they will need to raise $200 million from the markets, most of it this year, meaning perhaps 400% dilution, and until September 2025, they had never booked any revenue.

I think you get the idea of how risky this is, but I am going to take a full-size position of $600 (3% of equity), breaking my usual rule of half-size positions for Microcaps and high-risk bets because the potential is great, the catalysts at hand, and the outlook is enormously de-risked.

If you don’t like risk, are averse to drawdown or volatility, then this is probably not one for you.

## NRX Pharmaceuticals

NRX is pursuing a dual go-to-market strategy. On the one hand, it is seeking approval of its novel compounds NRX-100 and NRX-101 whilst at the same time building a healthcare services business (Hope Therapeutics) that will provide care for patients with mental health issues using NRX-101 and other mechanisms.

Both of these business models have real potential, but they do need to be looked at separately as they have very different risk and financial profiles. If and when the two businesses become established, a sum-of-the-parts methodology would be the best way to value the company.

## Vertical Integration

Management has followed DaVita's business model (explicitly mentioned in earnings reports). DaVita works as an integrated provider of help for Kidney disease patients providing a national chain of clinics with treatment delivered under standard protocols.

Hope Therapeutics will provide neuroplastic treatments, including its own drugs when approved, as well as TMS.

The great benefit of this strategy is it allows NRX to move towards cash flow generation independent of the drug approval process and capture value across the entire patient journey.

## Drug Development

NRX-100 (Ketamine IV drip)

The company is pursuing a dual-path regulatory strategy for this drug. They are trying to get it approved in two completely different ways, firstly as an entirely new drug in a new category, and secondly as a generic replacement for existing approved ketamine applications.

The New Drug Application aims to be the first FDA-approved pharmaceutical labelled specifically for acute suicidal. Currently, nothing exists for doctors to use when a patient appears at an ER in crisis and attempting suicide.

The more immediate prospect is the drug as a Generic ketamine.

Trademarked as KETAFREE NRX is pursuing an Abbreviated New Drug Application ANDA is the way companies get a generic drug approved. It’s abbreviated because there is no need to go through the years of clinical trials that the original drug had to. The FDA has set a July 29th date for announcing acceptance of this Generic Drug User Fee Amendment.

Ketamine is already used in many areas; it is approved as an anesthetic and for pain relief, but all current versions of the drug contain the preservative BZT, which is believed to be toxic. Ketafree does not contain any preservatives, and the company will be addressing the $750 million US market with its product when approved. The acceptance of the ANDA is the final step in the approval process, meaning they could see substantial revenue from this product in H2 2026.

NRX has said it has the capacity to scale immediately to 1 million vials per month and that commercial registration batches have already been manufactured. The US has ongoing shortages of ketamine products and the product aligns with both the onshoring of manufacturing policies and federal priorities of removing potentially toxic additives.

Ketafree will compete directly with existing approved indications in anesthesia and pain.

NRX has applied to have BZT banned from all ketamine products, but even if that citizen petition is unsuccessful, the removal of potential toxic compounds, combined with the reliability of supply due to US manufacturing facilities, suggests this drug will be an immediate success.

Acceptance of the ANDA de-risked this drug and application and the FDA said that no new phase trials are required for this use case.

## The New Drug Application [NDA]

The same NRX-100 drug discussed above, but NRX is seeking approval for it be used in emergency room-type treatment of patients in crisis with suicidal ideation.

The drug was awarded fast-track designation for this condition in 2025, and the FDA accepted the NDA in January, which puts them ahead of CMPS and means they are the only company with an accepted NDA in the psychedelic space.

The target market was expanded from the original restrictive Bipolar Depression to include Major Depressive Disorder with Suicidality. The only approved Ketamine drug approved for depression, Spravato, is specifically not available for these patients with a warning on the label.

The FDA has said it is open to accepting real-world data for this drug application, telling management that no further phase II or III studies are required. This acceptance is the de-risker for this NDA.

The FDA will accept real-world data for drugs treating serious, high-mortality conditions when randomized trials are virtually impossible. Nobody would consider giving a placebo to a patient in such a crisis and reporting if they killed themselves or not.

The real-world data, obtained from Osmind, include 70,000 patients and show that ketamine IV drips have demonstrated rapid and durable responses in suicidality.

I think the drug will be approved this year for both use cases, probably as a generic drug in July and perhaps the end of the year for use as an emergency treatment for acute suicidal ideation.

It will be the only pure ketamine available, the only pharmaceutical approved for suicidal ideation, manufactured in the US and available in volume.

## NRX-101

This is a very different drug, and its approval will be linked to the Hope Therapeutics offering and a recent agreement with Neurocare.

NRX-101 is a low dose of the active compound D-cycloserine. D-cycloserine is already approved as an antibiotic however it is used as a “if everything else fails” drug because of its significant side effects on the central nervous system. The drug is also a proven NMDA receptor agonist. These receptors are crucial for the brain’s ability to learn and make new connections. When combined with therapy it has been shown to allow patients to recover from conditions especially OCD, anxiety and phobias as it allows the brain to solidify new lessons learned.

NRX are seeking to have NRX-101 approved in conjunction with its treatment protocol based on the Apollo Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation device. The company reports a 87% response rate to treatment and a 72% remission rate for patients with moderate depression.

## Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS)

TMS uses coils placed against the scalp to deliver electrical pulses that attempt to alter thought patterns. It is a fairly well-established protocol that has seen some success.

A major breakthrough came with the FDA approval of the SAINT protocol (owned by Magnis Medical, still in early-stage funding), which uses an fMRI map of a patient’s brain to deliver a massive dose of TMS to a specific spot over 5 days of treatment.

SAINT is approved for depression relief and looks to have a response rate of around 70%. This is an extremely expensive treatment, clinics require a 3T MRI scanner, a TMS machine capable of delivering the dose to a specific part of the brian and the Magnus software after that they can only treat one or two patients a week per Magnus TMS machine, a machine far more expensive than standard TMS machines, because of the need for 10 sessions a day for five days for each patient.

NRX has developed a product, the “ONE-D” protocol, with a similar success rate to the SAINT therapy but uses NRX-101 to increase neural plasticity, allowing treatment to be completed in a single day. Clinics do not need the MRI scanning machine nor the rather expensive Magnus software.

Because of costs SAINT has been restricted to less than 20 clinics in the whole of the US, they are all centers of medical excellence like Stanford University.

NRX incorporated Hope Therapeutics at the start of 2024 and it acts as the commercial engine of the company. Functioning as a roll up vehicle for existing psychiatric practices to build a national footprint.

After acquiring Dura Medical and Cohen &Associates in Q3 2025 the company generated its first revenue (delivered with profit). The acquisition of clinics is continuing, but a 2026 partnership is a game-changer.

In January, Hope announced a strategic partnership with Neurocare Group AG to integrate their clinic network and deploy ONE-D across the US, aiming to place a treatment center within driving distance of every American by the end of this year. Neurocare has over 400 clinics with a TMS machine already in place, all they need is approval of NRX-101.

The clinics will administer a fixed dose of NRX-101, which is a tablet containing D-Cycloserine and Lurasidone.

Lurasidone is the current standard of care but has a dangerous side effect called akasthisia, a psychomotor agitation often linked to suicide.

In the first phase 3 trial NRX-101 showed similar efficacy to lurasidone but a statistically significant reduction in akathisia. NRX is hoping for accelerated approval based on this safety advantage. The FDA has recently changed policy on Phase III trials, now requiring only one rather than the previous two.

In late 2025, NRX released data showing that NRX-101 can double the effectiveness of TMS, leading to the efficacy of the ONE-D product.

The current standard of care using TMS and Lurasidone requires 6 weeks of daily treatment and delivers results similar to those of both the SAINT protocol and the ONE-D product.

## Patents

NRX-101 has composition of matter patents lasting to 2033 and NRX-100 (IV) has patents on preservative free formulation out to 2045. It ensures that NRX will have a durable competitive advantage, a significant first mover advantage and a ready market.

## Finances

Saying NRX has had financial issues would be very kind.

In 2025, a major $27 million financing package intended to fund Hope Therapeutics' growth was terminated when the investor failed to pay up. This is generally accepted as being the catalyst for the deal with Neurocare.

I found two further collapsed deals in the accounts relating to attempted acquisitions of clinics.

## Management

Although not controlled according to Nasdaq rules, the company is effectively controlled by its founder, Jonathan Javitt, and his brother, Daniel, who own 15% of the shares. The company also has significant related-party transactions and some issues regarding IP.

Glytech, wholly owned by Daniel Javitt, owns the IP to NRX-101 and NRX pays Glytech for its use. Zachary Javitt, the son of the CEO, receives $200K of IT services to the company. (We saw this exact setup with Electrovaya)

The company dismissed its auditors in late 2025 without explanation and in January this year received a non-compliance letter from Nasdaq for failing to hold its 2025 Annual meeting. This meeting has been scheduled for March.

In late 2025, management issued a going-concern notice because it lacked the cash to fund operations without significant capital raises.

Anson Funds provided critical financing last year, enabling some toxic debt to be retired, but Anson now has significant sway through senior secured notes and equity conversion rights. So far, they have been very supportive partners in the pivot from pure R&D to early commercial progress.

## Conclusion

The recent regulatory progress of this company has moved it from a Zombie operation to one with huge potential. They have a realistic chance of positive net income this year if they get their drugs approved on the timeline I have laid out, and if both drugs are approved, the clinic structure and manufacturing facilities are in place to roll out the offering quickly.

The timeline is another concern; the FDA seems chaotic at the moment. The recent Moderna flu vaccine rejection, followed a week later by an acceptance letter, is just a symptom of the way the agency was first gutted and then taken over by political managers rather than medical ones.
