## Company Overview

Voyager Technologies is a US-based "neo prime" — a new-generation defense technology prime built through acquisitions. IPO'd in June 2025. Operates three synergistic segments:

1. **Defense & National Security** — Controllable solid propulsion, signals intelligence, advanced guidance, navigation, and control systems. Largest and fastest-growing division. Aligned with NGI (Next Generation Interceptor) and Golden Dome programs.

2. **Space Solutions** — End-to-end space mission engineering, hardware and software systems, low-Earth orbit to deep space. Operates Bishop's Lock, the only permanent privately owned module on the ISS. Largest commercial user of the ISS (300+ satellites deployed, 1,000+ missions).

3. **Starlab Space Stations** — 61%-owned JV for a commercial replacement for the ISS. Partners: Airbus, Mitsubishi, MDA Space, Palantir, Space Applications Services. First to complete critical design review (Jan 2025). Launch contract signed with SpaceX Starship. Commercial payload already sold out. Forecast: \$4B revenue and \$1.5B FCF once operational. 30-year life DCF value: \$17B at 8% discount.

**Key technology:** Throttleable solid-fuel missile engine — patent-protected technology that allows solid-fuel engines to change thrust during flight (previously only possible with liquid-fuel engines). Enables precise maneuverability (+400%), extended range (+1,000%). US-based supply chain via acquisitions of ExoTerra Resources (propulsion) and Estes Energetics (solid fuel).

**Key contracts:**
- Lockheed Martin NGI program (roll-control and stage-separation propulsion for US homeland missile defense)
- NASA multi-million dollar contract (Mar 2026) via prime a.i. Solutions
- Icarus Robotics contract (Apr 2026)
- Palantir OS integration; MDA Space NGI guidance integration; RTX missile defense integration; Millennium Space (Boeing) small satellite constellation

## Investment Thesis

**Primary thesis (Apr 2026, Trade Alert #105):**

Two-pillar thesis: (1) throttleable solid-fuel missile engine, and (2) Starlab JV.

**Missile engine:** Engineers at Raytheon and Anduril both confirmed uniqueness. Raytheon engineer: demand for missiles has "exploded," no suppliers can keep up, supply chain constraints are major issue with legacy makers Aerojet and ATK. Voyager technology "a big differentiator." Kill vehicle can "turn off its thrust and coast, as well as perform maneuvers" — unique to solid-fuel systems. Potential application in NGI, THAAD extended-range, PAC-3, SM-3 block upgrades. Raytheon engineer believes TRL8-9 based on public documents and NGI contract win.

**Starlab:** If it reaches orbit, Voyager is a 10-bagger. StarLab and Axiom expected to win Phase 2 NASA funding of ~\$1.5B each.

**Combined valuation:** Defense thesis alone implies \$3.5B (44% CAGR 2026-2027, 10x revenue multiple). Defense + space = \$20.5B. Current market cap \$1.6B. SimplyWall DCF: \$546/share vs \$26 current.

**Risks:** Early-stage technology, significant debt, dilution likely, binary outcomes on Starlab/NGI contracts, two engineers gave conflicting TRL assessments (TRL4 vs TRL8-9).
