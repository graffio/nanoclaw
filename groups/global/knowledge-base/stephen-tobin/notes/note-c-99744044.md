# Note -- March 11, 2025

With Hesai jumping 50%, D-wave 16%, and Aris 9%, today has been a good day and has cut my losses this month in half! The market indices continue to drift lower; the S&P is down 0.75% today, but our portfolio is up significantly. The policies of diversification and moving to cash are paying off, but it is not complete. JOBY is starting to look expensive, and I am still concerned about ULBI; they have extended the deadline to report earnings, suggesting problems with a newly acquired subsidiary. I hope the news is not bad when we get it.

I have almost finished my review of Aris. I am increasing my price target and will look for a suitable entry point to increase the size of the position.

Hesai is now above my price target, and I will be reviewing it next week.

The four defense stocks I had on the prospect list—Babcock, Chemring, Qimtiq, Thyssenkrupp, and Hensoldt—have all failed my review, so we will not be buying soon. Babcock looks to be the best, but we would need a pullback to get the risk-reward equation right.

Avon Technologies and Origin Materials have also been rejected. That leaves 12 stocks still to be examined; hopefully, there is a good one among them. Being selective is the key to good returns. You cannot get them all right, but you don't need many Hesai's to make a decent amount of cash.

It is fun when things are going well. Since August 1st, 2024, my portfolio is up 109% whereas the S&P is up 0.9%. That means we are beating the S&P by 12,103% LOL.
