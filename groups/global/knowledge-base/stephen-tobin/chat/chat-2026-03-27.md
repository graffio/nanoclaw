ASPI being hit hard this morning following a raft of negative press releases in the last 24 hours. The stock is down 15% but the news appears very misleading. Vanguard reports a zero holding, but it is not a mass sell off. They announced that indiviso subsidiaries will now report holding separately following a re organisation in January, the news is being misinterpreted by AI scraping tools like Stock Titans who have put out multiple AI generate news items. Secondly several law firms issued pre releases yesterday reporting that there class action survived a motion to dismiss. The motion to dismiss was judged in December and was denied in part. The law firms are trying to get more people to join the lawsuit, in you held before Sept 2024 you can join. The lawsuit alleges that management said their technology could enrich uranium before they had proved it to raise money. These class action law suits are very common, so common that a couple of years ago I did a study for an article onSeekingAlpha about Bloom Energy. I found that 97% are settled out of court for a figure between $3 and $5 million all of which goes to cover the lawyers fees and the amount of the settlement is correlated with the number of claimants. In all cases the payment is covered by corporate insurance, which is why the companies are forced to settle 'without admitting guilt'.
We will have to wait and see how this all looks when the dust settles but it is not material news so under normal conditions would have no impact, these are not normal conditions.
