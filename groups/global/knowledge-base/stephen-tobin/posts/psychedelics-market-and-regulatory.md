## Key Takeaways

**The number of companies in the space has fallen dramatically** in the last year as many smaller operations have either run out of cash or been acquired. When I started following this sector, I had more than 60 companies on my spreadsheet.

Some have seen their drug candidates fail pivotal tests, and others have been unable to get approval to move from the lab to testing.

Having reviewed the industry, **I will continue to follow five companies** that have the technology and cash runway to be of interest.

**I have decided not to invest at this time**, as the companies remain too far away from commercialization, with the earliest realistic date of first volume sales being 2029. Risks are falling, but they are not yet low enough for me to commit money. I will continue to monitor this sector and review again next summer.

The psychedelic-drug market is **projected to experience substantial growth**, expanding from US $2.9 billion in 2023 to over US $8 billion by 2033. This growth is primarily fueled by the increasing demand for treatments for conditions such as treatment-resistant depression (TRD), PTSD, and alcohol-use disorder.

**Regulatory hurdles are progressively being cleared**, with significant developments like FDA Breakthrough Therapy designations, the establishment of Australia's prescriber scheme, and the implementation of adult-use regulations in Colorado and Oregon. These advancements suggest that the first New Drug Application (NDA) filings are anticipated in the second half of 2026, making 2027 the earliest realistic timeframe for product launches.

COMPASS Pathways' COMP360 has demonstrated a leading position with the first positive Phase 3 readout for TRD. However, other promising contenders are rapidly catching up. These include ATAI Life Sciences' BPL-003 (5-MeO-DMT), which offers shorter treatment sessions (≤2 hours), Cybin's CYB003 (deuterated psilocin), and MindMed's MM120 (LSD for generalized anxiety disorder, GAD). All of these have reported strongPhase 2 data and are expected to advance into pivotal trials by mid-2026.

The **economic viability of psychedelic clinics strongly favors molecules with ultra-short durations**. Key opinion leaders (KOLs) emphasize that a 2-hour discharge window for patients can be seamlessly integrated into the existing infrastructure of over 5,000 U.S. Spravato centers. This significantly reduces therapist time compared to classic psilocybin treatments and enhances the appeal to payers.

In terms of financial stability, **COMPASS Pathways (CMPS) and MindMed (MNMD) possess the most substantial cash reserves**, holding US $222 million and US $238 million, respectively. Even leaner operations like ATAI Life Sciences (ATAI) with US $108 million and Cybin (CYBN) with approximately US $99 million (plus a convertible facility) anticipate sufficient funding to last "into 2027." This covers the crucial Phase 3 readouts before any major commercial build-out.

The market outlook, as perceived by Wall Street, is generally optimistic but is tempered by the **inherent risk of dilution**. Sell-side financial models predict continued losses through 2028 and assume that companies will need to raise additional capital through fresh equity or convertibles to finance launch spending. This makes share-count expansion a central variable for investors.

**Despite positive developments, several execution bottlenecks persist**. These include mandatory REMS-style monitoring requirements, limitations in therapist-training capacity, and inconsistent state-level acceptance. These factors could potentially cap early adoption rates, even after federal rescheduling, thereby underscoring the critical need for scalable, short-duration dosing paradigms.

## **Psychedelic Therapeutics Market Potential Update**

Momentum behind psychedelic medicine is moving from niche clinics to the mainstream as late-stage trials progress and payers look for cost-effective alternatives to chronic pharmacotherapy.

**Market in 2025: still small, but scaling fast**

-   The **global psychedelic-drug market** generated about **US $2.9 billion in 2023** and is projected to reach **US $8.7 billion by 2033** , a CAGR of 11.6 %
    
-   Within that, the **psilocybin-assisted therapy segment** is estimated at **US $1.5 billion (2024)** and on track for **US $3.3 billion by 2031**
    
-   Commercial **ketamine (IV/IM plus J&J’s Spravato)** accounts for roughly **US $0.75 billion today** with broker estimates of **US $3-5 billion longer term**

### **Care-delivery economics versus incumbents**

-   **Spravato (esketamine nasal spray):** drug spend **US $25-60 k per patient-year** ; clinics earn ~US $150 per monitored hour, and >5,000 REMS-certified U.S. sites already exist, forming a ready channel for future psychedelics
    
-   **Parenteral ketamine:** a six-infusion course costs Québec’s health system ~ **US $2 k**, underscoring its affordability relative to electroconvulsive therapy
    
-   **Psychedelic macro-dosing (psilocybin or MDMA):** protocols require 6-8 hours of therapist time per administration but just one to three sessions annually, suggesting lower lifetime drug cost yet higher up-front staffing needs. While precise pricing is undecided, clinics can leverage the Spravato infrastructure, moderating capex requirements.

### **Commercialization Hurdles Across Markets**

-   **REMS & clinic infrastructure** – Experts anticipate mandatory two-hour post-dose observation for most psychedelics, echoing Spravato’s program and limiting early throughput
    
-   **Payer coverage uncertainty** – KOLs believe insurers will reimburse once an FDA-approved label exists, but billing codes for therapist time and facility fees must be established.
    
-   **Therapist capacity & training** – FDA guidance favours active-comparator designs and caps prior psychedelic exposure, implying ongoing demand for certified, experienced staff
    
-   **Cultural stigma** – Political polarity (e.g., faster adoption in “blue” states vs. resistance in Florida) may influence state uptake even after federal approva.l
    

Overall, regulatory momentum is positive—particularly with BTDs and early access schemes—but commercialization will hinge on demonstrating scalability within stringent safety frameworks and convincing payers of durable cost offsets.

### **Upcoming Regulatory Catalysts (capital-intensive milestones)**

-   **ATAI – BPL-003 (5-MeO-DMT) for TRD:** FDA Type-C meeting 3Q-25; Phase 3 initiation 1H-26
    
-   **CMPS – COMP360 (psilocybin) for TRD:** Six-month data from both pivotal studies expected 2H-26; company exploring accelerated filing options
    
-   **CYBN – CYB004 (deuterated DMT) for GAD:** Phase 2 completion mid-25; first Phase 3 read-out guided for 2H-26
    
-   **MNMD – MM120 (LSD) ODT for GAD:** Voyage and Panorama Phase 3 trials powered for 5-point HAM-A delta; top-line data 1H-26 and 2H-26, respectively
    
-   **GHRS – Lead candidate GH001 currently on hold, FDA has requested additional info relating to histology findings in animals. IND applied for GH003.**
    

### **Financing Outlook & Dilution Risk**

-   **ATAI:** Broker models imply cumulative >US $600 m equity issuance through 2029, a potentially material dilution over today’s ~194 m outstanding shares
    
-   **CMPS:** expect a US $100 m raise before 2027
    
-   **CYBN:** The High Trail structure provides draw-down flexibility yet embeds up to US $500 m of convertible overhang at a 30 % premium to current levels
    
-   **MNMD:** With ~US $240 m cash and debt capacity, no immediate need for new capital prior to Phase 3 data, though commercial build-out post-2026 may require additional funds
    
-   **GHRS: Completed a $150m raise in Q1, inevitably more will be needed as their main candidate is on hold.**
    

In aggregate, cash on hand should bridge all four developers to their next major data events. Post-read-out, reliance on external capital—especially for launch preparations—remains high, keeping dilution a central element of the investment case across the psychedelic landscape.
