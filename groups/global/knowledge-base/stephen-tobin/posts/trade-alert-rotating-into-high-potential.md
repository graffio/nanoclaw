# Trade Alert: Rotating into High Potential Stocks

*Putting money raised last week back to work*

My rules forced the exit from four trades last week, with three of them closing at a loss. Today, I will put some of the money raised from those three trades into one of our stocks that appears to have explosive near-term potential.

It will take the investment in this stock to the maximum allowed.

I have repeatedly targeted the Nuclear industry over the last couple of years. It is a sector primed for growth, and much of that growth will come from new emerging technologies.

The demand for enriched materials spans multiple sectors, from nuclear energy to medical drug development. Companies involved in enrichment are often overlooked as people focus on the companies building nuclear reactors.

Much of the technology being used for enrichment is prohibitively expensive, with long lead times and uncertain outcomes.

The established centrifuge technology is more than 40 years old and a facility can cost up to $1 billion to build; the industry is primed for a new emerging technology to enter.

Today, I will increase my investment in a company with a proven ability to build enrichment facilities at one-tenth the cost of established technology. It has operational sites and has started commercial shipments.

The price is low and the company remains under the radar; it should be a great investment, but as always, it is a small-cap pre-profit operation, so care is needed and risk management is crucial.

This may be the first in a slew of investments as I take advantage of today’s low prices.

### Product Pipeline & Commercial Status

-   **Carbon-14** – first ASP plant in Pretoria commissioned; full-capacity tolling deal signed with Canadian pharma CRO; first shipments targeted mid-2025.
    
-   **Silicon-28** – multi-isotope ASP facility to deliver kg-scale volumes in 2Q25; purchase orders in place with a U.S. semiconductor OEM and a global industrial-gas major.
    
-   **Ytterbium-176** – QE pilot producing gram-level samples; continuous reactor slated for 24/7 operation by Oct-25, scaling to ~2 kg/yr in 2026.
    
-   **Next-wave isotopes under ASP R&D** – Mo-100, Zn-68, Xe-129/136 (medical imaging), Ge-70/72/74 (semiconductors), Cl-37 (nuclear).
    
-   **QE roadmap** – Nickel-64, Gadolinium-160, Lithium-6/7; long-term goal is HALEU uranium-235 enrichment for advanced reactors, leveraging Yb-176 proof-point.
    

### Competitive Differentiation

Management highlights:

-   **Cost & energy advantage** – no moving parts plus laser selectivity drive sub-$50 /SWU economics vs. ~$140 /SWU centrifuge benchmark.
    
-   **Speed-to-market** – modular ASP units fabricated locally; initial plants completed within 18 months vs. multi-year greenfield centrifuge projects.
    
-   **IP depth** – two decades of proprietary fluid-dynamic modelling and undisclosed nozzle designs; competitors “lack equivalent know-how” per company filings.
    
-   **Multi-industry optionality** – ability to switch feedstock enables monetisation across pharma, quantum computing, and nuclear fuel cycles—diversifying revenue risk.
    

Collectively, these elements position ASPI as a potential structural disruptor in high-purity isotope supply, with a technology stack that could compress industry cost curves while expanding addressable applications.

## Market / Competitive

A structurally tight isotope market—exacerbated by geopolitical risks surrounding Russian supply—creates an unusual opportunity for ASPI. The company’s proprietary Aerodynamic Separation Process (ASP) and laser-based Quantum Enrichment (QE) technologies aim to address simultaneous shortages in **medical radio-isotopes, semiconductor/quantum-computing materials, and advanced nuclear fuel**, positioning ASPI as a potential “one-stop” Western supplier across three high-growth verticals.

### End-Market Demand Snapshot

-   Medical: Ytterbium-176 (feedstock for Lu-177 radiotherapeutics) and Gadolinium-160 (precursor to Tb-161) face persistent scarcity; ASPI began shipping Yb-176 samples in Aug-25 and has a supply deal for Gd-160 with Isotopia, targeting oncology indications experiencing double-digit volume growth.
    
-   Semiconductor / Quantum: Enriched Silicon-28 under long-term offtake with a U.S. chipmaker (deliveries from Q1-26) and a global gas major; Barium-137 purchase order received Sept-25(probably IONQ). Industry capex >US$300 bn in AI infrastructure is driving demand for isotopically pure gases and substrates that enhance qubit coherence and thermal performance.
    
-   Nuclear & Energy Transition: Western governments seek High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium (HALEU) for small modular reactors; ASPI’s QE spin-out (Quantum Leap Energy) targets first HALEU output in 2027, offering a domestic-allied alternative to Russia’s ~50 % global enrichment share.
