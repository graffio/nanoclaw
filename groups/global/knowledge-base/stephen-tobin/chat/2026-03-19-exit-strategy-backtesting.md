## Stephen Tobin (reply, 2026-03-19T17:40)

> I often back test the portfolio performance with different strategies. Stop loss and take profit are two I regularly play around with. Sometime last year I shared some backtesting findings that showed taking profit at 200% was the most successful strategy, however to allow trades to get to the 200% I had to remove what had previously been the stop loss figures, I also had to let trades run far longer and not close at what was my standard 100% figure. Of course all of this back testing fails when the conditions in the market change dramatically as they have done recently. All subscribers should choose when they want to exit but all the testing I have done suggests a failure of the thesis is the correct time to book a loss and 200% is the best time to book a profit. It does mean I suffer more losses and many of them are larger than they were previously however reviewing past performance shows I have missed out on more dollars by closing early than I have gained by limiting losses or closing early. It is interesting how peoples minds change depending on the situation, in mid 2025 subscribers were most upset that I had exited trades too early missing out on big secondary moves from ONDS, ELVA and the like; at the moment people worry I am exiting too late and missing out on the first move higher that eventually failed. There is no perfect answer to these questions, if I had known in advance about the Iran war I would have closed positions in profit and closed many with a small loss but obviously I didn't. I will re run models again when we get deeper into the current situation but at the moment I will continue to follow the rule of exit if the thesis fails, take profit is less clear — back testing suggests 200% but that may be more of a reflection of the final move stocks get to. I only have three years of data and suspect as the dataset grows the 200% will rise and it may be better to hold until target.
