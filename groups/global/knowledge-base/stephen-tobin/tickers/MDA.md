## Company Overview

MDA Space is a \$3 billion Canadian satellite manufacturing company and vertically integrated leader in the high-growth space, defence, and communications markets. Stephen had the company on his watchlist for over a year before buying, waiting for a pullback to create a reasonable entry point.

**Products / Segments:**

- Satellite Systems — manufacturing advanced, software-defined satellites for LEO, MEO, and GEO orbits. Pioneering high-volume contract manufacturing with new facilities targeting capacity of 2 satellites per day, unmatched in the industry.

- Space Robotics (Canadarm) — built the robotic arms for the Space Shuttle and ISS (Canadarm1, Canadarm2, Dextre). Next-generation Canadarm3 is an autonomous AI-driven robot funded by NASA for the Gateway program (\$1.8 billion contract).

- Geointelligence — operates RADARSAT-2 and launching CHORUS system in 2026. Provides high-precision radar imagery for defence and intelligence customers. Expanding into satellite tracking and counter-space defence systems. Highest margin segment.

**Revenue model:** Satellite system manufacturing + government contracts + geointelligence services.

- FY2025: CAD \$1.6B revenue (+50% YoY), adjusted EBITDA CAD \$324M (+50%), 20% EBITDA margin
- FY2025 FCF: CAD \$165M, net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio of 0.4x
- FY2026 guidance: revenue CAD \$1.7-1.9B, EBITDA CAD \$320-370M (~10% and ~7% growth at midpoints)
- Backlog: CAD \$4.0B (approximately three years of utilization)
- Total opportunity pipeline: CAD \$40B
- CapEx 2026: CAD \$225-275M (neutral to negative FCF expected as they invest in growth)

**Key contracts/deals:**

- Telesat Lightspeed program — detailed engineering and manufacturing preparation
- Globalstar constellation — engineering complete, 9 spacecraft on shop floor approaching launch. Financially backed by Apple (\$2B invested, 20% equity)
- 126-satellite LEO constellation antennas for US military
- RADARSAT+ first contract — could be worth \$500M long-term, first phase \$50M
- 49North subsidiary — launched Feb 2026 for Canadian defence missions (C4ISR capabilities)

## Competitive Position

MDA occupies a unique position as a vertically integrated space company with proven technology and a legacy stretching back 40+ years. The company's ability to manufacture satellites at scale (targeting 2 per day) is a key differentiator that competitors cannot match.

**Contract risks:** The share price suffered two notable pullbacks driven by SpaceX-related concerns:

- EchoStar contract cancellation — \$1.3B LEO constellation contract cancelled after EchoStar sold spectrum to SpaceX. Stephen views the early cancellation as arguably a "blessing" given EchoStar's going-concern doubts. MDA will be compensated per contract terms.

- Globalstar acquisition rumours — fears that SpaceX could acquire Globalstar and cancel MDA's contract. Stephen considers cancellation unlikely given Apple's \$2B investment and 20% equity stake, plus the advanced stage of the project (engineering complete, 9 spacecraft on shop floor).

**Space sector view:** Stephen considers space "a key sector" with a multi-year growth stage ahead, driven by new investment cycles from NATO countries viewing space as a critical defence domain.
