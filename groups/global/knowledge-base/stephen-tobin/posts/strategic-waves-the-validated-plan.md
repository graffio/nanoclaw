## Validated does not mean perfect

With 30 months of data, that includes 97 trades I have been looking at ways to improve the returns and reduce the one issue that forward testing has exposed.

Volatility, the results are profitable over the longer term, but volatile. This issue did not show up in the strategy's backtesting or in the trading I did before I began reporting all my results.

The trading plan has two distinct parts.

1.  **Strategic:** This involves identifying the right company by conducting a deep dive into all companies in a specific sector, and choosing the one I believe has a competitive advantage and is most likely to succeed with its corporate strategy.
    
2.  **Waves:-** This is the plan for taking positions. When to buy, when to sell, how much to buy, and how long to hold.
    

I am very happy with part 1. I consistently identify companies with solid share price gains and regularly select the best performer in each sector.

A thorough review has identified a weakness in part 2, it has been clear for some time that I have not managed to optimize the return from some investments but with a large body of data now gathered I have been able to identify a more profitable path forward. I am introducing a new term, “thesis validated”. I speak a lot about commercial validation, a prerequisite for an investment, but I had not applied the same thought process to my own trading.

Total return over the last 30 months is 553%; over the last 6 months, it has been 31%. It is hard to imagine a more profitable path forward, but there is one, and for the second half of the project I expect increased profits and reduced volatility.

## Thesis Validated

I have looked through all 97 trades in detail, and a clear pattern has emerged. Those companies that delivered profit continue to deliver profitable opportunities after I exit.

In fact, they deliver (as a whole) greater profit after I exit than they did before. It is really a proof of the strategic part 1 of the plan that the thesis was correct. It shows that, on the whole, the thesis remains correct longer than I held the position.

Looking at companies rather than trades, I have completely closed investments in 18 for a profit and 9 for a loss. Of those 18 showing profits, only 2 have not presented a profitable trading opportunity after I exited.

In some cases, large profits have been missed. LEU is up 239% since exit ONDS, ELVA and LUNR are all up more than 100%.

Looking in greater detail shows even bigger missed opportunities.

One example is Bitfarms (BITF). It was trade number 3 taken in October 2023, delivering a 200% return in 242 days. You can see the trade on the chart, green for entry, red for exit.

## Trading Tactics

What is the best way to trade these old names with a validated thesis?

It is a constant source of debate among subscribers about which trading strategy is best: should we buy and hold for the long term, or move in and out of stocks as the market moves?

It has been my main policy to move “in and out,” taking profits near a high and buying back near a low. It was the best strategy during backtesting and in my trading before this project began. Forward testing a strategy is the only real way.

I have a second strategy, “hold and add”. In the past, I have used this strategy less often.

This current plan has been very profitable, and we have achieved some excellent results. As I discussed last week, the portfolio cash flow is a key element of the plan. I have to make sure I have the cash needed to buy stocks when I want to, and I can’t do that if money is tied up.

## The New Strategy

It would not be sensible to use the hold-and-add strategy for all stocks and on all occasions.

Pullbacks can be caused by a failure in the thesis, I am just wrong, and I don’t want to keep adding in that situation.

Garners’s Hype Cycle-

This remains a key part of the plan and has helped me make some big profits. Market hype often drives these stocks and not the business fundamentals. ABAT is a good example. The big rise was just hype, and we booked a 300% return in a month, then bought again when the stock returned to a more acceptable level.

## Conclusion

The Strategic Waves trading plan is now validated, halfway through the project, and above target. 30 months and 97 trades are enough data for me to feel confident that the strategy works.

The data show that I can increase my returns and reduce some of the volatility by increasing the number of trades I take in companies with a validated thesis. Companies where the idea has been proven effective by the profits it delivers. These companies also deliver higher returns if I use the hold-and-add trading strategy.

Companies with a validated thesis will become a priority during the second half of the project. I will review them every week before looking for new names, and I will try to move more of these validated thesis stocks to a hold and add a trading strategy.
