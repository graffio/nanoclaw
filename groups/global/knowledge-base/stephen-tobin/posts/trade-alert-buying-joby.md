# Trade Alert: Buying JOBY

*100% return possible in short order.*

# Intro

I have written about Joby twice on Seeking Alpha and maintained a buy rating since the first article in April 2022. I am long but will increase the size of the position when the market opens next week.

The next earnings call is scheduled for February 21st when we will hopefully get a comprehensive update on the progress toward certification. The recent news suggests that this update might be very positive.

# News

We have seen many positive news announcements in recent weeks that show how Joby is gearing up for commercial operations. The most recent press release is important, showing they intend to start operations next year and are beginning to sign long-term exclusive deals that will guarantee revenue.

-   Feb 11th: signs exclusive 6-year contract to provide air taxi service in Dubai. Full operation due 2026, initial commercial service 2025. The deal includes an agreement with Skyports to build and operate the vertiports.
    
-   Feb 9th: FAA approval for the propulsion certification plan; this is the final plan requiring approval. 30 FAA tests were completed and passed in 2023
    
-   Feb 8th: FAA grants part 145 repair station certificate authorizing Joby to repair, maintain, and overhaul its aircraft.
    
-   Jan 23, 17, 8: Joby charging stations to be installed in NYC and South Carolina.
    

I have often said that JOBY is leading this particular race; the gap between them and the rest is growing. Archer is still building their first planes, EVEX is in the design stage, Lilium is building its first full-scale machine, Vertical seems to have lost its way, and although EHang has some Chinese approval, it is not in the same market.

Joby is clearly ahead and is now preparing for operations post-approval.

## Financials

JOBY is in excellent financial health with over a billion dollars of shareholder equity and no debt. It has over $1 billion in short-term assets that give it at least three years of cash on hand. Shareholders were diluted by 12% in the last year, and there has been some insider selling of late; we should monitor both of these metrics going forward. JOBY is 24% owned by the general public, 23% belongs to insiders, and the rest is with institutions and private companies.
