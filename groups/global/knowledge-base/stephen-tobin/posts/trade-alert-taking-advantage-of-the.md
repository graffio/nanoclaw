# Trade Alert #: Taking advantage of the sell off

*I suspect Fridays drop will be short lived*

The sell-off on Friday will likely be short-lived, as the US administration once again seems to be backing away from its threats while China stands firm.

I will take the opportunity to increase position size in some of my holdings.

When I increase position size, the reason is two-fold. Firstly, a technical position must have arisen that suggests a short-term low may have arrived. I never want to buy near a top, and just because the markets pulled back, it does not mean that all stocks are close to a short-term low.

PONY is a good example of this. It is a Chinese stock, so it pulled back sharply on Friday, but even after the pullback, stochastics remain relatively high, and it could easily drop to $16 without breaking the pattern I am following

The second reason is the most important, the technicals give me a reason to look at a stock, but ONLY its fundamentals give me a reason to buy it. Before buying any stock, I want to make sure there is a solid reason for believing it is likely to appreciate. I want to see recent news indicating that things are going well, that my original thesis still stands or has improved, or that new opportunities have arisen.

Any negative news would not necessarily cause me to exit the existing position, but it would pause a new entry, just as the absence of anything material would.

## Hesai Fundamentals

Hesai, a dominant LiDAR manufacturer, primarily serves the Chinese market. The investment thesis for Hesai is based on its ability to rapidly scale production. This scaling leads to significant economies of scale, allowing Hesai to offer lower prices, which in turn creates a barrier to entry for smaller manufacturers and could force them out of the market.

In October, we had the latest sales figures from the NE Times, the accepted industry monitor of LiDAR.

According to the NE Times, the LiDAR autonomous driving market in China grew 104% in the year to August (report published Oct 8th), reaching 1.61 million units YTD in 2025.

The Chinese market is highly concentrated, with the top three players accounting for 94% of total sales. This concentration and the scale they have achieved will make it very difficult for Western LiDAR producers to sell their products outside of the US.

The collapse in Seyond sales shows how the economies of scale are taking effect. I would expect to see Seyond withdraw from the market soon.

Huawei benefits from supplying large numbers of very low-cost blind spot LiDARs. A car will have one front-facing LiDAR and multiple corner LiDARs. If we take out sales of these low-cost, low-margin products (they are low margin because they compete with much lower value Radar). Then Hesai became the largest player by unit sales, Huawei supplied 58,622 blind spot radars, leaving 61,233 forward-facing LiDARs.

The LiDAR penetration rate of new automobiles in China increased 4% points to 10.9% in August, making it 8.9% YTD. This is likely due to the falling price of these units.

Hesai introduced its $200 ATX LiDAR, replacing its $2,500 AT128. Robosense has released its lower-cost MX unit, but I do not have prices. The lower prices are leading to the adoption by lower-priced vehicles, with Leap Motors B01 and B10, Toyota bz3X, and GAC AION RT all adopting LiDAR this year. These vehicles are all priced below US$20,000.

There has been a shift amongst the leading players. Huawei had a total share of almost 44% I think this is due to the volume ramp of blind spot radar and the ramp up in sales of the new AITO M8 SUV (manufactured by Huawei).

Hesai ranked second in total sales with 32% of the market, but if blind spot radar is excluded, it would rank first with 41% of the total. The other players are being squeezed out.
