## Company Overview

Compass Pathways is a biotech company developing COMP360, a synthetic psilocybin therapy for treatment-resistant depression (TRD). Stephen tracked the psychedelic therapeutics sector since 2021, writing about CMPS multiple times on both Seeking Alpha and Substack over four years before finally buying in February 2026. He describes it as a trade that "took a while" — four years of coverage before committing capital.

**Products:**

- COMP360 — synthetic psilocybin administered in a single oral dose under clinical supervision (8-hour clinic visit)
- Targeting TRD initially, with a Phase 2b/3 trial for PTSD also underway
- Testing across multiple other conditions at smaller scale (addictions, autism spectrum disorders, anorexia)

**Clinical program:**

- COMP005 (Phase III): COMP360 25mg vs placebo. Met 6-week primary endpoint. 26-week data shows sustained improvement — single dose effective for nearly 6 months. At week 10, patients offered second dose; 40% of partial responders went into remission after second dose (though sample is small — 31 partial responders, 12 remitters)
- COMP006 (Phase III): COMP360 25mg vs 10mg vs 1mg (low-dose placebo design to address the unblinding problem). Confirmed statistically significant improvement at 6 weeks. Clear dose-response — 25mg consistently better than 10mg, both better than 1mg. This dose-response is "something Spravato was never able to do"
- MADRS improvement vs placebo: 3.6 points (comparable to Spravato's 3.4 in its Phase III Transform 2)
- Total improvement: MADRS from 37 (severe depression) to 17 (mild depression) on active drug vs 21 (moderate depression) on placebo
- Trial design carefully excluded psychiatric care — effects isolated to drug alone, meaning real-world results packaged with therapy could be considerably better
- FDA approved rolling submission; commercial launch timeline brought forward to Q4 2026 (originally Q4 2027)
- Phase 2b showed median time to depressive event of 92 days on single-dose 25mg cohort

**Revenue model:** Pre-revenue. Stephen's forecast (in image form in Trade Alert #101) assumes FDA approval in 2027 with commercial ramp-up thereafter. Revenue forecasts do not include any conditions beyond TRD. Jefferies estimates \$17,000 per treatment, the same as Spravato.

**Financial position:**

- Aug 2025: Cash \$222M — among the largest in the psychedelic space alongside MindMed (\$238M)
- Feb 2026: Completed \$150M public offering at \$8.00/ADS (17.5M ADSs plus pre-funded warrants)
- Feb 2026: Exercised over 35M outstanding warrants (originally issued Jan 2025) yielding ~\$200M gross proceeds
- Expected to need ~\$100M additional raise before 2027 (per Aug 2025 analysis)

## Competitive Position

Stephen views the psychedelic therapeutics market as an emerging technology with "huge disruptive potential." Spravato (J&J's S-ketamine nasal spray) has proven the commercial case — revenue grew from \$689M in 2023 to \$1.7B in 2025, reportedly generating over \$1.5B per quarter by late 2025, with J&J targeting \$5B in the US alone by 2028. AbbVie paid \$1.2B for Gilgamesh Pharmaceuticals' psychedelic asset, setting a valuation benchmark.

**vs Spravato (J&J):** CMPS's COMP360 shows MADRS improvement at least as good as Spravato, requiring dramatically fewer doses. Spravato requires 2 doses/week for 8 weeks then ongoing weekly/biweekly maintenance; COMP360 guides to 1-4 doses per year. First-year cost: Spravato at \$595,000 vs COMP360 at \$68,000 (at 4 treatments/year at \$17,000 each). Even monthly treatments would only be \$170,000 — "less than half the cost of Spravato." Spravato itself only succeeded in 2 of 5 Phase III trials (Transform 1 failed, Transform 3 failed in 65+ patients, Aspire 1 and 2 failed vs placebo at 4 weeks). COMP360 data is "the best we have seen."

**vs Cybin (CYBN):** Strong competitor with CYB003 (deuterated psilocin). Phase 2 showed 71% remission at 12 months and extraordinary MADRS improvement. Phase 3 data due 2026. Has \$500M convertible facility. Both use psilocybin-based compounds requiring 8-hour clinic visits. Stephen's Nov 2025 base case: CYBN "should confirm its durability in 2026, and with its \$500m debenture runway, could move quickly to scale."

**vs ATAI Life Sciences:** BPL-003 (intranasal 5-MeO-DMT) earned Breakthrough Therapy Designation after strong Phase 2b. Competitive advantage: ~2-hour clinic visits vs 8 hours for psilocybin, fitting existing Spravato infrastructure. But further behind — Phase 3 initiation 1H-26. Broker models imply cumulative over \$600M equity issuance through 2029. "Has the issues of sickness and vomiting to deal with."

**vs MindMed (MNMD):** MM120 (LSD) targeting GAD, not TRD — different market. Three Phase 3 trials launching through 2026. \$238M cash. "78% GAD response as fantastic."

**vs NRX Pharmaceuticals (NRXP):** Nano-cap ketamine play Stephen bought alongside CMPS (Trade Alert #102). Has accepted NDA — "the only company with an accepted NDA in the psychedelic space" — putting it technically ahead of CMPS in FDA process. However, radically higher risk profile: negative equity, going-concern notice, \$7M cash.

**vs GH Research (GHRS):** Inhaled 5-MeO-DMT showing 73% remission at 6 months with 2-hour visits. Excellent early results but under US IND clinical hold due to rat respiratory histology safety concerns.

**Key competitive advantages for CMPS:** First-mover with the first positive Phase 3 readout for TRD. Only company with statistically significant Phase 3 data. Rolling FDA submission accelerates timeline. Over 3,000 existing Spravato clinics provide ready infrastructure. Insurance coverage proven via Spravato. "Compass Pathways will have a significant first-mover advantage and is the only company with statistically significant data to support its claims."

**Key competitive weakness:** 8-hour clinic visit — "may not be feasible given current infrastructure" vs 2-hour visits for BPL-003 and GH001. "Session duration creates competitive differentiation" and KOLs rate shorter sessions as "superior."

## Investment Thesis

**Pre-investment tracking (2021–2025):** Stephen tracked the psychedelic sector since 2021, writing at least five articles across Seeking Alpha and Substack covering ATAI, CMPS, MNMD, and CYBN. Repeatedly concluded it was too early. In Aug 2025: "I have decided not to invest at this time, as the companies remain too far away from commercialization, with the earliest realistic date of first volume sales being 2029. Risks are falling, but they are not yet low enough for me to commit money." In Nov 2025: "I am not investing yet, but have identified the companies that warrant a deep dive." CMPS consistently identified as the sector leader. In Jan 2026 chat: "I think CMPS is probably still the best bet."

**Investment thesis (Feb 2026, Trade Alert #101):** Finally bought after four years of tracking. Triggered by COMP005 26-week data release and COMP006 topline results. Key arguments: (1) COMP360 data shows efficacy at least as good as Spravato with a fraction of the doses required; (2) both Phase III trials met primary endpoints — "the Compass drug looks likely to pass both Phase III trials, meeting its primary endpoints and showing a clear improvement over both a placebo and a low dose of the drug"; (3) trial design carefully excluded psychiatric care, meaning real-world results could be much better; (4) Spravato has proven the commercial model — insurance, 3,000+ clinics, blockbuster revenue; (5) massive cost advantage at \$68,000/year vs \$595,000/year; (6) rolling FDA submission accelerates timeline. "By training, I am a mathematician, and as such, my conclusion is based on the statistics presented by Compass and nothing else." Target \$40, implying ~365% upside from \$8.60 entry.

**Concerns:** (1) 87% white patient cohort in trials — "may have to be addressed in future studies"; (2) small sample sizes in second-dose data (31 partial responders, 12 remitters — "these numbers are very small to take too much from"); (3) 8-hour clinic visit requirement vs competitors' 2-hour sessions; (4) \$150M capital raise day after data release — "likely going to keep a lid on the share price for a little while"; (5) dilution risk — expected \$100M+ additional raise; (6) still a long way to profitability; (7) FDA currently "seems chaotic" following political turmoil — Moderna vaccine rejection/reversal cited as symptom; (8) unblinding problem inherent to all psychedelic trials (patients know if they had the drug).
