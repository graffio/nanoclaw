## Company Overview

Hesai is the world's number one LiDAR manufacturer by unit volume. A Chinese company headquartered in Shanghai, it designs and manufactures laser-based sensors for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS), autonomous vehicles, and robotics. Stephen views Hesai as a "second dimension" play on Chinese EV adoption: exposure to Chinese electric vehicle sales without buying an overpriced Chinese EV maker.

**Products:**

- ATX LiDAR — low-cost ADAS sensor replacing the \$2,500 AT128 at approximately \$200 per unit; over 1 million units shipped since Q1 2025 launch with a 4 million unit backlog
- JT64P — 360-degree x 189-degree FoV, 1.15M points/sec, minimum detection range 0m, 30% precision improvement (launched Mar 2026)
- Infinity Eye — complete LiDAR solutions for L2-L4 autonomous driving (launched Apr 2025)
- System-on-chip LiDAR with proprietary "Photon Isolation" technology (unveiled Nov 2025)

**Revenue model:** Hardware sensor sales to automotive OEMs and robotaxi operators. Revenue growing rapidly as LiDAR penetration rises in Chinese new vehicles (8.9% YTD as of Aug 2025, up 4 points).

- Q2 2025: Revenue +54% YoY to RMB 706M (approximately \$98.6M); first quarterly net profit of RMB 44M
- Q3 2025: Non-GAAP EPS \$0.28 (beat by \$0.17); ADAS shipments +193% YoY; total shipments +229% YoY to 441,398 units
- Cumulative deliveries passed 185 million units by Nov 2025
- First LiDAR manufacturer to produce over 1 million units in a single calendar year (2025)
- Cash: RMB 7.37B (approximately \$1.04B) as of Sep 2025, boosted by HK listing proceeds

**Key customers/deals:**

- Exclusive supplier to a major US robotaxi firm (deal value over \$40M, announced Sep 2025)
- Toyota ADAS design win and C-sample milestone with a top European OEM (Aug 2025)
- Volume production awards from Cadillac, Pony.AI, Zeekr, DiDi
- Grab strategic partnership for LiDAR deployment across Southeast Asia (Feb 2026)
- 61% of the global robotaxi LiDAR market (unchanged from 2023)

**Corporate developments:**

- Hong Kong dual listing completed Sep 2025, raising approximately \$475M via 20M Class B shares at \$27.11
- Class B share redesignation approved (Mar 2026)
- No. 1 globally for LiDAR patents per KnowMade data (Jan 2026)

## Competitive Position

Stephen's view: "Hesai appears to be maintaining its dominant position; the other Chinese LiDAR manufacturers seem to be focused on non-automotive verticals" (Aug 2025 LiDAR review). "The growth rate is staggering, the economies of scale have become a significant barrier to entry which should protect this investment" (Nov 2025 chat).

**vs RoboSense:** Second-largest Chinese LiDAR player. Together with Hesai, captured approximately 80% of Velodyne's former mechanical scanning LiDAR market share. Stephen expects RoboSense to "continue losing market share" to Hesai.

**vs Huawei:** Third player in Chinese market. Benefited from volume ramp of low-cost blind spot LiDAR. Reached 44% total market share including blind spot units, but excluding those, Hesai leads with 41%. Stephen expects Hesai to "announce a blind spot LiDAR at a price below that of Huawei."

**vs Seyond:** Third Chinese supplier collapsing. "The collapse in Seyond sales shows how the economies of scale are taking effect. I would expect to see Seyond withdraw from the market soon" (Oct 2025).

**vs Western players (Luminar, Innoviz, Ouster):** Chinese companies achieved earlier economies of scale through domestic adoption. Top three Chinese players account for 94% of Chinese market. Western companies face "slower adoption rates." Proposed US regulations could ban Chinese LiDAR hardware, but Stephen notes Hesai's US robotaxi deal shows Western demand persists. "It is particularly interesting that the deal is with a US firm, I think the market generally assumed that the western LiDAR makers would get all of this business" (Sep 2025 chat).

**Market data (Yole Group 2024):** Hesai held 33% of worldwide LiDAR market (down from 38% in 2023). Total market grew 60% YoY to \$859M. Hesai held 61% of robotaxi LiDAR market.

**Industry shift:** Stephen notes LiDAR "is no longer an emerging technology" (Nov 2025). It has become "as crucial to safety as an airbag." This makes Hesai "a value play rather than an emerging play" dependent on the broader economy and car/truck sales.

**Original thesis (Oct 2024):** "Second dimension" play on Chinese EV adoption. Hesai has successfully transitioned to large-scale production, achieving economies of scale. Partnerships with high-profile OEMs in China and Europe. Low-cost devices and strategic alliances position it as the dominant supplier.

**Revised thesis (Apr 2025):** Bought again after 278% return on first trade. New Infinity Eye product line extends dominance and opens new markets. Original target of \$28 remains valid. Short seller report dismissed as "a complete work of fiction."

**LiDAR deep dive thesis (Aug 2025):** Full sector review completed. Hesai maintaining dominant position. Chinese LiDAR market grew 104% YTD. Top three Chinese players control 94% of sales. Buying with technical target of \$50 and fair value model target of \$30.

**Current thesis (Nov 2025, Trade Alert #86):** Technology open day prompted multiple analyst upgrades. System-on-chip LiDAR and Photon Isolation technology unveiled. ATX backlog of 4 million units. Cumulative deliveries past 185 million units. "Probably my final LiDAR trade" — expects stock to reach all-time high early 2026, then target of \$50.

**Concerns:** Losing overall market share to Huawei (though excluding blind spot radar, Hesai leads). HK dual listing caused dilution and short-term selling pressure. Dependent on Chinese auto sales and broader economy. Oil crisis assessment (Mar 2026): Short-term risk Medium, Medium-term risk Medium — "Dependent on EV sales in China." Guggenheim downgraded to neutral (Jun 2025) citing market share loss. Q3 2025 revenue missed consensus estimates despite strong unit growth.
