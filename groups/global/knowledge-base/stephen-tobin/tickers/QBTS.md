## Company Overview

D-Wave is the only commercially operating quantum computer company. Their annealing technology is working today with
paying customers, unlike gate-based competitors (IBM, IonQ) which Stephen considers a decade or more away from
commercial viability.

**Products:**

- Advantage 2 quantum annealer — commercially operational
- Quantum Computing as a Service (QCaaS) — cloud access to the annealer
- Developing dual-rail qubit gate-model system (via acquisition of Quantum Circuits Inc.)

**Technology position:** First-mover advantage in commercial quantum. Annealing approach solves optimization problems (
defence, logistics, pharma). Gate-based quantum (IBM, IonQ, Rigetti) requires ~1M qubits for useful computation — only
Microsoft has a clear path there. Stephen believes D-Wave is "wasting money" on gate research, calling it a "dead end,"
but later revised this view when they acquired Quantum Circuits and pivoted to dual-rail qubits.

**Revenue model:** System sales + QCaaS subscriptions. Revenue still minimal but growing rapidly:

- FY2024: ~\$8.8M total revenue
- FY2025: \$24.6M (+179% YoY), gross profit +265%
- Jan 2026 alone: over \$30M bookings (FAU \$20M, Fortune 100 \$10M QCaaS)
- Cash: \$884.5M (strongest balance sheet in history)

**Key customers/deals:**

- Florida Atlantic University: \$20M system purchase (Jan 2026)
- Fortune 100 company: \$10M 2-year QCaaS agreement
- 5 computers sold to date (as of Feb 2026)
- New US government business unit established

**Technology roadmap (from Feb 2026 trade alert):**

- 2026: 100,000-qubit annealer + commercial 17-qubit gate machine
- 2028: 181-qubit error-corrected gate system
- 2030: 1,000-qubit system
