## Company Overview

EHang is a Chinese autonomous aerial vehicle company and the world's first and only certified pilotless passenger-carrying eVTOL operator. Stephen considers it his most profitable company ever, having bought it at \$4 in 2022 and ridden a 400% rise that "paid for the RV we still live in."

**Products:**

- EH216-S — pilotless passenger-carrying eVTOL, priced at \$400,000 per unit, used primarily for low-altitude tourism in China. Received the world's first Pilotless Passenger-Carrying eVTOL Type Certificate from CAAC.
- EH216-F — firefighting variant, entered production and delivered to emergency rescue departments.
- VT-35 — long-range tilt-and-cruise aircraft unveiled in Q4 2025 with first public demonstration flight and initial deliveries (5 units sold in Q4 2025). Similar specs to JOBY and ACHR aircraft.
- VT-25 — additional long-range aircraft, started selling, still going through approval but has completed demonstration flights including transition.

**Technology position:** Fully autonomous flight operations — no pilot required. This is a fundamental differentiator from all Western competitors (JOBY, ACHR, EVTL) which are piloted. Stephen's characterisation: "Fast movers" with a "monopoly in autonomous flight operations."

**Revenue and financials:**

- FY2023: Q4 revenue RMB 56.6M (US\$8.0M), up 260.9% YoY; gross margin 64.7%; 23 units sold in Q4
- FY2024: Revenue RMB 454M (14% above Stephen's forecast of RMB 399M); 218 units sold
- FY2025: 221 units sold (slight YoY increase); Q2 achieved first-ever GAAP profit; Q3 revenue fell 28% YoY and 37% QoQ with deliveries dropping to 42 units; Q4 recovered with 100 units sold (vs 78 in Q4 2024 and 41 in Q3 2025); record quarterly and annual revenues up 48.4% QoQ and 11.7% YoY; first GAAP profitable quarter; non-GAAP profitability for second consecutive year; RMB 509.5M FY revenue
- FY2026 guidance: RMB 600M (below some analyst expectations of RMB 887.7M)
- Cash: US\$162M on hand (Q4 2025)
- Gross margin: 62% (Q4 2025, up from 60.8%)
- Operating loss narrowed to US\$0.9M from US\$13.4M a year earlier

**Key milestones:**

- Obtained world's first pilotless passenger-carrying eVTOL Type Certificate from CAAC
- Operating licenses granted (Mar 2025) allowing ticketed passenger flights
- Manufacturing capacity expanded to 1,000 units per year (vs Joby at ~50/year)
- Manufacturing partner agreement with GAC (Guangzhou Automobile Group)
- 1,100+ new orders reported (Aug 2024)
- 50-unit order for tourism in Guizhou (Jul 2025)
- Strategic cooperation with Minth Group for airframe systems (Jul 2025)
- Europe's first multi-point-to-point urban emergency-response flights in Zaragoza, Spain (Dec 2025)
- Commercial ticketed tourist flights launched in Guangzhou and Hefei (Mar 2026)
- International expansion: Thailand sandbox, Japan testing in 18 cities with Mitsubishi, Qatar operational authorization granted

## Competitive Position

Stephen views EHang as operating in a fundamentally different market from Western eVTOL companies.

**vs JOBY:** "Although EHang has some Chinese approval, it is not in the same market" (Feb 2024). Joby is piloted, targeting US/Middle East air taxi. EHang is autonomous, targeting Chinese low-altitude tourism. Both held simultaneously in portfolio.

**vs ACHR (Archer):** Stephen deeply distrusts Archer management: "I do not trust this management team and never have. I still believe they stole the designs for the Maker aircraft from Wisk Air."

**vs EVTL (Vertical):** "Best pick" among Western eVTOL but 2+ years from revenue and certification.

**eVTOL industry position (May 2025 sector review):**

- EHang: Fully certified and operational, fully autonomous, fully operational large-scale manufacturing
- Joby: 62% through final certification, 5 aircraft manufactured
- Archer: Still planning first piloted flight
- Vertical: Targeting 2028 certification
- Eve: Still on drawing board

**Key differentiator — autonomy:** EHang is already fully autonomous. Joby bought Xwing for autonomy; Vertical partnered with Honeywell; Eve relies on Embraer; Archer must use Boeing autonomy per Wisk settlement. Neither Ehang nor Eve announced hybrid propulsion plans (most others pursuing range extension).

**Key risk — the operator bottleneck:** Despite aircraft approval, revenue growth stalled because of a lack of licensed ground crew and certified operators. Licensing is at the discretion of the Chinese regulator with limited timing visibility. In Q3 2025, management shifted focus to helping existing customers through approval. 100 operators were due for certification in H1 2026, and Q4 2025 earnings confirmed the pool was "significantly expanded."

**US regulatory risk:** Stephen noted (Oct 2025) that US anti-China surveillance rules "might preclude Hesai, Pony, Ehang and WeRide from starting operations in the US but none of them had any intention of doing so."

**Broker ratings:** Jefferies \$24; JPMorgan \$12 (historically negative, downgraded Jan 2026); Deutsche Bank upgraded to Buy with 100% target (Apr 2025); Beatrice Lam initiated Overweight \$26 target citing 69% revenue CAGR (Aug 2025).
