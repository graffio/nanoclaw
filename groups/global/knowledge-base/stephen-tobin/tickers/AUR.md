## Company Overview

Aurora Innovation is a US-based autonomous trucking company developing the Aurora Driver, a full-stack self-driving package that replaces the human driver in Class 8 trucks. At approximately \$11 billion market cap, it is above Stephen's normal maximum but justified by its market-leading technology, clear demand, and limited competition.

**Products:**

- Aurora Driver — full-stack self-driving system (sensors + software)
- Transport-as-a-Service (current) — Aurora owns and operates trucks, charges per mile
- Driver-as-a-Service (from 2027) — customer-owned trucks pay per-mile fee for Aurora Driver

**Technology position:** First company to operate commercial self-driving trucks on US highways and first to operate fully autonomously anywhere in the world. More than 3 million testing miles with zero incidents. 250,000+ commercial driverless miles, zero collisions. Night driving and bad weather safety cases completed.

**Revenue model:** Per-mile charge for autonomous freight delivery. Current rate \$1.84/mile vs human-driven \$2.30/mile, expected to drop to \$1/mile by 2027 when Continental-manufactured Driver enables scaled deployment.

- FY2025: \$3M (first commercial revenue)
- 2026 guidance: \$14-16M (+400% YoY)
- Q4 2026 expected to account for over 50% of full-year revenue
- Liquidity: ~\$1.5B, sufficient to reach positive FCF in 2028

**Key partnerships:**

- Continental: manufacturing partnership for Gen 3 hardware, production in tens of thousands from 2027. Continental guided this will be worth "billions per year"
- Paccar (29.6% truck market share) and Volvo (16.8%): OEM agreements covering 46.8% of US truck market
- Fabrinet: manufacturing Gen 2 hardware in smaller volumes
- AUMOVIO (spun out of Continental): will manufacture Gen 3, based on NVIDIA Thor chips

**Key customers:** Uber Freight, FedEx, Hirschbach, Werner, Schneider. Detmar deal (Dec 2025) for 24-hour autonomous delivery in Permian Basin. Commercial truck capacity fully committed through Q3 2026.

**Hardware roadmap:**

- Gen 2 (Fabrinet): 50% cost reduction, 1,000m perception range, B samples delivered
- Gen 3 (AUMOVIO/Continental): scaled production from 2027, designed for 1 million miles, NVIDIA Thor

## Competitive Position

Stephen views autonomous trucking as a more immediately profitable market than robotaxis. The US trucking market is 200 billion miles/year (10x the ride-share market) with existing infrastructure.

**vs Kodiak (KDK):** "For the time being, it is AUR v KDK." Aurora leads in highway commercialization with completed safety cases and active driverless operations since April 2025. Kodiak focuses on off-road/industrial/defence verticals. Aurora has ~5 year first-mover advantage.

**vs Waabi:** Privately held, partnered with Uber Freight and Volvo, but targeting early 2030s for highway operations.

**vs Torc (Daimler):** Stephen believes Daimler will likely adopt Aurora alongside Torc rather than go single-source, as developing Torc fully would cost ~\$2B that doesn't fit their capital allocation.

**vs Tesla/Waymo:** Waymo publicly ended autonomous truck work. Tesla not close to launch in trucking.

**Daimler opportunity:** With 39.6% truck market share (Freightliner + Western Star), a Daimler partnership "would be an enormous catalyst for the stock."

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Aug 2025, Trade Alert #66):** "I can see it as clear as day." First commercially operating autonomous truck company with proven technology on US highways. Mathematical model gives fair value of \$16.40 vs stock at \$6.20. Clear first-mover advantage, OEM agreements with 46.8% of truck market, enormous pull-through demand from driver shortage and cost savings. Break even forecast 2029, will need ~\$1.5B additional equity (15% dilution).

**Revised thesis (Dec 2025, Trade Alert #87):** Re-bought after exiting at 30% loss during risk-off. "The price went down, but the fundamentals improved." Q3 showed accelerating progress: 50,000 miles in 5 weeks, over \$1M revenue, 10 routes operational, night driving ahead of schedule. Guiding to positive gross profit by end 2026, positive income by end 2027. Hundreds of trucks in 2026, thousands in 2027.

**Concerns:** Still an ~\$7B company with almost no revenue. Single crash could set back years. \$150M quarterly cash burn. Oil crisis assessment (Mar 2026): Medium short-term risk, Medium medium-term risk — "uses ICE vehicles, long term growth linked to economic growth."
