# Trade Alert: Adding to Elva

*Waching QBTS, BYRN and ULBI*

I published a new article on Electrovaya today on SeekingAlpha highlighting the company's strong technical advantage, particularly its industry-leading battery cycle life.

The key technical advantage is a 14,000 life cycle, much better than any competitor, which gives the battery a low total cost of ownership

The key business advantages are the exclusivity deal with Toyota Materials Handling for forklift trucks in the US and the agreement with the Japanese trading house Sumitomo for Japanese construction OEMs. Both of these markets look set for significant growth, and ELVA is guiding toward a doubling of revenue in the next two years.

With a new US manufacturing facility in the works and expanding into new markets, Electrovaya seems poised for significant growth.

Unlike the last time we invested they now have strong financials, my mathematical model gives a price target of $9.70.

I am going to increase my position size today. [I covered position size in this note](https://substack.com/@stephentobin/note/c-101955908). It is an essential part of making money in these small-cap, high-risk companies. I have increased my initial investment size to $500 due to the portfolio's high cash position. So far, I have invested $350 in Electrovaya and will issue a mid-market order to buy an additional 50 shares today.

Here is the portfolio performance so far. As you can see, the cash balance is very high. We decided in early December to increase it, but this is probably too big.

### QBTS

I am watching D-Wave very closely. Once again, the price shot higher after we bought. The speed and size of the advance do not seem sustainable, and I will close out the position if it appears to falter. It will be a big couple of weeks with the Nvidia quantum conference, and the D-Wave investor days are due.

Today, they released another interesting paper showing how their machine can run a blockchain. I have no idea if that is worth any money but it is likely to add to the hype.

I don't think I will let it drop below $9 (booking in 100%) and will close if it gets near $20.

### BYRN

The stock continues to fall, but I cannot see any real reason. The business appears to be meeting its targets and performing well. It recently started running ads on Amazon Prime, which was a key aim for the year, and Q1 revenue was good. I will continue to hold, but I will refer you back to my note on position size.

### ULBI

Down 40%, I am still going to wait for the much-delayed earnings call. Perhaps I am being a fool and breaking one of my key rules about taking losses early. We shall see.
