## Company Overview

Aptiv PLC is a European-headquartered (domiciled in Ireland, operationally based in Switzerland) global Tier-1 automotive supplier with 149 manufacturing facilities across 49 countries and 21,000 engineers. The company provides the electronic architecture, cabling, connectors, sensors, and compute capability that enables the transition to autonomous, electric, and connected vehicles.

**Business segments:**

- Advanced Safety and User Experience: electronic active safety, in-cabin tech, LiDAR/radar/ultrasound sensors, perception systems, HPC compute, autonomous driving packages, vehicle connectivity (OTA updates)
- Signal and Power Solutions: complete design, manufacture, and assembly of vehicle electrical architecture, wiring, connectors, harnesses, power distribution

**Key acquisition:** Wind River Systems (\$3.5 billion, 2022) — global leader in delivering software OTA to edge devices, adding vehicle-to-vehicle and vehicle-to-infrastructure connectivity and opening defence/aerospace verticals.

**Strategic events during holding period:**

- Motional JV with Hyundai: stake reduced from 50% to 15%, generating \$600 million gain
- EDS spin-off: announced Jan 2026 that Electrical Distribution Systems division would become independent as "Versigent" (NYSE: VGNT), tax-free spin-off targeted for Apr 1, 2026
- \$3 billion share repurchase program completed in Q1 2025, reducing share count by 18%

**Customer penetration:** The top 25 global auto OEMs are repeat customers. In 2024, 17 of the top 20 selling vehicles in the US, 17 of the top 20 in Europe, and 12 of the top 20 in China contained Aptiv products.

**Financial highlights (during holding period):**

- Q1 2025 revenue: \$4.8B (beat guidance of \$4.6B)
- Q2 2025 revenue: record \$5.2B, EPS record \$2.12 (+30%), EBITDA \$821M
- FY2025 full-year guidance: \$20-\$20.5B revenue
- \$27 billion new business awards in FY2025, ~75% from China
- Q4 2025 revenue +3% YoY; 2026 guidance: +4% revenue growth, EPS \$8.15-\$8.75
- Liquidity: \$3.4B; debt reduced from \$9.5B to \$7.8B

## Competitive Position

Stephen initially bought APTV believing that ADAS platform orders going to the market were flowing to Aptiv rather than pure-play competitors like Arbe (radar). This thesis was later revised.

**vs Arbe:** Stephen originally believed "orders expected to go to ARBE and its radar were actually going to APTV." By Mar 2026, he concluded this was "likely wrong and it is probably LiDAR that is being adopted instead of Radar. AEVA recently reported that it has won the Mercedes deal with its 4D LiDAR solution. Mercedes had been a key Arbe target."

**vs Minth Group:** Both serve the automotive market, but Minth has exposure to the robotics actuator market via its JV with LeaderDrive. Stephen ultimately rotated APTV into Minth, citing similar end-markets but higher growth potential in Minth's robotics exposure.

**Gen 6 ADAS platform:** Multi-OEM wins including a luxury German OEM, two large Japanese OEMs, and Chinese EV makers (Cherie, Zeeker, GAC, Chang'an, Xiaomi, Great Wall). Scalable L0 to L4 architecture, sensor/software agnostic.

**China positioning:** "Made in China for China" strategy — \$7 billion new China business in Q4 2024, with 70% from domestic manufacturers (up from negligible). Q4 2024 local OEM revenue mix reached 53%.

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (May 2025, Trade Alert):** Convergence of three automotive mega-trends: Safe, Green, Connected. Aptiv provides complete electronic architecture and computing for autonomous vehicles and robotaxis. 100% upside to fair value of \$107. "One thing we do know is that whichever manufacturers win the robotaxi race, their vehicles will be full of Aptiv parts, components, computing, and software." Strong-Buy rating. 3-statement DCF model with 8% discount rate and 0.5% terminal growth.

**Revised thesis (Aug 2025, Trade Alert #67):** After Q2 earnings beat, "confirming my Strong-Buy recommendation." Fair value increased from \$107 to \$128. Record EBITDA margin of 12.1% (vs consensus 11.4%). "APTV is doing an excellent job. They cannot control global auto manufacturing demand, but they can control their own operating costs."

**Rating upgrade (Feb 2026, Chat):** "Changing the rating on APTV to hold, previously it was exit near. The company seems to be developing some momentum across multiple vertical markets and with incredible scale. Cash flow is really strong and the spin off moves the focus to high growth markets. I have worked through the model today and the fair value of shares moved to over \$150."

**Exit thesis (Mar 2026, Trade Alert #103):** "Rotating into Minth as they serve similar markets, and I do not want to hold two auto suppliers. Minth has the Robot market, whereas APTV appears to have lost the Radar advantage, which was the thesis for the trade." Original radar thesis proved wrong — LiDAR won over radar for key OEM deals. Growth profile softened. Small profit booked.
