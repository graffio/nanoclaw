## Company Overview

WeRide is a Chinese autonomous driving company operating robotaxis, robobuses, and robosweepers across multiple countries. Unlike its closest peer Pony.AI, which pursues an asset-light, China-first, robotaxi-only strategy, WeRide takes a broader, more capital-intensive approach: multiple vehicle types across global markets.

**Products / Services:**

- Robotaxis — core business, operating in Guangzhou (24/7 license), Beijing, Abu Dhabi, Saudi Arabia, France (first robotaxi license in the country), and via Uber partnership in 15+ cities globally
- Robobus — deployed at Zurich Airport and Saudi Arabia (King Fahad Medical City, Aramco residential communities)
- Robosweeper S1 — Saudi Arabia's first monetized autonomous sanitation project
- WeRide GENESIS — platform uniting physical and generative AI for autonomous driving simulation (announced Jan 2026)

**Revenue model:** Service fees from robotaxi rides, fleet deployment contracts, and partnerships. Still pre-profit with heavy scaling investment.

- Q1 2025: \$10M revenue (+1.8% YoY), net loss \$53.1M
- Q2 2025: total revenue +68% YoY, robotaxi revenue +840% YoY
- Breakeven forecast: unit profitability in 2026, company-level breakeven H2 2029/H1 2030, first full profitable year 2030

**Key partnerships/deals:**

- Uber: expansion to 15 new cities over five years (Europe, Middle East); first commercial robotaxi service launched together (Feb 2026)
- Tencent Cloud: \$100M equity investment to accelerate global expansion
- Geely: deal for 2,000 additional taxis (Mar 2026), targeting 2,600 taxis in 2026 (up from 1,000 in 2025), tens of thousands by 2030
- Saudi Arabia Transport General Authority: trial operations with full commercial launch planned
- Morgan Stanley: Overweight rating, \$13 price target (Jun 2025)

## Competitive Position

Stephen treats PONY and WRD as a single combined investment because of their high correlation and direct competition in the robotaxi space. They are the only pure-play publicly traded robotaxi companies.

**vs Pony.AI (PONY):** "Pony.ai's focused commercial strategy — prioritizing robotaxis, the Chinese market, and Tier 1 cities — offers a more straightforward path to profitability. WeRide's broader approach, encompassing taxis, buses, and road sweepers across global markets without a dedicated app, presents a less defined trajectory to profitability and carries higher operational costs." (May 2025 review). Pony was the initial pick when both received "Buy" ratings. The two stocks have historically been highly correlated, with WRD typically commanding a price premium.

**vs Tesla FSD:** Stephen is critical of Tesla's approach: "I am amazed that the US is allowing Tesla to test its software on open roads and openly admits it makes changes to the software after a crash occurs." He views PONY and WRD as the real market leaders in autonomous driving.

**vs Aurora (AUR):** Stephen added AUR to the portfolio later and views it alongside PONY and WRD as part of the autonomous driving theme. In Mar 2026: "I like all of the autonomous driving stocks... PONY looks good... AUR, KDK and WRD are not quite as solid I think."

**Tariff immunity:** Stephen notes China-focused operations are immune to US tariffs: "PONY, WRD, EH, ACMR conduct their operations inside China, they do not export to the US and will benefit from a tariff war as US companies will be less competitive inside China." (Oct 2025 chat)

## Investment Thesis

**Original thesis (Jun 2025):** Robotaxi sector forecast to grow "almost exponentially." WeRide and Pony are the only pure-play companies. WeRide has a broader global, multi-vehicle strategy compared to Pony's focused approach. Bought as a paired trade with PONY, treating them as a single correlated investment. Combined target \$600 (\$300 each). "They are high-risk, highly correlated Chinese stocks operating in an industry that doesn't yet exist; I don't think it gets any riskier."

**Mean reversion thesis (May 2025):** PONY had surged while WRD lagged. "The two stocks have been highly correlated in the past, with WRD typically demanding a price premium." Stephen expected mean reversion to close the gap.

**Maintained thesis (Jul 2025):** After Q2 earnings showed 68% total revenue growth and 840% robotaxi revenue growth, Stephen said: "I continue to think this sector will outperform going forward and will take opportunities to add additional holdings."

**Addition thesis (Nov 2025):** Added half-size position during pullback: "I am trying to take advantage of the recent pullback by increasing my position in stocks that have dropped significantly in value, whilst improving their fundamentals."

**Autonomous theme thesis (Dec 2025):** "I think Autonomous will be a big theme for 2026, Pony, WRD and AUR all look ready to develop significant operations. I keep looking for names to add but I think those three will be the big winners."

**Profitability model (Feb 2026):** Stephen's model "predicts unit profitability in 2026 with scaling leading to breakeven company operations in H2 2029/H1 2030 with full year profitable operations for the first time in 2030. WRD have been guiding to this timeline for some time but it is dependent on the roll out of tens of thousands of robotaxis."

**Current concerns (Mar 2026):** Position under review. Operations in Dubai suspended due to Iran conflict. Stephen rates PONY more favorably: "WRD are not quite as solid I think." Oil crisis assessment: "very little concern about the autonomous driving companies; they will all reduce costs for adopters." WeRide's Middle East exposure is a risk during conflict but "should not have any lasting impact." Short-term oil risk: Medium. Medium-term oil risk: Low. Prolonged oil crisis makes autonomous EVs more competitive.
